# Response Patterns — DRP v2.0

Standard response patterns for common interrogatory and RFP types. All patterns
follow the response-first rule: substantive answer FIRST, objections AFTER using
the "Notwithstanding" transition.

---

## Interrogatory Patterns

### Identity / Background Information

**Common requests:** Full name, DOB, address, marital status, household members.

```
ANSWER:

[Plaintiff's full name] was born on [DOB] and resides at [address],
[city], [state] [zip]. [He/She] is [married/single/divorced/widowed].
[CLIENT INPUT NEEDED: current marital status if not in file].
[He/She] resides with [household members or CLIENT INPUT NEEDED].

Notwithstanding this answer, Plaintiff objects to this Interrogatory to the
extent it seeks "each address she had throughout her lifetime" and "who lived
with her at each address." This is a [case type] action; a proportional scope
is limited to addresses during the period of treatment at issue and the period
during which damages are claimed. No information is being withheld on the basis
of this objection.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

interrogatories].

limited to the five years preceding the incident and the period from the
incident to present, which is the period relevant to lost wage and earning
capacity claims. No information is being withheld on the basis of this
objection.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.

injuries at issue in this litigation. Plaintiff's claims involve [describe

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional

affecting the same body parts claimed to be injured in this action. No

information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.

### Expert Witnesses

```
ANSWER:

response. Plaintiff anticipates retaining experts in the following areas:

a. [Area of expertise — e.g., "standard of care and breach (medical
   malpractice)"]
b. [Area of expertise — e.g., "causation and prognosis"]
c. [Area of expertise — e.g., "life care planning / future medical needs"]
d. [Area of expertise — e.g., "economic damages / lost earning capacity"]

in accordance with the deadlines established by the Court's Scheduling Order.

Notwithstanding this answer, Plaintiff objects to this Interrogatory to the
extent it seeks expert opinions, analysis, or conclusions that are the subject
of expert disclosures to be made pursuant to the Scheduling Order. Rule
1-026(B)(5) NMRA governs expert discovery, and the Scheduling Order establishes
the timeline for these disclosures. No information is being withheld on the
basis of this objection; expert disclosures will be provided per the Scheduling
Order.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

### Damages / Losses Claimed

**Common requests:** Itemize all damages, state amounts, describe losses.

```
ANSWER:

Plaintiff claims the following categories of damages:

a. Past medical expenses: $[amount or "to be determined upon completion of
   treatment"]. See medical records and billing statements produced herewith
   or to be produced.
b. Future medical expenses: [CLIENT INPUT NEEDED: anticipated future treatment
   needs; expert disclosure will address future medical costs].
c. Past lost wages/income: $[amount or CLIENT INPUT NEEDED]. See employment
   records produced herewith.
d. Future lost earning capacity: [to be addressed by expert disclosure].
e. Past pain and suffering: [describe nature and severity of pain, impact on
   daily activities, or CLIENT INPUT NEEDED].
f. Future pain and suffering: [describe ongoing symptoms and prognosis, or
   CLIENT INPUT NEEDED].
g. Loss of enjoyment of life: [describe specific activities impacted, or
   CLIENT INPUT NEEDED].
h. [Additional categories as applicable: emotional distress, loss of
   consortium (if claimed by spouse), punitive damages (if applicable)]

See also Plaintiff's answers to Interrogatory Nos. [cross-reference medical,
employment, and treatment interrogatories].

Notwithstanding this answer, Plaintiff objects to this Interrogatory to the
extent it requires Plaintiff to state a precise dollar amount for non-economic
damages, which are by nature not susceptible to precise calculation and are
within the province of the jury. Discovery and investigation are ongoing, and
Plaintiff's damages continue to accrue.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

### Contention Interrogatories ("State Each and Every Fact")

**Common requests:** State all facts supporting negligence, causation, damages.

```
ANSWER:

The material and principal facts supporting Plaintiff's claim of [negligence /
breach of the standard of care / causation / etc.] include:

a. [Fact 1 — from CFP or case knowledge]
b. [Fact 2]
c. [Fact 3]
[Continue with material facts]

Plaintiff is not required to provide a narrative account of each and every act
or omission constituting negligence. See Lucero v. Valdez, 240 F.R.D. 591, 594
(D.N.M. 2007). Discovery and investigation are ongoing, and Plaintiff reserves
the right to supplement this response as additional facts are developed through
depositions, expert analysis, and document production.

See also Plaintiff's answers to Interrogatory Nos. [cross-reference related
interrogatories].

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

### Authorization Requests

**Common requests:** Execute medical/employment authorizations.

```
RESPONSE:

Plaintiff will execute reasonable and appropriately limited authorizations for
medical records related to the injuries at issue in this litigation.

Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to the authorizations attached
to Defendant's discovery requests to the extent they are overbroad in scope,
duration, or subject matter. Specifically, Plaintiff objects to authorizations
that:

a. Are not limited to the body parts and conditions at issue;
b. Seek records from before [appropriate date] without demonstrated relevance;
c. Do not contain a termination date;
d. Authorize disclosure of information protected by federal or state privacy
   laws unrelated to this litigation.

Plaintiff will provide properly limited authorizations within [14/30] days.
[CLIENT INPUT NEEDED: review proposed authorizations and provide appropriately
limited versions for attorney review.]

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

## Request for Production Patterns

### Medical Records and Bills

```
RESPONSE:

Plaintiff will produce non-privileged medical records and billing statements
from the healthcare providers identified in Plaintiff's answers to
Interrogatories, for treatment related to the injuries at issue, to the extent
such records are in Plaintiff's possession, custody, or control. See documents
to be produced herewith or within [14/30] days.

Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it seeks medical records unrelated to the injuries and conditions at issue in
this litigation. A proportional scope is limited to records from providers who
treated the injuries claimed. No information is being withheld on the basis of
this objection; Plaintiff is producing records within the proportional scope.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

---

### Employment / Income Records

```
RESPONSE:

Plaintiff will produce non-privileged employment records, pay stubs, W-2 forms,
and tax returns relevant to Plaintiff's lost wage and earning capacity claims,
to the extent such documents are in Plaintiff's possession, custody, or
control. See documents to be produced herewith or within [14/30] days.

[CLIENT INPUT NEEDED: gather and provide pay stubs, W-2s, and/or tax returns
for the relevant period.]

Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it seeks employment and financial records beyond the scope of the lost wage and
earning capacity claims. A proportional scope is limited to the [3-5] years
preceding the incident through the present. No information is being withheld on
the basis of this objection.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

---

### Photographs / Social Media

```
RESPONSE:

Plaintiff will produce non-privileged photographs depicting Plaintiff's
injuries, the scene of the [incident], and property damage, to the extent such
photographs are in Plaintiff's possession, custody, or control.

Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it seeks "all photographs" without limitation as to subject matter or time
period. At its broadest reading, this Request would encompass every photograph
Plaintiff has ever taken or appeared in, which is neither proportional nor
reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence. A
proportional scope is limited to photographs depicting the injuries, the
incident scene, or Plaintiff's physical condition during the period from the
incident to present. No information is being withheld on the basis of this
objection.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

---

### Communications / Correspondence

```
RESPONSE:

Plaintiff will produce non-privileged communications relevant to the claims at
issue, to the extent such communications are in Plaintiff's possession, custody,
or control.

Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it seeks communications protected by the attorney-client privilege or work
product doctrine. Plaintiff withholds communications between Plaintiff and
Plaintiff's counsel on the basis of this objection and will produce a privilege
log. Plaintiff further objects to the extent this Request seeks "all
communications" without limitation as to subject matter, sender/recipient, or
time period, which is overly broad. A proportional scope is limited to
communications relating to the incident, the injuries, or the damages claimed,
from the date of the incident to present.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

## Request for Admission Patterns

### Admit Factual Foundation
```
RESPONSE:

Admitted.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

### Deny Factual Assertion
```
RESPONSE:

Denied. [Brief factual basis if required by jurisdiction or helpful to clarify
the scope of the denial.]

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

### Partial Admit / Partial Deny
```
RESPONSE:

Admitted that [specific facts admitted]. Denied that [specific facts denied].
Plaintiff lacks sufficient knowledge or information to admit or deny [specific
portions], and therefore denies same.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

### Insufficient Knowledge
```
RESPONSE:

After reasonable inquiry, the information known to or readily obtainable by
Plaintiff is insufficient to enable Plaintiff to admit or deny this Request.
Plaintiff therefore denies same. [Brief explanation of inquiry conducted, if
helpful.]

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```

### RFA Seeking Legal Conclusion
```
RESPONSE:

Denied.

Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it calls for a legal conclusion rather than an admission of fact. Requests for
admission are properly limited to statements of fact, the application of law to
fact, or the genuineness of documents. See Rule 1-036(A) NMRA. No information
is being withheld on the basis of this objection.

Plaintiff reserves the right to amend or supplement this response as additional
information becomes available through continuing investigation and discovery.
```
