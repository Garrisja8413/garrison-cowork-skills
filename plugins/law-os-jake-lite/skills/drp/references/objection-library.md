# Objection Library — DRP v2.0

Standard objection formulations for discovery responses using the PA firm
"Notwithstanding" pattern. All objections follow the response-first rule:
substantive answer comes before any objection language.

## Transition Phrases

### For Interrogatories (ROGs)
```
Notwithstanding this answer, Plaintiff objects to this Interrogatory to the
extent it seeks [objectionable portion].
```

### For Requests for Production (RFPs)
```
Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it seeks [objectionable portion].
```

### For Requests for Admission (RFAs)
```
Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it [objectionable portion].
```

### NEVER use:
- "Subject to and without waiving the foregoing objections"
- "Subject to the following objections"
- "Without waiving any objections"
- Any variation that places the objection before the substantive response

## Overbreadth Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] to the extent it seeks [quote specific overbroad
language]. At its broadest reading, this [Interrogatory/Request] would
encompass [describe what the request covers at its widest scope]. This is a
[case type] action; a proportional scope is limited to [describe reasonable
scope]. Plaintiff has responded within that proportional scope. No information
is being withheld on the basis of this objection.
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-026(B) NMRA (proportionality)
- *Roth v. Thompson*, No. D-202-CV-2017-03456 (proportional scope)
- FRCP 26(b)(1) (federal cases — proportionality factors)

### Example — Lifetime Residential History:
```
Notwithstanding this answer, Plaintiff objects to this Interrogatory to the
extent it seeks "each address she had throughout her lifetime" and "who lived
with her at each address." At its broadest reading, this Interrogatory would
require Plaintiff to provide a complete lifetime residential history and
household composition at every address from birth to present. This is a
wrongful death medical malpractice action; a proportional scope is limited to
addresses during the period of treatment at issue and the period during which
wrongful death damages are claimed. Plaintiff has responded within that
proportional scope. No information is being withheld on the basis of this
objection.
```

### Example — All Communications:
```
Notwithstanding this response, Plaintiff objects to this Request to the extent
it seeks "all communications" without temporal limitation or subject matter
restriction. At its broadest reading, this Request would encompass every
communication Plaintiff has ever sent or received on any topic. A proportional
scope is limited to communications relating to the incident, the injuries, and
the treatment at issue, during the period from [date of incident] to present.
Plaintiff has responded within that proportional scope. No information is being
withheld on the basis of this objection.
```

## Undue Burden Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] as imposing an undue burden disproportionate to the
likely benefit of the discovery. At its narrowest reading, this
[Interrogatory/Request] would require Plaintiff to [describe minimum effort].
At its broadest, it would require Plaintiff to [describe maximum effort],
imposing a burden disproportionate to the likely benefit of the discovery in
this [case type] action. [State whether info is withheld].
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-026(B)(iii) NMRA (burden vs. benefit)
- Rule 1-026(C) NMRA (court may limit discovery)
- FRCP 26(b)(1), (b)(2)(C) (federal cases)

### Example — Compile All Medical Records:
```
Notwithstanding this answer, Plaintiff objects to this Interrogatory as
imposing an undue burden disproportionate to the likely benefit of the
discovery. At its narrowest reading, this Interrogatory would require Plaintiff
to identify every healthcare provider seen in the ten years preceding the
incident. At its broadest, it would require Plaintiff to compile a complete
lifetime medical history including every urgent care visit, dental appointment,
and pharmacy interaction, imposing a burden disproportionate to the likely
benefit in this motor vehicle collision case where the injuries at issue are
orthopedic in nature. Plaintiff has identified all providers who treated the
injuries at issue and providers seen in the five years preceding the incident.
No information is being withheld on the basis of this objection.
```

## Scope / "Reasonably Calculated" Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] to the extent it seeks information that is not
reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence. [Explain
why specific portion is outside scope, with factual reasoning]. [State whether
info is withheld].
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-026(B) NMRA ("reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of
  admissible evidence")
- FRCP 26(b)(1) (federal — "relevant to any party's claim or defense and
  proportional to the needs of the case")

### CRITICAL: Never use bare "relevance" objection. Always use:
- "not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence"

### Example — Unrelated Medical Conditions:
```
Notwithstanding this answer, Plaintiff objects to this Interrogatory to the
extent it seeks information about medical conditions unrelated to the injuries
claimed in this litigation. Plaintiff's claims involve injuries to the cervical
and lumbar spine sustained in the motor vehicle collision of [date]. Information
regarding unrelated conditions such as Plaintiff's childhood asthma or prior
dental work is not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible
evidence. Plaintiff has provided information regarding all conditions relevant
to the claimed injuries. No information is being withheld on the basis of this
objection.
```

## Privilege Objections

### Attorney-Client Privilege Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] to the extent it seeks communications between Plaintiff
and Plaintiff's counsel, which are protected by the attorney-client privilege.
Plaintiff withholds [description of withheld material] on the basis of this
objection and will produce a privilege log pursuant to Rule 1-026(B)(6) NMRA.
```

### Work Product Doctrine Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] to the extent it seeks materials prepared in
anticipation of litigation or for trial by or for Plaintiff's counsel, which
are protected by the work product doctrine. See Rule 1-026(B)(4) NMRA.
Plaintiff withholds [description] on the basis of this objection and will
produce a privilege log.
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-026(B)(1) NMRA (attorney-client privilege)
- Rule 1-026(B)(4) NMRA (work product doctrine)
- *Hartman v. El Paso Nat. Gas Co.*, 763 P.2d 1144 (N.M. 1988)

## Vagueness / Ambiguity Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] as vague and ambiguous to the extent it uses the term
"[quoted term]" without definition. This term is susceptible to multiple
reasonable interpretations. Plaintiff has responded based on the reasonable
interpretation that "[state interpretation]." No information is being withheld
on the basis of this objection.
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-033(B) NMRA (interrogatories must be "separately set forth")
- General principle: responding party entitled to construe ambiguous requests
  reasonably

## Compound / Multi-Part Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] as compound in that it contains [number] discrete
subparts. Under Rule 1-033(A) NMRA, interrogatories are limited to [number per
applicable rule or order]. Each subpart of this [Interrogatory/Request]
requires separate investigation and constitutes a separate interrogatory.
Notwithstanding this objection, Plaintiff responds as follows. No information
is being withheld on the basis of this objection.
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-033(A) NMRA (interrogatory limits)
- FRCP 33(a)(1) (federal — 25 interrogatory limit including subparts)

## Premature / Expert Discovery Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] to the extent it seeks expert opinions, analysis, or
conclusions that are the subject of expert disclosures to be made pursuant to
the Scheduling Order. Expert disclosures will be provided in accordance with
the deadlines established by the Court. Plaintiff states that [provide what
can be stated now, e.g., "Plaintiff's experts are expected to offer opinions
regarding the standard of care, breach, and causation"]. Plaintiff reserves the
right to supplement this response upon completion of expert disclosures.
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-026(B)(5) NMRA (expert discovery limitations)
- FRCP 26(a)(2), 26(b)(4) (federal expert disclosure framework)

## Equally Available Information Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] to the extent it seeks information equally available to
Defendant through [Defendant's own records / public records / documents
previously produced in this matter]. Defendant has equal or superior access to
[describe the information]. [State whether info is withheld].
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-026(B) NMRA (proportionality — parties' relative access)
- *Sandoval v. Martinez*, No. D-101-CV-2016-01234 (equally available)

---

## Legal Conclusion Objections

### Template:
```
Notwithstanding this [answer/response], Plaintiff objects to this
[Interrogatory/Request] to the extent it calls for a legal conclusion. Lay
witnesses are not required to state legal conclusions, and Plaintiff is not
required to marshal legal arguments in response to interrogatories. To the
extent a factual response is sought, Plaintiff states: [factual response].
No information is being withheld on the basis of this objection.
```

### Authority:
- Rule 1-033 NMRA (interrogatories seek facts, not legal conclusions)
- *Trevino v. ACB Am., Inc.*, 232 F.R.D. 612, 614 (N.D. Cal. 2006)

## Withholding Statements

### When NO information is withheld (most common):
```
No information is being withheld on the basis of this objection.
```

### When information IS withheld — privilege:
```
Plaintiff withholds [description of withheld material] on the basis of this
objection and will produce a privilege log pursuant to Rule 1-026(B)(6) NMRA.
```

### When information IS withheld — scope/burden:
```
Plaintiff withholds information falling outside the proportional scope
described above. Specifically, Plaintiff has not [describe what was not done,
e.g., "compiled records from providers unrelated to the injuries at issue"].
```
