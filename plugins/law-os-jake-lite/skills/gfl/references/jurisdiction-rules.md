# Jurisdiction Rules — GFL Reference

## New Mexico (State Court)

### Meet-and-Confer Requirement
- **Rule:** NMRA 1-037(A) — motion to compel requires prior good faith effort
- **Standard:** Genuine attempt to resolve without court intervention
- **No specific format required** — letter is standard practice

### Discovery Scope
- **Rule:** NMRA 1-026(B) — broad relevance standard
- **Proportionality:** Not codified as explicitly as federal, but courts apply reasonableness

### Objection Standards
- General/boilerplate objections are disfavored
- Objections must state specific grounds
- Failure to timely object = waiver (NMRA 1-033(B), 1-034(B))

### Common Cure Period
- **14 days** is customary (not statutory)
- Shorter period acceptable if discovery cutoff is imminent

### Key Authorities to Cite (should be in LPB)
- Discovery scope and relevance
- Objection specificity requirements
- Waiver by late/general objection
- Burden-shifting (objecting party bears burden to show undue burden)

## Federal — D.N.M.

### Meet-and-Confer Requirement
- **Rule:** D.N.M. LR-Civ 7.1(a) — certification of good faith conference required
- **Standard:** "Reasonable effort" to resolve by agreement
- **Format:** Letter + conference attempt (phone/in-person preferred)
- **Certification language** required in any subsequent motion

### Discovery Scope
- **Rule:** FRCP 26(b)(1) — relevance + proportionality
- **Proportionality factors** (must address in GFL when opposing party raises burden):
  1. Importance of issues at stake
  2. Amount in controversy
  3. Parties' relative access to relevant information
  4. Parties' resources
  5. Importance of discovery in resolving issues
  6. Whether burden outweighs likely benefit

### Objection Standards
- **FRCP 33(b)(4):** Grounds must be stated with specificity
- **FRCP 34(b)(2)(B):** Objections must state whether materials are being withheld on basis of objection
- General objections = waiver in many circuits
- "Subject to" objections disfavored

### Common Cure Period
- **30 days** is customary for federal
- Court scheduling order may control

### Key Authorities to Cite (should be in LPB)
- FRCP 26(b)(1) proportionality
- FRCP 33/34 specificity requirements
- D.N.M. local rules on discovery disputes
- Burden-shifting framework
- Waiver doctrine for untimely/boilerplate objections

## Jurisdiction Detection

Determine jurisdiction from the DPB Set_Metadata `Jurisdiction` field:
- `NM` → New Mexico state court rules
- `FED` or `D.N.M.` → Federal rules + D.N.M. local rules

If Jurisdiction is missing from the DPB packet, ask the user.
If both NM and FED sets appear in the same packet, draft separate
letters or clearly delineate sections by jurisdiction.
