# R11 Motion Structure — Reference

## BODY-ONLY Template

The SA shell provides: caption, case number, court, COS, signature block.
This skill drafts only the argument body (Sections I–VI).

**Important:** FRCP 11(c)(2) requires that a motion for sanctions "must be made
separately from any other motion." Do not combine with a motion to strike or
judgment on the pleadings. Those are separate filings using separate skills.

---

### I. INTRODUCTION

**Template:**
```
[MERGE:CaseName] respectfully moves this Court for an order imposing
sanctions under [Rule 11 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure /
NMRA 1-011] against [Respondent / Respondent's counsel] for [presenting /
maintaining] [the Answer / the Motion / the pleading] in violation of
the certification requirements of [Rule 11(b) / NMRA 1-011(A)].

[If FRCP 11:]
In compliance with Rule 11(c)(2), [MERGE:CaseName] served this motion
on [Respondent's counsel] on [safe harbor service date]. The twenty-one-
day safe harbor period expired on [expiration date] without withdrawal
or correction of the challenged [pleading / contentions].

[If NMRA 1-011:]
[MERGE:CaseName] provided notice to [Respondent's counsel] on [notice date]
identifying the certification deficiencies in [the Answer / the pleading].
[Respondent's counsel] has [failed to respond / declined to cure / failed
to adequately cure] the identified deficiencies.

[Pattern summary:]
[Respondent's] [Answer / pleading] contains [count] [denials / defenses /
contentions] that violate the certification standard because [brief summary
of the pattern — e.g., "they deny facts that are established by
[Respondent's] own documents," "they assert defenses foreclosed by binding
New Mexico precedent," "they deny facts plainly within [Respondent's] own
knowledge without any reasonable inquiry"]. This is not an isolated
drafting error — it is a pattern of [certification violations / pleading
deficiencies] that warrants sanctions to deter repetition and compensate
[MERGE:CaseName] for the expenses necessitated by this conduct.
```

**Traceability:** Pattern count from ADR Defect Matrix CERT-FAILURE rows.
Safe harbor dates from evidence_layer documentation. Rule citations from LPB.

### II. PROCEDURAL BACKGROUND

**Template:**
```
On [complaint filing date], [MERGE:CaseName] filed the [Complaint /
Amended Complaint] in this action. (ECF [No.] / [Docket entry].)

On [answer filing date], [Respondent] filed [the Answer / the Amended
Answer]. (ECF [No.] / [Docket entry].)

[If meet-and-confer occurred:]
On [meet-confer date], [MERGE:CaseName]'s counsel [sent a letter to /
conferred with] [Respondent's counsel] identifying [count] specific
certification deficiencies in the [Answer]. (Exhibit [X].)
[Respondent's counsel] [did not respond / responded but did not cure /
partially cured — describe].

[Safe harbor — FRCP 11:]
On [service date], [MERGE:CaseName] served this motion for sanctions
on [Respondent's counsel] pursuant to Rule 11(c)(2). (Exhibit [X] —
proof of service.) The twenty-one-day safe harbor period expired on
[expiration date]. [Respondent] has [not withdrawn or corrected /
partially corrected — describe remaining violations] the challenged
[contentions / denials / defenses].

[Notice — NMRA 1-011:]
On [notice date], [MERGE:CaseName] provided [Respondent's counsel]
with written notice identifying the specific certification violations
and requesting correction. (Exhibit [X].) [Describe response or lack
thereof.] [MERGE:CaseName] now moves for sanctions pursuant to
NMRA 1-011(C), which authorizes sanctions after notice and a reasonable
opportunity to respond.
```

**Traceability:** All dates from evidence_layer safe harbor documentation
and meet_confer_log. ECF/docket entries from CFP File_Metadata.
No characterization of opposing counsel's motives — just chronological facts.

### III. CERTIFICATION STANDARD

**Template:**
```
[Build from LPB authority. Use the following structure:]

[If FRCP 11:]
Rule 11(b) provides that by presenting a pleading to the court, an
attorney certifies that to the best of the attorney's knowledge,
information, and belief, formed after an inquiry reasonable under the
circumstances:

(1) it is not being presented for any improper purpose, such as to
    harass, cause unnecessary delay, or needlessly increase the cost
    of litigation;
(2) the claims, defenses, and other legal contentions are warranted
    by existing law or by a nonfrivolous argument for extending,
    modifying, or reversing existing law or for establishing new law;
(3) the factual contentions have evidentiary support or, if specifically
    so identified, will likely have evidentiary support after a
    reasonable opportunity for further investigation or discovery; and
(4) the denials of factual contentions are warranted on the evidence
    or, if specifically so identified, are reasonably based on belief
    or a lack of information.

[LPB authority + pinpoint for each subsection.]

[If NMRA 1-011:]
NMRA 1-011(A) provides that by presenting a pleading, motion, or other
paper to the court, an attorney certifies that to the best of the
attorney's knowledge, information, and belief, formed after reasonable
inquiry:

(1) it is not presented for any improper purpose;
(2) the claims, defenses, and other legal contentions are warranted by
    existing law or by a nonfrivolous argument for modification;
(3) the factual contentions have evidentiary support or will likely
    have support after further investigation or discovery; and
(4) the denials of factual contentions are warranted on the evidence
    or are reasonably based on lack of information or belief.

[LPB authority + pinpoint.]

The standard is objective — whether a reasonable attorney, after
reasonable inquiry, would have [filed / maintained] the challenged
[contentions]. [LPB authority + pinpoint.] The court need not find
subjective bad faith. [LPB authority + pinpoint.]

The purpose of [Rule 11 / NMRA 1-011] is to deter baseless filings
and curb abuses of the judicial system. [LPB authority + pinpoint.]
Sanctions should be sufficient to deter repetition of the conduct
or comparable conduct by others similarly situated. [LPB authority +
pinpoint.]
```

**Note:** Every sentence in this section must cite an LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.
If a rule statement lacks LPB backing, mark `[VERIFY-CITE]`.

### IV. STATEMENT OF SANCTIONABLE CONDUCT

**This is the core of the motion. Organize by category of violation,
then within each category, address each specific instance.**

**General structure per category:**

```
[Letter]. [Category Heading — e.g., "Denials Not Warranted on the Evidence"]

[Rule 11(b)(X) / NMRA 1-011(A)(X)] requires that [restate the specific
certification obligation]. [LPB authority + pinpoint.]

[Respondent's] [Answer / pleading] violates this requirement in [count]
specific instances:
```

**Then for each instance within the category:**

```
[Number]. [Answer ¶ / Defense #] — [Brief Description]

In Complaint ¶ [X], [MERGE:CaseName] alleged that [brief summary of
the averment]. (Complaint ¶ [X].)

[Respondent] responded: "[Quote the exact denial / defense language]."
(Answer ¶ [X] / [Defense #].)

This [denial / defense / contention] lacks any reasonable basis because
[analysis — citing specific record evidence]:

[For denials contradicted by record:]
[Respondent's] own [document type — e.g., medical records, business
records, correspondence] establish that [state the fact the record
proves]. ([DocID] at [Bates/Page:Line].) No reasonable attorney, after
reasonable inquiry, would deny [the fact] in the face of [Respondent's]
own [records / admissions / testimony].

[For denials of facts within party's knowledge:]
[The fact denied] — [description] — is a matter within [Respondent's]
own knowledge and records. [Respondent] [was present at / authored /
received / maintained records of] [the event / document]. A reasonable
inquiry would have revealed that this denial is unwarranted.
([Cross-reference: ADR Flag [LKI-IMPROPER / BLANKET-DENIAL], ¶ [X].])

[For legal contentions foreclosed by binding authority:]
[Respondent] asserts [defense / legal position]. This contention is
foreclosed by [binding authority — LPB citation + pinpoint], which holds
that [state the holding]. [Respondent] offers no nonfrivolous argument
for extending, modifying, or reversing this authority. A reasonable
attorney would not present this contention.

[For improper purpose — use only with strong record support:]
The objective circumstances indicate that [the filing / these contentions]
serve no legitimate litigation purpose. [Describe the pattern — e.g.,
"[Respondent] denied [count] factual averments that are established by
[Respondent's] own records, necessitating [count] hours of additional
discovery and motion practice to establish facts that should have been
admitted"]. [LPB authority on improper-purpose indicators + pinpoint.]
```

**ADR Cross-Reference:** Each instance should cite the ADR Defect Matrix row
(Defect Flag + Complaint ¶ / Defense #) to maintain traceability.

---

### IV-A. FACTUAL CONTENTIONS WITHOUT EVIDENTIARY SUPPORT — Rule 11(b)(3) / 1-011(A)(3)

Use the general template above. Focus on:
- Affirmative factual contentions in the Answer or affirmative defenses
  that lack any evidentiary support
- Claims of fact that contradict the documentary record
- Factual predicates of defenses that are unsupported

---

### IV-B. DENIALS NOT WARRANTED ON THE EVIDENCE — Rule 11(b)(4) / 1-011(A)(4)

Use the general template above. Focus on:
- Denials of facts established by the party's own documents
- Denials of facts within the party's personal knowledge
- Blanket denials of multi-fact paragraphs where some facts are undeniable
- Improper "lacks knowledge" responses for facts within party's control
- Cross-reference ADR flags: BLANKET-DENIAL, LKI-IMPROPER, EVASIVE

---

### IV-C. LEGAL CONTENTIONS NOT WARRANTED BY EXISTING LAW — Rule 11(b)(2) / 1-011(A)(2)

Use the general template above. Focus on:
- Defenses foreclosed by binding NM Supreme Court or Court of Appeals authority
- Legal positions contradicted by the plain text of governing statutes/rules
- Arguments abandoned or rejected in prior proceedings in the same case
- Cross-reference ADR flags: BOILERPLATE (when defense has no legal basis,
  not merely insufficient factual detail)

---

### IV-D. IMPROPER PURPOSE — Rule 11(b)(1) / 1-011(A)(1) — OPTIONAL

**Include this section only when strong objective evidence supports it.**

This is the most difficult category to prove and the most inflammatory
to allege. Include only with senior attorney approval and only when the
record supports it.

Focus on objective indicators:
- Filing intended to delay proceedings (pattern of dilatory conduct)
- Filing intended to increase litigation costs (necessitating extensive
  motion practice on matters that should be undisputed)
- Filing intended to harass (pattern of unsupported counterclaims or
  baseless affirmative defenses)

**Never speculate about subjective intent.** Present the objective pattern
and let the court draw inferences.

### V. SANCTIONS REQUESTED

**Template:**
```
[If FRCP 11:]
Rule 11(c)(4) authorizes the Court to impose sanctions "limited to what
suffices to deter repetition of the conduct or comparable conduct by
others similarly situated." [LPB authority + pinpoint.]

[If represented party — monetary sanctions limited:]
Because [Respondent] is a represented party, monetary sanctions for
[Rule 11(b)(2) / (3) / (4)] violations are appropriately directed to
[Respondent's counsel]. Rule 11(c)(5)(A). [LPB authority + pinpoint.]

[If improper purpose (Rule 11(b)(1)) — monetary sanctions against party:]
For Rule 11(b)(1) violations (improper purpose), monetary sanctions may
be imposed on the [party / attorney / both]. [LPB authority + pinpoint.]

[MERGE:CaseName] respectfully requests that the Court impose the
following sanctions:

1. An order directing [Respondent / Respondent's counsel] to [withdraw /
   correct / amend] the [identified contentions / denials / defenses]
   within [N] days;

2. An award of [MERGE:CaseName]'s reasonable attorney's fees and costs
   incurred [in bringing this motion / as a result of the sanctionable
   conduct], in an amount to be established by [affidavit / declaration /
   supplemental briefing]; [Rule 11(c)(4) / NMRA 1-011(C)] [LPB + pinpoint];

3. [If warranted:] Such other nonmonetary directives as the Court deems
   appropriate to deter repetition, including [a requirement that counsel
   certify completion of a review of the applicable pleading rules /
   restrictions on future filings / other appropriate directives].

[If NMRA 1-011:]
NMRA 1-011(C) authorizes the Court to impose "appropriate sanctions"
after notice and opportunity to respond. [LPB authority + pinpoint.]

[MERGE:CaseName] requests:

1. An order directing [Respondent / Respondent's counsel] to [withdraw /
   correct / amend] the challenged [contentions / denials / defenses];

2. An award of reasonable attorney's fees and costs necessitated by the
   sanctionable conduct;

3. Such other sanctions as the Court deems appropriate.
```

---

### VI. CONCLUSION

**Template:**
```
WHEREFORE, [MERGE:CaseName] respectfully requests that this Court:

1. Find that [Respondent / Respondent's counsel] violated [Rule 11(b) /
   NMRA 1-011(A)] by [presenting / maintaining] the [identified
   contentions / denials / defenses] without [adequate factual basis /
   legal warrant / reasonable inquiry];

2. Order [Respondent / Respondent's counsel] to [withdraw / correct /
   amend] the offending [contentions / denials / defenses] within
   [N] days;

3. Award [MERGE:CaseName] reasonable attorney's fees and costs
   [incurred in connection with this motion / resulting from the
   sanctionable conduct];

4. Impose such additional sanctions as the Court deems sufficient to
   deter repetition; and

5. Grant such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.
```

## Exhibit Strategy

The motion should reference exhibits that the attorney will assemble:

| Exhibit | Content | Source |
|---------|---------|--------|
| A | The offending pleading (Answer, Motion, etc.) | SA document archive |
| B | Safe harbor notice / service documentation | Attorney correspondence file |
| C | Proof of service of safe harbor notice | Attorney correspondence file |
| D | Opposing counsel's response (if any) to safe harbor notice | Attorney correspondence file |
| E | Meet-and-confer correspondence (if any) | Meet-confer log |
| F | Record evidence contradicting improper denials | CFP PACKET sources |
| G | Billing records (if fees requested) | Firm billing system |

The BODY-ONLY draft references these as "(Exhibit [X])" — the attorney
attaches them during SA shell assembly.

## Safe Harbor Letter Structure (SAFE HARBOR LETTER Mode)

When operating in SAFE HARBOR LETTER mode, produce a letter (not a motion)
with the following structure:

### 1. Opening
- Identify the offending pleading
- State that this letter serves as the safe harbor notice under Rule 11(c)(2)

### 2. Identification of Certification Violations
- List each specific contention, denial, or defense that violates Rule 11(b)
- For each: cite the specific Rule 11(b) subsection violated and why
- Reference the record evidence contradicting the contention

### 3. Demand
- Demand withdrawal or correction of the identified contentions within 21 days
- State that if not withdrawn or corrected, the sanctions motion will be filed
- Specify the sanctions that will be sought

### 4. Professional Tone
- Firm but professional — this letter may be shown to the judge
- No threats beyond the procedural consequences stated in Rule 11
- Focus on the objective deficiencies, not opposing counsel's character
