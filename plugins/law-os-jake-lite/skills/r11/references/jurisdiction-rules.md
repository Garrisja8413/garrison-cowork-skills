# Jurisdiction Rules — R11 Reference

## New Mexico (State Court — NMRA 1-011)

### Certification Standard

- **Rule:** NMRA 1-011(A) — by presenting a pleading, motion, or other paper,
  an attorney certifies that to the best of the attorney's knowledge,
  information, and belief, formed after reasonable inquiry:
  - (1) It is not presented for any improper purpose
  - (2) Legal contentions are warranted by existing law or nonfrivolous argument
  - (3) Factual contentions have evidentiary support (or are identified as
    likely to have support after investigation)
  - (4) Denials are warranted on the evidence or reasonably based on lack of
    information or belief
  [VERIFY-CITE]

### Sanctions Procedure

- **Rule:** NMRA 1-011(C) — sanctions may be imposed after notice and a
  reasonable opportunity to respond [VERIFY-CITE]
- **No explicit safe harbor period** in the rule text — unlike FRCP 11(c)(2)
- **Best practice:** Provide written notice to opposing counsel identifying
  the specific certification deficiencies and allowing a reasonable time to
  cure before filing the motion
- **Court-initiated sanctions:** The court may initiate sanctions proceedings
  on its own, with notice and opportunity to respond [VERIFY-CITE]

### Who Can Be Sanctioned

- The attorney who signed or presented the pleading
- The party (if unrepresented)
- **Key distinction from federal:** NMRA 1-011 does not contain the FRCP 11
  limitation restricting monetary sanctions against represented parties to
  Rule 11(b)(1) violations only — confirm current NM practice [VERIFY-CITE]

### Types of Sanctions Available

- "Appropriate sanctions" — the rule gives broad discretion [VERIFY-CITE]
- Attorney's fees and costs
- Nonmonetary directives (withdrawal of pleading, corrective amendments)
- Monetary penalties (in severe cases)
- **Note:** NM courts generally follow the federal principle that sanctions
  should be the minimum sufficient to deter — confirm with NM case law
  [VERIFY-CITE]

### Standard of Review on Appeal

- Abuse of discretion [VERIFY-CITE]
- Appellate court reviews whether the trial court applied the correct legal
  standard and whether the sanction imposed was reasonable [VERIFY-CITE]

### NM-Specific Considerations

- NM courts may be more reluctant to impose sanctions than federal courts —
  sanctions are considered an "extraordinary remedy" [VERIFY-CITE]
- The movant should demonstrate a pattern, not an isolated instance
- NM Rules of Professional Conduct (16-301 through 16-804) provide additional
  framework but are not directly enforceable through 1-011 motions
- **Timing:** No specific deadline for filing sanctions motions in NMRA 1-011,
  but delay in seeking sanctions may undermine the motion. File promptly after
  the safe harbor / notice period expires
- **Local rules:** Check district-specific rules for any additional meet-and-
  confer or briefing requirements

## Federal — D.N.M. (FRCP 11 + Local Rules)

### Certification Standard

- **Rule:** FRCP 11(b) — by presenting a pleading, written motion, or other
  paper to the court, an attorney certifies that to the best of the person's
  knowledge, information, and belief, formed after an inquiry reasonable under
  the circumstances:
  - (1) Not presented for any improper purpose (harassment, unnecessary delay,
    needless increase in cost of litigation)
  - (2) Legal contentions warranted by existing law or nonfrivolous argument
    for extending, modifying, or reversing existing law or establishing new law
  - (3) Factual contentions have evidentiary support or, if specifically so
    identified, will likely have evidentiary support after further investigation
    or discovery
  - (4) Denials warranted on the evidence or, if specifically so identified,
    reasonably based on belief or a lack of information
  [VERIFY-CITE]

### Safe Harbor Procedure — MANDATORY

- **Rule:** FRCP 11(c)(2) — motion for sanctions "must be made separately from
  any other motion" [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Service requirement:** Must be served on the opposing party under Rule 5
  **at least 21 days before filing** with the court [VERIFY-CITE]
- **21-day cure period:** If the challenged paper, claim, defense, contention,
  or denial is withdrawn or appropriately corrected within 21 days after
  service, the motion **must not** be filed [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Strict compliance required:** Failure to comply with the safe harbor
  procedure is a basis for denial of the motion [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Partial cure:** If opposing party corrects some but not all violations,
  the motion may proceed as to the uncorrected violations only

**Safe harbor timeline:**
```
Day 0:  Serve sanctions motion on opposing counsel (not filed with court)
Day 1-21: Safe harbor period — opposing party may withdraw/correct
Day 22: If not withdrawn/corrected, file motion with court
```

### Who Can Be Sanctioned

- **Rule:** FRCP 11(c)(1) — sanctions may be imposed on attorneys, law firms,
  or parties [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Critical limitation — FRCP 11(c)(5)(A):** Monetary sanctions **must not**
  be awarded against a represented party for violating Rule 11(b)(2) (legal
  contentions) — only against the attorney/firm [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Rule 11(b)(1) exception:** Monetary sanctions for improper purpose may be
  imposed on the party, the attorney, or both [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Firm liability:** A law firm is held jointly responsible for violations by
  its partners, associates, or employees, unless the violation occurred in a
  context where joint responsibility would be unjust [VERIFY-CITE]

### Types of Sanctions Available

- **FRCP 11(c)(4):** Sanctions must be "limited to what suffices to deter
  repetition of the conduct or comparable conduct by others similarly situated"
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Nonmonetary directives:** Order to withdraw or correct the offending
  pleading, restrictions on future filings, education requirements
- **Monetary penalties payable to the court** (not to the movant, unless the
  court determines a different recipient is appropriate) [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Attorney's fees and costs:** Reasonable expenses, including attorney's fees,
  incurred as a direct result of the violation. Under Rule 11(c)(2) (motion-
  initiated), fees may be awarded to the movant [VERIFY-CITE]
- **FRCP 11(c)(5)(B):** Monetary sanctions against represented party only for
  Rule 11(b)(1) violations [VERIFY-CITE]

### Motion Requirements

- **Separate motion:** Must be a standalone filing, not combined with another
  motion [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Specificity:** Must describe the specific conduct alleged to violate
  Rule 11(b) [VERIFY-CITE]
- **D.N.M. LR-Civ 7.1(a):** Certification of good faith effort to resolve
  the matter may be required — check current local rules [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Brief limits:** Subject to D.N.M. page limits for motions

### Standard of Review on Appeal — 10th Circuit

- **Abuse of discretion** [VERIFY-CITE]
- **10th Circuit standard:** The court reviews (1) whether the legal standard
  was correctly applied, (2) whether factual findings are clearly erroneous,
  and (3) whether the sanction was reasonable [VERIFY-CITE]
- **De novo review** of the legal standard applied [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Clearly erroneous** standard for factual findings underlying the sanctions
  determination [VERIFY-CITE]

### Federal-Specific Considerations

- **Continuing obligation:** Rule 11(b) obligations are not limited to the
  date of signing — an attorney has a continuing duty to withdraw contentions
  that become unsupported [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Sua sponte sanctions:** The court may impose sanctions on its own initiative
  under Rule 11(c)(3), but must issue an order to show cause before imposing
  sanctions and may impose only nonmonetary sanctions (unless the conduct
  warranting sanctions was subject to Rule 11(c)(2) motion) [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Aiding and abetting:** Under 28 U.S.C. § 1927, an attorney who multiplies
  proceedings unreasonably and vexatiously may be required to satisfy the
  excess costs personally. This is a separate basis from Rule 11 [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Inherent authority:** The court retains inherent authority to sanction
  bad-faith conduct even beyond Rule 11. This requires a finding of bad faith,
  a higher standard than Rule 11's objective reasonableness [VERIFY-CITE]

## D.N.M. Judge-Specific Considerations

If a `<behavioral_layer>` (judge profile) is available, adjust:

- **Sanctions philosophy:** Some judges impose sanctions readily when
  certification violations are clear; others view sanctions as an
  extraordinary remedy requiring extreme circumstances
- **Safe harbor strictness:** Some judges require strict compliance with
  every procedural step; others take a more pragmatic approach
- **Monetary sanctions:** Some judges prefer nonmonetary sanctions
  (withdrawal, correction) before imposing fees; others award fees
  as a matter of course when violations are established
- **Tone preferences:** Some judges prefer measured, understated motions
  that let the record speak; others respond to more forceful advocacy
- **Hearing preferences:** Determine whether the judge decides on the papers
  or wants oral argument on sanctions

If no judge profile is available, draft in a conservative, evidence-driven
style and note in Open Items: `[DECISION REQUIRED] No judge profile provided —
consider adjusting tone and sanctions request for assigned judge.`

## Jurisdiction Detection

From the case docket or evidence_layer:
- `NM` or state court → NMRA 1-011
- `FED` or `D.N.M.` or federal court → FRCP 11 + D.N.M. local rules

If jurisdiction is unclear, flag `[DECISION REQUIRED — JURISDICTION]`:
"Confirm whether this case is in NM state court (NMRA 1-011) or federal
court (FRCP 11). The safe harbor procedure differs significantly."

## Cross-Jurisdiction Comparison

| Feature | NMRA 1-011 | FRCP 11 |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Certification standard | Substantially similar | Substantially similar |
| Safe harbor | Not specified in rule; best practice | Mandatory 21-day period |
| Separate motion requirement | Not specified | Required — FRCP 11(c)(2) |
| Monetary sanctions on represented party | Broad discretion [VERIFY-CITE] | Limited to Rule 11(b)(1) only |
| Continuing obligation | [VERIFY-CITE] | Yes — duty to withdraw |
| Sua sponte sanctions | Yes, with notice | Yes, nonmonetary only |
| Sanctions standard | Appropriate sanctions | Limited to deterrence |
| Fee awards | Available [VERIFY-CITE] | Available under (c)(2) motion |
| Appellate review | Abuse of discretion | Abuse of discretion |
