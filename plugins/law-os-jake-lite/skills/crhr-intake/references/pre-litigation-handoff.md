# Phase 6: Pre-Litigation Investigation & Handoff

## Purpose

Coordinate with criminal defense counsel, review the bodycam footage against
the client's narrative, ensure the client is receiving psychological treatment,
and establish the ongoing monitoring cadence that will carry the case through
pre-litigation and into filing.

## Step 15: Criminal Defense Coordination

### The 48-Hour Rule

Within 48 hours of case acceptance, the assigned Civil Rights Attorney MUST
contact the client's criminal defense attorney if:
- Criminal charges are pending (Heck status = PENDING-CHARGES)
- Criminal charges have been filed but not yet resolved
- The client was recently arrested and charges may be forthcoming

### Coordination Objectives

| Objective | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| Prevent destructive plea deals | A guilty plea to "Battery on a Peace Officer" or "Resisting Arrest" almost certainly destroys the excessive force civil claim under Heck v. Humphrey |
| Share discovery | The criminal attorney can subpoena bodycam video through criminal discovery (Rule 5-501 NMRA) faster than the civil firm can obtain it through IPRA |
| Align timelines | Criminal case timeline may affect when civil suit can be filed |
| Coordinate witness preparation | Same witnesses may testify in both proceedings |
| Avoid Fifth Amendment conflicts | Client's testimony in civil case could be used in criminal case if not coordinated |

### Key Coordination Questions for Criminal Attorney

| Question | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| "What are the current charges?" | Assess Heck exposure for each charge |
| "Is the prosecution offering a plea deal?" | Must evaluate civil rights impact of any plea |
| "Have you received bodycam footage through discovery?" | Criminal discovery is faster than IPRA |
| "Can you share the discovery with our firm?" | Need written consent from client |
| "What is the next court date?" | Calendar criminal milestones |
| "Is there any chance of dismissal?" | Dismissed charges = no Heck barrier |
| "Are there any conditions of release that restrict our client?" | May affect civil investigation |

### Plea Deal Review Protocol

**No plea deal should be accepted without civil rights attorney review.**

| Plea Offer | Civil Rights Impact | Recommendation |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| Dismissal of all charges | Ideal — removes all Heck barriers | Accept |
| Nolle prosequi | Good — charges dropped by prosecution | Accept (confirm no re-filing) |
| Deferred sentencing / diversion | Evaluate — may not trigger Heck | Review terms carefully |
| Plea to minor offense (trespass, disorderly) | May survive Heck analysis if not inconsistent with civil claim | Attorney must evaluate specific facts |
| Plea to resisting arrest | Potentially fatal to false arrest claim | OBJECT — negotiate alternative |
| Plea to battery on peace officer | Almost certainly fatal to excessive force claim | STRONGLY OBJECT — explore alternatives |
| Plea to assault on officer | Fatal to excessive force claim | STRONGLY OBJECT |
| Alford plea | May avoid Heck (no admission of facts) | Explore if available |
| Nolo contendere | May still trigger Heck in some circuits | Attorney must research current 10th Circuit law |

### Criminal Discovery Sharing Agreement

The client must sign a written authorization allowing the criminal defense
attorney to share discovery materials with the civil rights firm:

```
AUTHORIZATION TO SHARE CRIMINAL DISCOVERY

I, [Client Name], hereby authorize my criminal defense attorney,
[Attorney Name], to share all discovery materials received in
[Criminal Case No.] with my civil rights attorneys at [Firm Name]
for use in connection with my civil rights claims.

This authorization includes, but is not limited to:
- Body-worn camera footage
- Dashcam footage
- Police reports
- Witness statements
- Forensic reports
- Any other materials received through criminal discovery

I understand this sharing is for the purpose of pursuing my civil
rights claims and does not waive any privileges.

Client Signature: _______________
Date: _______________
```

## Step 16: The Video Review Party

### Upon IPRA Bodycam Footage Arrival

When the IPRA-requested bodycam footage arrives, the legal team schedules an
internal video review session. This is one of the most critical moments in the
case.

### Video Review Protocol

| Step | Action | Participants |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Schedule dedicated review time — no interruptions | Lead attorney + paralegal + case manager |
| 2 | Review all footage from start to finish — no skipping | Full team |
| 3 | Create detailed timeline log with timestamps | Paralegal |
| 4 | Compare footage against client's narrative point-by-point | Lead attorney |
| 5 | Identify discrepancies between video and client's story | Lead attorney |
| 6 | Identify discrepancies between video and police report | Lead attorney |
| 7 | Note all uses of force with exact timestamps | Paralegal |
| 8 | Note all commands/warnings given (or not given) | Paralegal |
| 9 | Identify all officers visible in footage | Paralegal |
| 10 | Identify all civilian witnesses visible | Paralegal |
| 11 | Document any gaps or interruptions in recording | Lead attorney |

### The Ethics Gate (Rule 11 / NMRA 1-011)

**CHECKPOINT 3 — This is a mandatory attorney decision point.**

After reviewing the footage, the attorney must make an honest assessment:

| Video Assessment | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| Video supports client's narrative | PROCEED — case is strengthened |
| Video is partially consistent but some details differ | PROCEED WITH CAUTION — adjust narrative, investigate further |
| Video shows officer misconduct not described by client | PROCEED — case may be stronger than client realized |
| Video is ambiguous / multiple interpretations | PROCEED — present strongest reasonable interpretation |
| Video shows camera was "accidentally" off or obstructed | PROCEED — missing footage itself is evidence of misconduct |
| Video definitively contradicts client's core narrative | EVALUATE — attorney must determine if any viable claim remains |
| **Video definitively shows client was the clear aggressor** | **DISENGAGE** — firm must withdraw before filing |

### Rule 11 / NMRA 1-011 Obligations

Under Fed. R. Civ. P. 11 (federal) and NMRA 1-011 (state), an attorney who
signs and files a pleading certifies that:
- The factual contentions have evidentiary support (or will likely have
  support after discovery)
- The claims are warranted by existing law or a nonfrivolous argument for
  change
- The filing is not for an improper purpose (harassment, delay, etc.)

**If the video unambiguously shows the client lied and was the clear aggressor,
filing a civil rights complaint would violate Rule 11.** Sanctions include
monetary penalties, costs, and attorney fees awarded to the defense.

### Disengagement Protocol (If Required)

If the video requires disengagement:
1. Attorney notifies client of the video findings
2. Explain that the video does not support the civil rights claims
3. Send formal withdrawal letter
4. Return any unused retainer funds
5. Advise client to seek independent counsel for evaluation
6. Do NOT return the video to the client (it was obtained through IPRA)
7. Log disengagement in `crhr_activity_log` with detailed reasoning

## Step 17: Psychological Management & Check-In Cadence

### The Dual Purpose of Counseling

Civil rights violations — especially police violence, wrongful arrest, and
jail abuse — cause severe psychological trauma. Consistent counseling serves
two essential purposes:

1. **Client well-being:** Genuine trauma recovery and support
2. **Damages evidence:** Documented counseling records are the primary way
   to prove and maximize emotional distress damages at trial

### Counseling Referral Protocol

| Task | Timeline | Responsible |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Ask if client is already in counseling | During INTERVIEW phase | Intake staff |
| If not, provide referral to trauma-specialized therapist | Within 1 week of signing | Case manager |
| Confirm client has scheduled first appointment | 2-week check-in | Case manager |
| Monitor ongoing attendance | Every 30-day check-in | Case manager |
| Obtain counseling records (with authorization) when needed | Pre-demand / pre-trial | Paralegal |

### Recommended Counseling Specialties

| Case Type | Counseling Specialty |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Police violence / excessive force | Trauma / PTSD specialist |
| Wrongful arrest / false imprisonment | Anxiety / PTSD counselor |
| Jail abuse / conditions | Institutional trauma specialist |
| Employment discrimination | Workplace trauma / career counseling |
| Housing discrimination | Stress / anxiety counselor |
| Sexual harassment / assault | Sexual trauma specialist |

### 30-Day Check-In Cadence

Set up automated, recurring CMS tasks:

| Cadence | Task | Assigned To |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Every 30 days | Client check-in call | Case manager |
| Every 30 days | Criminal case status check (SOPA) | Paralegal |
| Every 30 days | IPRA response tracking | Paralegal |
| Every 60 days | Attorney case strategy review | Lead attorney |
| Every 90 days | Comprehensive case assessment | Lead attorney + case manager |

### 30-Day Check-In Call Agenda

During each recurring client check-in:

| Topic | Questions / Actions |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Physical recovery** | "How are you feeling physically? Any new symptoms or medical visits?" |
| **Mental health** | "Are you attending your counseling appointments? How are you doing emotionally?" |
| **Criminal case** | "Any updates on your criminal case? Any court dates coming up? Has your criminal attorney contacted you?" |
| **Employment** (if applicable) | "Any changes in your employment situation? New job? Lost income?" |
| **IPRA status** | Update client on what records have been received or are still pending |
| **Social media** | "Have you maintained your social media lockdown? Any media contact?" |
| **Contact from defense** | "Has anyone from the city/county/state contacted you about this? Any investigators?" |
| **New witnesses** | "Have you remembered any other witnesses or discovered any new evidence?" |
| **Questions** | "Do you have any questions for us? Is there anything else you need?" |

## Transition to Litigation

When all evidence has been gathered, criminal case issues resolved (or
sufficiently managed), and the case remains viable after video review,
the case transitions from pre-litigation monitoring to active litigation
preparation:

1. Trigger **CFP** (Case Fact Pack) skill to build the evidentiary foundation
2. Trigger **LPB** (Law Pack Builder) to assemble applicable civil rights law
3. Use **PCM** (Proof of Claims Matrix) to map facts to legal elements
4. Draft complaint using **Complaint** skill
5. File in selected forum (NM State Court for NMCRA, D.NM for § 1983, or both)

The workflow chain for civil rights litigation:
```
CRHR-INTAKE → CO → LOR → CFP → LPB → PCM → Complaint → PA-DOC-FORMAT-PLEADINGS
```
