# Phase 2: Comprehensive Rights Interview

## Purpose

Establish the specific constitutional or statutory violation, inventory all
available evidence, identify witnesses, and evaluate the damages profile.
This phase produces the factual foundation for attorney case evaluation.

## Step 4: Incident Narrative & Claim Identification

### 4th Amendment Claims (Search / Seizure / Force)

These are the most common civil rights claims. The 4th Amendment protects
against unreasonable searches, seizures, and use of force by government actors.

| Data Point | Question | Required | Notes |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------|
| Initial contact | "What was the initial reason for the stop or contact with police?" | YES | Establishes whether the stop itself was lawful |
| Authority | "Did they show a warrant? Did they say you were under arrest?" | YES | Warrantless contact requires reasonable suspicion/probable cause |
| ID of officers | "How many officers were involved? Can you describe them? Did you get badge numbers or names?" | YES | Critical for identifying individual defendants |
| Agency | "What agency were they from? What did their uniforms/vehicles say?" | YES | Confirms entity classification from triage |
| Commands | "What commands or orders did the officers give you?" | YES | Graham v. Connor factor: were warnings given? |
| Compliance | "Did you comply with their commands?" | YES | Non-compliance doesn't justify excessive force but is relevant |
| Resistance | "Did you resist in any way? Pull away? Run?" | YES | Must be asked honestly — defense will claim resistance |
| Force used | "Describe exactly what force was used against you" | YES | Must be specific: taser, K-9, strikes, chemical agents, chokehold, etc. |
| Force sequence | "Walk me through the order of events — what happened first, second, third?" | YES | Timeline of escalation matters |
| Warnings before force | "Did officers give any verbal warnings before using force?" | YES | Graham v. Connor factor |
| After force | "What happened after the force was used? Were you handcuffed? Taken to hospital?" | YES | Post-force conduct can be a separate violation |
| Search | "Did they search you? Your car? Your home?" | YES | 4th Amendment search issues |
| Property seized | "Did they take anything from you? Phone? Money? Vehicle?" | YES | Property seizure claims |
| Duration of detention | "How long were you held? Were you booked into jail?" | YES | False arrest / false imprisonment damages |

### 1st Amendment Retaliation Claims

| Data Point | Question | Required | Notes |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------|
| Protected activity | "Were you filming the police when this happened?" | YES | Filming police is protected 1st Amendment activity |
| Protest/speech | "Were you protesting, demonstrating, or speaking out about something?" | YES | Political speech is core 1st Amendment protection |
| Criticism of police | "Had you recently filed a complaint against this officer or department?" | YES | Retaliation for complaints is actionable |
| Sequence | "Did the police action happen BECAUSE of your filming/speech/protest?" | YES | Must establish causal connection |
| Statements by officers | "Did any officer say anything about your filming/protest/speech?" | YES | Direct evidence of retaliatory motive |

### Jail/Prison Conditions Claims

| Data Point | Question | Required | Notes |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------|
| Facility | "Which facility were you housed in? Which pod/unit?" | YES | Identifies specific conditions |
| Medical neglect | "Did you request medical attention? How many times? Were you denied?" | YES | Deliberate indifference to serious medical needs (8th/14th Amend) |
| Assault by guards | "Were you physically assaulted by any staff member?" | YES | Excessive force in custody |
| Assault by inmates | "Were you assaulted by another inmate? Did staff know you were in danger?" | YES | Failure to protect claim |
| Conditions | "Describe the conditions — water, food, temperature, sanitation, overcrowding" | YES | Conditions of confinement claims |
| Solitary/segregation | "Were you placed in solitary or segregation? For how long? What reason?" | YES | Extended solitary may violate 8th Amendment |
| Property | "Was your personal property destroyed or confiscated?" | YES | Due process property claims |
| Grievances filed | "What grievances did you file? Do you have copies?" | YES | PLRA exhaustion documentation |

### Human Rights / Discrimination Claims (NMHRA)

| Data Point | Question | Required | Notes |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------|
| Protected class | "What do you believe is the reason for the discrimination?" | YES | Race, color, religion, national origin, ancestry, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, spousal affiliation, age (40+), physical/mental disability |
| Context | "Was this in employment, housing, or at a public business/accommodation?" | YES | Determines statutory framework |
| Employer size | "How many employees does this employer have?" | YES (employment) | Title VII requires 15+; NMHRA covers 4+ |
| Specific acts | "What specific actions were taken against you?" | YES | Hiring, firing, promotion denial, harassment, housing denial, service refusal |
| Comparators | "Were other people who are not [protected class] treated differently in the same situation?" | YES | Comparator evidence is critical for discrimination claims |
| Timeline | "When did each discriminatory act occur? Was it ongoing?" | YES | 300-day clock runs from each discrete act |
| Paper trail | "Do you have any emails, text messages, HR complaints, or written records?" | YES | Documentary evidence is extremely valuable |
| Witnesses | "Did anyone else see or hear the discriminatory conduct?" | YES | Corroboration |
| HR complaints | "Did you report this to HR, a supervisor, or management? What happened?" | YES | Internal complaint history matters |
| Retaliation | "After you complained, were you treated differently or punished?" | YES | Retaliation is a separate claim |
| Prior complaints | "Has anyone else filed complaints about this person or organization?" | YES | Pattern evidence |

## Step 5: Digital Evidence & Witness Identification

### Evidence Priority Matrix

Civil rights cases live and die by video evidence. Prioritize accordingly:

| Priority | Evidence Type | Source | Preservation Urgency |
|----------|-------------|--------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Bodycam/OBRD footage** | Police department via IPRA | HIGH — request within 48 hours |
| 2 | **Client cell phone video** | Client's device | IMMEDIATE — secure copy now |
| 3 | **Bystander video** | Civilian witnesses | HIGH — contact witnesses within 1 week |
| 4 | **Dashcam footage** | Police patrol vehicles via IPRA | HIGH — request within 48 hours |
| 5 | **Jail CCTV** | Facility via IPRA/preservation letter | CRITICAL — overwrites in 14-30 days |
| 6 | **Commercial surveillance** | Businesses near the scene | CRITICAL — overwrites in 14-30 days |
| 7 | **Social media posts/live streams** | Public platforms | MEDIUM — screenshot and archive |
| 8 | **911 audio / CAD records** | Dispatch center via IPRA | MEDIUM — request with IPRA batch |

### Bodycam Requirements in NM

APD, BCSO, and NMSP are legally required to wear body-worn cameras (OBRD):

| Agency | Policy | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| APD | SOP 2-8 — All uniformed officers and detectives during enforcement | Activation required for all calls for service and enforcement contacts |
| BCSO | BCSO Policy — All deputies with issued OBRD | Must be activated during all law enforcement encounters |
| NMSP | NMSP General Order — All officers with issued OBRD | Activation required for traffic stops, enforcement contacts |

**Key question:** "Were the officers wearing body cameras?" If the agency is
required to have them and claims no footage exists, this itself may be evidence
of policy violation or spoliation.

### Witness Identification Protocol

| Witness Type | Questions | Action |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Civilian bystanders | "Who else was there? Did anyone see what happened?" | Get full name, phone, address |
| Co-detainees (jail cases) | "Were other inmates present? Can you identify them?" | Get names, booking numbers if known |
| Medical personnel | "Who treated you afterward? Paramedics? ER staff?" | They are witnesses to injuries |
| Other officers | "Were there other officers who seemed to disagree with what happened?" | Potential favorable witnesses |
| Family members present | "Was anyone with you — spouse, child, parent?" | Eyewitnesses + bystander emotional distress |

**Police reports notoriously omit civilian witnesses who contradict the
officer's narrative.** Always ask the client independently.

## Step 6: Damages Assessment

| Data Point | Question | Required |
|-----------|----------|----------|

| ER treatment | "What did the ER diagnose? What treatment was provided?" | YES |
| Visible injuries | "Do you have visible scars, bruises, or marks?" | YES |

### Punitive Damages Assessment

Punitive damages are available under § 1983 (uncapped) and may be available
under the NMCRA. Evaluate:

| Factor | Question |
|--------|----------|
| Willfulness | Was the conduct intentional, not just negligent? |
| Recklessness | Did the officer act with reckless disregard for rights? |
| Pattern | Is this officer or agency known for similar conduct? |
| Cover-up | Did the officer file a false report? Destroy evidence? |
| Supervisory failure | Did supervisors know about the officer's history and fail to act? |
