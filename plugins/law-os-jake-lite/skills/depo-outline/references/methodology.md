# DEPO-OUTLINE Reference: Examination Methodology

## Purpose

This reference defines the deposition examination methodology used by the
DEPO-OUTLINE skill. It covers the funnel technique for question sequencing,
topic organization strategies, element-driven questioning principles, and
mode-specific examination approaches (direct, cross, expert, 30(b)(6)).

---

## 1. The Funnel Technique

The funnel technique is the foundational questioning methodology for all
deposition outlines. It moves from broad, open-ended questions to narrow,
specific questions within each topic area.

### Funnel Structure (Per Topic)

```
LEVEL 1: BACKGROUND (Broad, Open)
  "Tell me about your role at [Company]..."
  "Describe your involvement with..."
  Purpose: Build rapport, establish foundation, let witness talk

LEVEL 2: NARROWING (Focused)
  "Specifically regarding [event], what did you..."
  "When you say [term], what do you mean by that..."
  Purpose: Focus on specific facts, clarify terms, establish timeline

LEVEL 3: KEY QUESTIONS (Targeted)
  "On [date], at [time], you were at [location], correct?"
  "You reviewed [document] before making that decision, didn't you?"
  Purpose: Establish critical facts, elicit admissions, create record

LEVEL 4: LOCK-DOWN (Pinpoint)
  "So it's your testimony today that [specific statement]?"
  "There's nothing else that would change your answer, correct?"
  Purpose: Commit witness to position, close escape routes, preserve for trial
```

### Funnel Application by Examination Type

| Level | Direct Exam | Cross Exam |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Level 1 | Open narrative questions | Brief background only |
| Level 2 | Guided follow-up | Transition to leading questions |
| Level 3 | Foundation questions | Leading questions with doc confrontation |
| Level 4 | Summarize favorable testimony | Lock-down commitments |

### Why the Funnel Works

1. **Prevents premature commitment:** Witnesses who are pressed too early
   become defensive and qualify everything
2. **Reveals unexpected information:** Open questions at Level 1 may surface
   facts not in the CFP or DPB
3. **Builds a progression:** The record shows the witness was given every
   opportunity to explain before being pinned down
4. **Supports trial presentation:** The jury sees a fair examiner who gave
   the witness room to explain

## 2. Topic Sequencing Strategy

### Default Sequencing Order (Cross Examination)

For adverse witnesses, sequence topics to maximize control and minimize
witness preparation for confrontation:

```
1. BACKGROUND & RAPPORT (5-10 min)
   - Professional background, role, employment history
   - Establishes the witness as competent (useful for binding admissions)
   - Non-threatening; witness relaxes

2. UNDISPUTED FACTS (10-15 min)
   - Facts both sides agree on
   - Timeline events not in dispute
   - Gets witness in "yes" pattern (agreeing)
   - Establishes credibility of examiner

3. FAVORABLE ADMISSIONS (15-25 min)
   - Facts that support our case elements
   - Duty acknowledgments, standard-of-care testimony
   - Causation testimony
   - Damages testimony
   - Each admission tied to a PCM element

4. DOCUMENTS & FOUNDATIONS (10-15 min)
   - Authenticate documents needed at trial
   - Establish witness authored/received/reviewed documents
   - Lay foundation for exhibits

5. AREAS OF VULNERABILITY (15-25 min)
   - Topics where witness testimony may be inconsistent
   - Prior statement confrontation preparation zones
   - Qualification or hedging areas
   - Knowledge gaps

6. IMPEACHMENT CONFRONTATION (10-20 min)
   - Prior inconsistent statements (commit-credit-confront)
   - Document contradictions
   - Internal inconsistencies from earlier in the deposition
   - Save strongest impeachment for last

7. CLEAN-UP & LOCK-DOWN (5-10 min)
   - Return to any topics needing clarification
   - Final lock-down questions on critical admissions
   - Ensure all elements are addressed
```

### Default Sequencing Order (Direct Examination)

For friendly witnesses, sequence topics to build a compelling narrative:

```
1. BACKGROUND & CREDIBILITY (5-10 min)
   - Qualifications, experience, relationship to the case
   - Establishes witness as trustworthy and knowledgeable

2. SCENE SETTING (5-10 min)
   - Context and circumstances before the incident
   - What was normal / expected

3. NARRATIVE DEVELOPMENT (20-30 min)
   - Chronological account of events
   - What the witness saw, heard, did
   - Sensory details and emotional context
   - Tied to liability elements

4. DAMAGES TESTIMONY (10-20 min)
   - Impact on the plaintiff
   - Before vs. after comparisons
   - Specific, concrete examples
   - Tied to damages elements

5. DOCUMENT SUPPORT (5-10 min)
   - Documents that corroborate testimony
   - Records, photographs, communications

6. ADDRESSING WEAKNESSES (5-10 min)
   - Proactively address known vulnerabilities
   - Explain apparent inconsistencies before opposing counsel exploits them
   - Show completeness and honesty
```

### Sequencing Modifications by Case Type

**Motor Vehicle Accident (PI):**
- Lead with undisputed accident facts (police report, scene)
- Move to liability (speed, attention, signals, visibility)
- Then causation (mechanism of injury)
- Close with damages (treatment, impact)

**Premises Liability:**
- Lead with property ownership/control
- Move to knowledge of condition
- Then to notice (actual or constructive)
- Causation of the specific injury
- Close with damages

**Medical Malpractice:**
- Lead with qualifications and standard of care
- Move to what was done (breach)
- Then causation (medical causation)
- Close with damages and prognosis

**Product Liability:**
- Lead with product identification and use
- Move to defect (design, manufacturing, or warning)
- Then causation
- Close with damages

## 3. Element-Driven Questioning

### Core Principle

Every question topic in the outline must serve a litigation purpose traceable
to a legal element from the LPB/PCM. Questions without element connections
are excluded or flagged as `[DECISION REQUIRED: No element connection]`.

### Element-to-Question Mapping Process

```
Step 1: Identify all elements from LPB/PCM that this witness can address
Step 2: For each element, identify the proof status from PCM:
        - UNPROVEN: highest priority — must develop testimony
        - FACTS-ONLY: need testimony to strengthen factual record
        - PROVEN: lower priority — lock down existing proof
Step 3: Map each element to specific question topics
Step 4: Within each topic, draft questions at all four funnel levels
Step 5: Identify supporting documents for each topic
Step 6: Flag impeachment opportunities for each topic
```

### Priority Mapping

| PCM Status | Question Priority | Outline Treatment |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| UNPROVEN | CRITICAL | Must include; extensive questioning; multiple approaches |
| FACTS-ONLY | HIGH | Include; focused questioning to establish testimony |
| PROVEN | MEDIUM | Include for lock-down; brief confirmation |
| CONCEDED | LOW | Brief confirmation only; do not over-examine |

### NM-Specific Elements (Common PI Claims)

**Negligence (UJI 13-1601 through 13-1604):**
1. Duty of care owed by defendant
2. Breach of that duty (what a reasonably prudent person would have done)
3. Cause-in-fact (the negligence was a cause of the injury)
4. Proximate cause (foreseeability)
5. Damages (actual harm suffered)

**Negligence per se (NMSA 1978 Section 66-8-114, traffic violations):**
1. Defendant violated a statute or regulation
2. The statute was designed to protect the class of persons plaintiff belongs to
3. The violation was a cause of the harm
4. Damages

**Premises liability (UJI 13-1309 through 13-1316):**
1. Defendant owned/controlled/maintained the premises
2. Dangerous condition existed
3. Defendant knew or should have known (notice)
4. Defendant failed to exercise ordinary care
5. Causation and damages

**Products liability (NMSA 1978 Section 66-1-1 et seq. for vehicles):**
1. Product was defective (design, manufacturing, or warning)
2. Defect existed when product left defendant's control
3. Defect was a cause of plaintiff's injuries
4. Damages

## 4. Question Formulation Patterns

### Direct Examination Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Open narrative** | "Describe what happened when..." | Elicit story in witness's own words |
| **Scene setting** | "Where were you on [date]?" | Establish context |
| **Sensory** | "What did you see/hear/feel?" | Create vivid record |
| **Temporal** | "What happened next?" | Build chronology |
| **Clarifying** | "When you say [X], what do you mean?" | Pin down meaning |
| **Foundation** | "Have you seen this document before?" | Lay exhibit foundation |
| **Impact** | "How has this affected your [daily life/work]?" | Develop damages |

### Cross Examination Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Leading (fact)** | "You were driving 45 mph, correct?" | Establish specific facts |
| **Leading (admission)** | "You agree that X is true?" | Extract admissions |
| **Prior statement** | "You previously stated under oath that..." | Set up impeachment |
| **Document confrontation** | "Let me show you Exhibit 3. This is your report?" | Confront with documents |
| **Exhaustion** | "Is there anything else? Nothing else? You're sure?" | Close escape routes |
| **Looping** | "You just told us X. And earlier you said Y..." | Highlight contradictions |
| **Chapter method** | Complete one topic fully before moving to next | Maintain control |

### 30(b)(6) Examination Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Preparation scope** | "What did you do to prepare for this deposition?" | Assess adequacy of preparation |
| **Knowledge scope** | "Does the company have information about X beyond what you've told us?" | Test completeness |
| **Binding confirmation** | "So the company's position is that [X]?" | Create organizational admission |
| **Documents reviewed** | "What documents did you review in preparation?" | Identify document universe |
| **Other persons** | "Who else at the company has knowledge about this topic?" | Identify additional witnesses |

## 5. SDC Compliance: Telegraphing Prevention

### What Not to Telegraph

When SDC (Strategic Disclosure Controller) identifies items as THEORY-HIDDEN
or UNDISCLOSED, the deposition outline must avoid questions that reveal:

1. **Hidden legal theories** — Do not ask questions that only make sense if you
   are pursuing a specific undisclosed theory
2. **Undisclosed evidence** — Do not reference documents or facts that have not
   been disclosed through discovery
3. **Future witness strategy** — Do not telegraph which witnesses you plan to
   call or what they will say
4. **Settlement posture** — Do not ask questions that reveal settlement strategy

### How to Handle SDC Restrictions

- If a question topic would telegraph a hidden theory:
  `[DECISION REQUIRED: This question may telegraph THEORY-HIDDEN item [ID].
  Confirm with SDC REVEAL authorization before including.]`

## 6. Impeachment Integration

### How DEPO-OUTLINE Uses Impeachment Sources

When DEPO-IMPEACH output or DPB prior statements are available, the outline
integrates impeachment opportunities at the topic level:

```
### Impeachment Setup (within topic block)

1. COMMIT: Get witness to restate current position firmly
   - "Your testimony today is that [current statement], correct?"
   - "You're sure about that?"
   - "Nothing would change your answer?"

2. CREDIT: Establish the reliability of the prior statement
   - "You were under oath when you answered interrogatories?"
   - "You reviewed your answers before signing?"
   - "You understood the importance of being accurate?"

3. CONFRONT: Present the prior inconsistent statement
   - "Let me read from your [source]: [verbatim quote]"
   - "Did I read that correctly?"
   - [Let the contradiction speak for itself — do not argue]
```

### Impeachment Sourcing Requirements

Every impeachment reference must include:
- Exact source (DPB SetID/ReqNum, prior depo page:line, document Bates#)
- Verbatim quote of the prior statement
- The specific contradiction identified
- Foundation requirements under NMRA 11-613 or FRE 613

If the source cannot be verified: `[VERIFY-IMPEACH: Source not confirmed]`

## 7. Document Usage Strategy

### Pre-Deposition Document Organization

For each topic in the outline, identify:
1. **Documents to mark as exhibits** — Documents that will be shown to the witness
2. **Documents for confrontation** — Prior statements or contradicting documents
3. **Documents for foundation** — Documents that need authentication by this witness
4. **Documents to withhold** — Documents in our possession but strategically
   better to use at trial (coordinate with SDC)

### Document Reference Format in Outline

```
**Doc: [Bates range or Exhibit#]** — [Brief description]
Purpose: [Authentication / Confrontation / Corroboration / Foundation]
Questions:
- "I'm showing you what has been marked as Exhibit [N]. Do you recognize this?"
- "This is [description], correct?"
- [Topic-specific questions using the document]
```

## 8. Time Allocation Guidelines

### Federal (7-Hour Limit)

With a hard 7-hour limit under FRCP 30(d)(1), time allocation is critical:

| Topic Category | Percentage | Approx. Time |
|----------------|------------|--------------|
| Background | 5-10% | 20-40 min |
| Liability topics | 30-40% | 2-3 hours |
| Damages topics | 20-25% | 1.5-2 hours |
| Document examination | 10-15% | 45-60 min |
| Impeachment | 10-15% | 45-60 min |
| Clean-up / buffer | 5-10% | 20-40 min |

### NM State (No Time Limit)

While NM has no presumptive time limit, practical considerations apply:

| Guideline | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Standard fact witness | 3-4 hours |
| Key party witness | 4-6 hours |
| Expert witness | 4-6 hours |
| Corporate designee (30(b)(6)) | 4-8 hours depending on topics |
| Non-party fact witness | 1-3 hours |

## 9. Mode-Specific Methodology Notes

### DIRECT Mode

- Emphasis on open questions that let the witness tell the story
- Minimize leading questions (judge at trial may sustain objections to leading)
- Develop the narrative chronologically
- Preserve favorable testimony for trial use (deposition of unavailable witness)
- Consider: if the witness becomes unavailable, will this deposition transcript
  effectively present the testimony needed?

### CROSS Mode

- Emphasis on leading questions and witness control
- Never ask a question you do not know the answer to (or do not control the answer)
- Chapter method: complete each topic before moving to the next
- End each chapter with a lock-down question
- Keep questions short (one fact per question)
- Use documents to control the witness

### EXPERT Mode

- Begin with qualifications (establish and then challenge)
- Map opinions to report sections
- Probe methodology (Daubert/NMRA 11-702 factors)
- Challenge assumptions and data relied upon
- Compare to learned treatise positions
- Explore bias (compensation, repeat retention, litigation history)
- Specific attention to NM UJI standards for expert testimony

### CORPORATE-30b6 Mode

- Organize strictly by noticed topic designations
- Begin each topic by assessing designee preparation adequacy
- Explore whether organization has additional information beyond what
  the designee knows
- Create binding organizational admissions on each topic
- Flag inadequate preparation for potential motion to compel
