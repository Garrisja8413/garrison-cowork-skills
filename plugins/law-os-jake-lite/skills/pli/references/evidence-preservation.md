# Evidence Preservation & Spoliation Prevention

## Purpose

Ensure all relevant evidence is preserved before it can be destroyed,
overwritten, or lost. This reference covers spoliation letters, preservation
protocols, and Tort Claims Notice filing for NM pre-litigation investigation.

---

## Spoliation Letters

### What Is a Spoliation Letter?

A spoliation letter (also called a "preservation of evidence letter" or
"litigation hold letter") is a written demand to an adverse party or third
party to preserve evidence relevant to a potential legal claim. Failure to
preserve evidence after receiving a spoliation letter can result in adverse
inference instructions, sanctions, or even default judgment.

### When to Send

**Within 48 hours of case acceptance.** Every hour of delay increases the risk
that surveillance footage is overwritten, vehicles are repaired, or records
are purged per routine retention policies.

### Who Receives Spoliation Letters

| Recipient | Evidence at Risk |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Defendant (individual) | Vehicle, dashcam, cell phone data, clothing |
| Defendant's employer (if commercial vehicle) | Driver logs, vehicle maintenance, GPS/telematics, drug test results |
| Defendant's insurance carrier | Recorded statements, adjuster notes, photos |
| Business at incident location | Surveillance video, incident reports, maintenance logs |
| Government agency (police, DOT) | Bodycam, dashcam, traffic camera footage, CAD logs |
| Tow company | Vehicle condition photos, storage location |
| Body shop / repair facility | Pre-repair photos, damage assessment, replaced parts |
| Hospital / EMS | ER photos, triage notes, ambulance records |
| Cell phone carrier | Cell tower data, usage records (for distracted driving) |
| Vehicle manufacturer | EDR/black box data specifications |
| Property owner/manager | Surveillance, maintenance logs, inspection records |

### Spoliation Letter Template — General

```
[FIRM LETTERHEAD]

VIA CERTIFIED MAIL, RETURN RECEIPT REQUESTED
AND VIA EMAIL: [email]

[Date]

[Recipient Name / Custodian of Records]
[Company/Entity Name]
[Address]

RE: PRESERVATION OF EVIDENCE DEMAND
    Incident Date: [DOI]
    Incident Location: [Location]
    Our Client: [Client Name]
    Your Insured / [Relationship]: [Name]

Dear [Recipient]:

This firm represents [Client Name] in connection with injuries sustained
on [DOI] at [Location]. This letter serves as formal notice that
[Client Name] intends to pursue legal claims arising from this incident.

YOU ARE HEREBY DIRECTED TO IMMEDIATELY PRESERVE AND MAINTAIN all
documents, electronically stored information (ESI), tangible items,
and other evidence relating to this incident, including but not
limited to:

1. ALL VIDEO AND PHOTOGRAPHIC EVIDENCE
   - Surveillance camera footage (interior and exterior)
   - Dashcam or in-vehicle camera recordings
   - Body-worn camera footage
   - Traffic camera footage
   - Photographs taken of the scene, vehicles, or injuries
   - Drone or aerial footage

2. ALL ELECTRONIC DATA
   - GPS and telematics data from vehicles
   - Event Data Recorder (EDR / "black box") data
   - Cell phone records, text messages, and call logs
   - Computer-Aided Dispatch (CAD) records
   - Radio transmission recordings
   - Mobile Data Terminal (MDT) communications
   - Email communications regarding the incident

3. ALL DOCUMENTS AND RECORDS
   - Incident reports and accident reports
   - Police reports and supplements
   - Investigation files
   - Witness statements
   - Employee records for involved personnel
   - Training records for involved personnel
   - Maintenance and inspection records for involved vehicles/premises
   - Insurance policies and claim files
   - Prior incident reports at the same location
   - Safety audits and inspection records

4. ALL PHYSICAL EVIDENCE
   - Vehicles involved (DO NOT repair, sell, or dispose)
   - Vehicle components and parts
   - Clothing worn by involved parties
   - Equipment or products involved
   - Debris from the scene

This preservation obligation attaches immediately upon receipt of this
letter. ANY destruction, alteration, or failure to preserve the above
evidence — whether pursuant to routine document retention policies or
otherwise — may constitute spoliation of evidence and subject your
organization to significant legal sanctions, including adverse inference
instructions, monetary penalties, and other remedies available under
New Mexico and federal law.

Please confirm receipt of this letter and your compliance with this
preservation demand in writing within ten (10) days.

If you have any questions regarding the scope of this preservation
request, please contact our office immediately.

Sincerely,

[Attorney Name]
[Firm Name]
[Address]
[Phone]
[Email]

cc: [Client file]
```

### Spoliation Letter — Commercial Vehicle Supplement

For trucking and commercial vehicle cases, add these specific items:

```
In addition to the general items above, you are specifically directed
to preserve the following:

DRIVER RECORDS:
- Driver qualification file (49 CFR 391.51)
- Hours of Service (HOS) logs — paper and electronic (ELD)
- Drug and alcohol testing records (pre-employment, random, post-accident)
- Medical examiner's certificate and medical history
- Driving record (MVR) for the past 3 years
- Training records and safety certifications
- Prior accidents and incidents involving this driver

VEHICLE RECORDS:
- Vehicle inspection reports (pre-trip and post-trip)
- Maintenance and repair records
- Tire records (purchase dates, tread depth measurements, rotation)
- Brake adjustment and inspection records
- Annual inspection reports (49 CFR 396.17)
- Out-of-service reports

ELECTRONIC DATA:
- Electronic Logging Device (ELD) data for 30 days before and after incident
- GPS tracking data for 30 days before and after incident
- Engine Control Module (ECM) data
- Forward-facing camera footage (if equipped)
- Telematics data (speed, braking, acceleration)

COMPANY RECORDS:
- Safety policies and procedures
- FMCSA inspection and audit results
- Compliance Safety Accountability (CSA) scores
- Prior complaints and violations
- Insurance policy declarations pages
```

### Spoliation Letter — Premises Liability Supplement

For slip-and-fall and premises cases, add:

```
In addition to the general items above, you are specifically directed
to preserve the following:

SURVEILLANCE AND DOCUMENTATION:
- ALL security camera footage from [date range] covering [specific areas]
- Incident/accident reports completed by your staff
- Photographs taken by your staff or agents

MAINTENANCE AND INSPECTION:
- Maintenance logs and work orders for [specific area]
- Floor cleaning schedules and products used
- Snow/ice removal schedules and contractors
- Inspection checklists and completed forms
- Repair requests for the area where the incident occurred
- Prior complaints about the same condition

PROPERTY RECORDS:
- Building plans and blueprints for the area
- ADA compliance assessments
- Building code inspection reports
- Lease agreements (if tenant premises)
- Property management contracts
- Vendor contracts for cleaning, maintenance, and snow removal

PRIOR INCIDENTS:
- All incident reports for [similar incidents] at this location
  for the past 5 years
- All claims or lawsuits involving [similar conditions] at this
  location for the past 5 years
- Workers' compensation claims related to [similar conditions]
```

## Tort Claims Notice (New Mexico)

### When Required

A Tort Claims Notice is required when the defendant is a New Mexico
governmental entity or public employee acting within the scope of duty.

**Statutory basis:** NMSA Section 41-4-16(A) — New Mexico Tort Claims Act

**Deadline:** Written notice must be filed within **90 days** after the
date of the occurrence giving rise to the claim.

**If missed:** The claim is barred. There is no cure, no extension, and
no equitable tolling in most circumstances. This is the single most
important deadline in any government-defendant case.

### Who Must Receive Notice

| Defendant Type | Notice Recipient | Statute |
|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| State of New Mexico | Risk Management Division, General Services Dept | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| State agency or employee | Risk Management Division + agency head | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| County | County Clerk + County Manager | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| Municipality (city/town) | City/Town Clerk | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| School district | Superintendent + Risk Management | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| Public hospital | Hospital administrator + Risk Management | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| University | President + General Counsel + Risk Mgmt | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| Law enforcement (state) | NMSP Chief + Risk Management | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |
| Law enforcement (local) | Chief/Sheriff + City/County Clerk | NMSA 41-4-16(A) |

**Best practice:** Send to BOTH the specific entity AND the Risk Management
Division. Over-notice is always safer than under-notice.

### Required Content

The Tort Claims Notice must contain (per NMSA 41-4-16(B)):

1. **Date** of the notice
2. **Name and address** of the claimant
3. **Date, time, place, and circumstances** of the occurrence
4. **Name of the governmental entity** and, if known, the public employee(s)
5. **Description of the damages** claimed
6. **Amount of damages** claimed (or a statement that damages exceed $10,000)

## Vehicle Preservation Protocol

### When to Preserve the Vehicle

Preserve the vehicle when:
- High-speed impact suggesting vehicle defect
- Airbag deployment failure or unexpected deployment
- Seatbelt failure
- Tire blowout or mechanical failure
- Need for EDR (Event Data Recorder / black box) download
- Vehicle is evidence of force of impact for damages

### EDR (Black Box) Download

**Timing is critical.** The EDR must be downloaded BEFORE:
- The vehicle is repaired (repair can overwrite data)
- The vehicle is sold at auction
- The vehicle is scrapped/crushed
- The battery dies or is disconnected for extended period (some systems)

**How to preserve:**
1. Send spoliation letter to tow company / storage yard: DO NOT release vehicle
2. Retain qualified EDR download technician
3. Download data using Bosch CDR tool (most common) or manufacturer-specific tool
4. Obtain certified report from the technician
5. Photograph the vehicle exterior and interior before and after download

**What EDR captures (varies by make/model/year):**
- Pre-crash speed (typically 5 seconds before impact)
- Brake application (yes/no and timing)
- Throttle position
- Steering input
- Seatbelt status
- Airbag deployment timing
- Delta-V (change in velocity at impact)
- Engine RPM

### Vehicle Storage

If the vehicle must be preserved:
1. Notify client's insurance carrier of preservation need
2. Arrange storage at a secure facility
3. Document vehicle condition upon arrival (photos, written description)
4. Maintain chain of custody log for all access
5. Do NOT allow repairs until attorney authorizes release

## Digital Evidence Preservation

### Social Media Preservation

**Client's social media:**
1. Instruct client to set ALL profiles to private (IKP welcome packet)
2. Instruct client NOT to delete any posts (spoliation risk)
3. Download and preserve current state of all profiles
4. Screenshot relevant posts with timestamps and URLs

**Adverse party and witness social media:**
1. Search and screenshot relevant public posts
2. Use timestamps — content may be deleted
3. Consider third-party preservation services for authenticated captures
4. Do NOT create fake accounts or use deception to access private profiles

### Cell Phone Data

**Client's phone:**
- Preserve text messages related to the incident
- Preserve photos and videos from the time of the incident
- Download call logs showing activity at time of incident
- GPS/location data if relevant

**Defendant's phone (for litigation):**
- Cannot access pre-suit — note in investigation file for discovery
- Spoliation letter should demand preservation of phone data
- Cell tower records require subpoena (carrier records)

### Dashcam / Vehicle Camera

If client or defendant had a dashcam:
1. Obtain the memory card or cloud account access (client)
2. Preserve original files — do not edit or compress
3. Note timestamps and file metadata
4. Send spoliation letter demanding preservation (defendant)
