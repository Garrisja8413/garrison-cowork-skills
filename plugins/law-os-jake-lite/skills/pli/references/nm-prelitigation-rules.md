# New Mexico Pre-Litigation Rules & Requirements

## Purpose

NM-specific statutes, deadlines, and procedural requirements that govern
pre-litigation investigation for personal injury cases in New Mexico.

## New Mexico Tort Claims Act — Key Provisions

### Government Immunity and Waivers

The NM Tort Claims Act (NMSA 41-4-1 et seq.) provides governmental immunity
with specific waivers. A claim must fit within a statutory waiver or it is
barred regardless of fault.

**Common Waivers (Waiver of Immunity):**

| Waiver | Statute | Covers |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Motor vehicle operation | NMSA 41-4-5 | Government employee negligent operation of motor vehicle |
| Building/premises | NMSA 41-4-6 | Dangerous condition of building or grounds |
| Airport operation | NMSA 41-4-7 | Airport-related negligence |
| Medical facilities | NMSA 41-4-9 | Healthcare at public hospitals, clinics |
| Law enforcement | NMSA 41-4-12 | Assault, battery, false imprisonment, malicious prosecution by LEOs |
| Public utilities | NMSA 41-4-8 | Operation of gas, water, electric utilities |

### Damages Cap (Tort Claims Act)

| Period | Cap Amount | Statute |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Before 7/1/2021 | $200,000 per occurrence | NMSA 41-4-19(A) (former) |
| 7/1/2021 through 6/30/2022 | $400,000 per occurrence | NMSA 41-4-19(A)(2) |
| 7/1/2022 through 6/30/2023 | $600,000 per occurrence | NMSA 41-4-19(A)(3) |
| 7/1/2023 through 6/30/2024 | $750,000 per occurrence | NMSA 41-4-19(A)(4) |
| On or after 7/1/2024 | $1,000,000 per occurrence | NMSA 41-4-19(A)(5) |
| Medical malpractice (public facility) | $750,000 per occurrence | NMSA 41-4-19(B) |

**Note:** The NM Civil Rights Act (NMSA 41-4A-1 et seq.) has NO damages cap
for constitutional violations. If both TCA and CRA claims exist, the CRA
claim avoids the TCA cap.

## Comparative Fault

### NM Pure Comparative Fault Rule

New Mexico follows **pure comparative fault** under NMSA 41-3A-1.

- A plaintiff's recovery is reduced by their percentage of fault
- A plaintiff can recover even if 99% at fault (recovery = 1% of damages)
- The jury allocates fault among all parties including nonparties (NMSA 41-3A-1(B))
- This is more favorable to plaintiffs than modified comparative fault states

### Impact on Investigation

| Investigation Focus | Why It Matters |
|--------------------|---------------|
| Defendant's fault evidence | Primary case — prove defendant was negligent |
| Client's potential fault | Anticipate defense arguments; investigate and mitigate |
| Third-party fault | Other defendants to add or fault to allocate away from client |
| Comparative fault percentages | Build facts supporting low client fault allocation |

## Pre-Suit Demand Requirements

### NM Insurance Code — Policy Limits Disclosure

**NMSA 39-2-2(B):** Upon written request from a claimant or their attorney,
an insurer must disclose policy limits within 30 days.

- Send written request to defendant's insurer after Letter of Representation
- If insurer refuses or fails to respond, this may support bad faith claim
- Document all requests and responses

### Pre-Suit Demand Letter Best Practices (NM)

Before filing suit, most PI cases should attempt pre-suit resolution:

1. **Complete investigation first** — Never send a demand before investigation is done
2. **Include all supporting documentation** — Medical records, bills, wage loss, photos
3. **State a specific demand amount** — Must be reasonable and supported by evidence
4. **Set a reasonable response deadline** — 30 days is standard in NM
5. **Reference applicable law** — Negligence elements, comparative fault, damages categories
6. **Preserve bad faith arguments** — Document all insurer conduct for potential extra-contractual claim

## Insurance Requirements and Rules

### NM Mandatory Insurance Minimums

**NMSA 66-5-215 — Financial Responsibility:**

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|--------------|--------------|
| Bodily injury (per person) | $25,000 |
| Bodily injury (per accident) | $50,000 |
| Property damage (per accident) | $10,000 |

### UM/UIM Requirements

**NMSA 66-5-301 — Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage:**

- UM/UIM must be offered with every auto policy
- Rejection must be in writing, signed by named insured
- Invalid rejection = statutory minimum UM/UIM applies
- Stacking: permitted unless anti-stacking provision properly offered and accepted

**Key Cases:**
- *Jordan v. Allstate* (2010 NM): Rejection form must be separate from policy application
- *Montano v. Allstate* (2019 NM): Each renewal without valid rejection resets coverage
- *Progressive Nw. Ins. Co. v. Weed Warrior Servs.* (2019 NM): Stacking applies absent valid waiver

### MedPay / PIP

- MedPay pays regardless of fault up to policy limits
- No deductible, no copays
- Claim should be filed immediately at case acceptance
- Some carriers assert subrogation rights — check policy language
- MedPay can fund treatment while investigation proceeds

## Pre-Litigation Discovery Tools

### Inspection of Public Records Act (IPRA)

**NMSA 14-2-1 et seq.**

IPRA allows any person to request and inspect public records from New Mexico
government entities without demonstrating a need or interest.

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Who can request | Any person (no standing requirement) |
| Response deadline | 15 business days, extendable to 30 for "large or complex" |
| Format | Written request (email acceptable) |
| Fees | Reasonable copy costs; first hour of search free |
| Appeal | Administrative appeal to AG, then mandamus action in district court |
| Penalties | Damages and attorney fees for unreasonable denials (NMSA 14-2-12) |

**Best practices:**
- Send separate, targeted requests (video, reports, personnel) to avoid "large/complex" delay
- Send by email AND certified mail for proof of service
- Request "unedited, native format" files — agencies try to produce curated excerpts
- Calendar the 15-business-day deadline and follow up promptly
- If denied, file administrative appeal with the AG immediately

### Pre-Suit Subpoenas

New Mexico does not generally allow pre-suit subpoenas for discovery purposes.
Pre-suit evidence gathering relies on:

1. **IPRA** (government records)
2. **Voluntary production** with authorization (medical, employment records)
3. **Spoliation letters** (preservation demands)
4. **Insurance carrier cooperation** (claim file, recorded statements)
5. **Court order** under NMRA 1-027.1 (pre-suit deposition to perpetuate testimony in narrow circumstances)

### Pre-Suit Deposition to Perpetuate Testimony

**NMRA 1-027(A):** A person who expects to be a party to an action may
petition the court to perpetuate testimony if:

- The petitioner expects to be unable to bring the action within the SOL OR
- The prospective witness is elderly, ill, or about to leave the jurisdiction

This is rarely used but critical when a key witness is dying or leaving NM.

## Liens and Subrogation

### Common Lien Types in NM PI Cases

| Lien Holder | Authority | Impact on Recovery |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Medicare | 42 U.S.C. 1395y(b)(2) (MSP Act) | Must repay Medicare's payments; conditional payment must be resolved before settlement |
| Medicaid | NMSA 27-2-20.1 | State Medicaid lien; must be satisfied from recovery |
| Health insurer (ERISA) | 29 U.S.C. 1132 | Federal preemption; must repay per plan terms |
| Health insurer (non-ERISA) | NMSA 59A-22-31 | NM made-whole doctrine and common fund apply |
| Hospital lien | NMSA 48-8-1 et seq. | Must be filed within 120 days of discharge; max 50% of recovery |
| VA / Tricare | 38 U.S.C. 1729; 10 U.S.C. 1095 | Federal liens; must be resolved |
| Workers' compensation | NMSA 52-5-17 | WC carrier has lien on third-party recovery |
| LOP provider | Contract | Contractual lien; negotiate at settlement |
| Child support (state) | NMSA 40-4A-5 | State may intercept settlement |

### Lien Investigation Protocol

During pre-litigation investigation:

1. **Identify all payors** — Who has paid for client's medical treatment?
2. **Confirm lien status** — Has the payor asserted a lien or subrogation right?
3. **Obtain lien amounts** — Request itemized lien statements
4. **Evaluate lien reduction** — NM common fund and made-whole doctrines may reduce non-federal liens
5. **Calendar Medicare conditional payment** — Must request conditional payment statement from MSPRC
6. **Factor liens into case valuation** — Net recovery after liens determines case viability

## Ethics Rules Relevant to Pre-Litigation Investigation

### NMRA 16-402 — Communication with Represented Persons

A lawyer shall not communicate about a matter with a person the lawyer knows
is represented by another lawyer, unless:
- The other lawyer consents, OR
- Authorized by law or court order

**Impact on investigation:** Before contacting any adverse party, verify
whether they have counsel. If represented, all contact must go through
their attorney.

### NMRA 16-403 — Dealing with Unrepresented Persons

When dealing with unrepresented witnesses or adverse parties:
- Do not give legal advice (except advice to get a lawyer)
- Do not imply disinterestedness
- Identify yourself and your role
- Clarify whose interests you represent

### NMRA 16-304(a) — Fairness to Opposing Party and Counsel

A lawyer shall not:
- Unlawfully obstruct another party's access to evidence
- Falsify evidence
- Counsel or assist a witness to testify falsely
- Make frivolous discovery requests

### NMRA 16-804(c) — Conduct Involving Dishonesty

A lawyer shall not engage in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or
misrepresentation. This applies to investigation methods — no pretexting,
misrepresentation of identity, or deceptive practices to obtain evidence.

## NM-Specific Investigation Considerations

### Tribal Land Jurisdiction

If the incident occurred on tribal land (Pueblo, Navajo Nation, Apache):

| Issue | Consideration |
|-------|--------------|
| Jurisdiction | Tribal courts may have concurrent or exclusive jurisdiction |
| Sovereign immunity | Tribal entities have sovereign immunity unless waived |
| Federal Indian law | May implicate FTCA if federal road/facility involved |
| Investigation access | May need tribal permission to access the scene |
| Applicable law | Tribal law may differ from NM state law |

**Action:** Consult with an attorney experienced in Indian law before
investigating cases on tribal land.

### Border / Federal Land Issues

| Location | Jurisdiction | Implications |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Interstate highways | State (usually) | Normal NM investigation |
| National forests / BLM | Federal | FTCA applies; federal SOL and procedures |
| Military bases | Federal | FTCA applies; restricted access |
| Border Patrol checkpoints | Federal | FTCA and Section 1983 possible |
| National parks | Federal | FTCA applies |
