# RFP Objection Validity Standards — OA-RFP Reference

**Version:** 1.0
**Applies to:** OA-RFP (Objection Analyzer — Requests for Production)

## Governing Rules

### Federal
- **FRCP 34(a):** Scope — documents, ESI, tangible things, land inspection
- **FRCP 34(b)(1)(A):** Reasonable particularity requirement for requests
- **FRCP 34(b)(1)(C):** Form of ESI production
- **FRCP 34(b)(2)(A):** 30-day response period
- **FRCP 34(b)(2)(B):** Response must state with specificity grounds for objection; state whether items withheld
- **FRCP 34(b)(2)(C):** Objection to part requires production of non-objectionable remainder
- **FRCP 34(b)(2)(E):** Organization of production (as kept or labeled to correspond)
- **FRCP 26(b)(1):** Proportionality factors for scope of discovery
- **FRCP 26(b)(2)(C):** Court limits on cumulative, unreasonable, or disproportionate discovery
- **FRCP 26(b)(5)(A):** Privilege log requirements

### New Mexico
- **NMRA 1-034:** Requests for production (tracks federal rule with NM modifications)
- **NMRA 1-034(B):** Response within 30 days of service
- **NMRA 1-026(B):** Proportionality factors (NM variant)

## Proportionality Analysis Framework (FRCP 26(b)(1))

Six factors for proportionality:
1. Importance of the issues at stake in the action
2. Amount in controversy
3. Parties' relative access to relevant information
4. Parties' resources
5. Importance of the discovery in resolving the issues
6. Whether the burden or expense outweighs the likely benefit

## ESI-Specific Standards

### Form of Production
- Requesting party may specify form (FRCP 34(b)(1)(C))
- If no form specified, produce as ordinarily maintained OR in reasonably usable form
- Need not produce same ESI in more than one form

### Reasonably Accessible vs. Not Reasonably Accessible
- Party need not provide discovery of ESI from sources identified as not reasonably accessible due to undue burden or cost (FRCP 26(b)(2)(B))
- If challenged, responding party must show sources are truly inaccessible
- Court may order production from inaccessible sources with conditions (cost-shifting)

### Metadata Preservation
- Producing party must preserve metadata unless parties agree otherwise
- ESI protocol should address metadata handling explicitly

## Privilege Log Requirements (FRCP 26(b)(5)(A))

When withholding on privilege grounds, the privilege log must:
1. Describe the nature of the documents, communications, or tangible things not produced
2. Do so in a manner that enables other parties to assess the claim without revealing privileged information
3. Include sufficient detail: date, author, recipients, subject matter, privilege claimed
