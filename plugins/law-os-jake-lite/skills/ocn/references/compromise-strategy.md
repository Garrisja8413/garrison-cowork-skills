# Compromise Strategy Framework — OCN Reference

**Version:** 1.0
**Applies to:** OCN (Objection Compromise Negotiator)

## Purpose

The OCN (Objection Compromise Negotiator) generates compromise proposals for discovery
disputes. This reference defines the strategy framework for constructing proposals that
maximize information yield while respecting legitimate objections.

## Compromise Tier Structure

### Tier 1 — Full Concession
The requesting party concedes the objection and narrows the request.
- **When to use:** Objection is clearly VALID with strong legal basis
- **Benefit:** Builds credibility with court; demonstrates reasonableness
- **Example:** Withdraw overbroad request and substitute narrow version

### Tier 2 — Partial Narrowing
The requesting party narrows scope but preserves core information sought.
- **When to use:** Objection is PARTIALLY-VALID; some narrowing is reasonable
- **Benefit:** Compromises without surrendering important discovery
- **Example:** Limit date range from "all time" to "5 years preceding incident"

### Tier 3 — Alternative Method
Propose a different discovery mechanism to obtain the same information.
- **When to use:** The objection targets the method, not the information itself
- **Benefit:** Preserves access to information through a less objectionable path
- **Example:** Convert overbroad interrogatory to focused 30(b)(6) deposition topic

### Tier 4 — Phased Discovery
Propose obtaining information in stages rather than all at once.
- **When to use:** Burden/proportionality objection; information may narrow with discovery
- **Benefit:** Reduces upfront burden; later phases targeted by earlier results
- **Example:** Initial production of key custodians; additional custodians if needed

### Tier 5 — Conditional Withdrawal with Reservation
Withdraw the request but reserve the right to re-serve after additional discovery.
- **When to use:** Premature contention or expert-related objection
- **Benefit:** Avoids motion practice; preserves right
- **Example:** Withdraw contention ROG pre-deposition; re-serve post-deposition

## Good Faith Letter Integration

OCN proposals are designed to feed directly into the GFL (Good Faith Letter) skill:
- Each compromise proposal maps to a GFL request-by-request entry
- Tier classification determines the language and tone of the GFL proposal
- Proposals rejected in the GFL process feed the MTC (Motion to Compel)

## Negotiation Principles

1. **Lead with reasonableness** — Offer concessions before demanding compliance
2. **Document the meet-and-confer** — Every proposal creates a record for the court
3. **Preserve the record** — Rejected compromises strengthen MTC arguments
4. **Group related requests** — Negotiate related discovery as a package
5. **Quantify burden** — When narrowing, specify the reduction in scope/cost
