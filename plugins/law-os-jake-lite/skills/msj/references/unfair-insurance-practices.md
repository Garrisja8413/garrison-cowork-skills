# MSJ Theory: Unfair Insurance Practices

## Theory Code: UNFAIR-INSURANCE

## Governing Law

- **NMSA 1978, § 59A-16-20** — Unfair claims practices; penalties
- **NMSA 1978, § 59A-16-19** — Unfair claims settlement practices defined
- **NMSA 1978, § 59A-16-30** — Private right of action
- **Dydek v. Dydek**, 2012-NMCA-088 `[VERIFY-CITE]`
- **Sloan v. State Farm Mut. Auto. Ins. Co.**, 2004-NMSC-004 `[VERIFY-CITE]`
- **Hovet v. Allstate Ins. Co.**, 2004-NMSC-010 `[VERIFY-CITE]`

## NM Standard

NMSA § 59A-16-20 makes it unlawful for an insurer to engage in unfair
claims settlement practices. § 59A-16-19 defines specific prohibited
practices. An insured may bring a private action under § 59A-16-30 for
violations.

Unlike common law bad faith, the statutory claim does not require proof
of bad faith intent — the focus is on whether the insurer's conduct falls
within the enumerated prohibited practices. `[VERIFY-CITE]`

## Element Checklist

### Element 1: Insurance Relationship
- Defendant is an insurer or person engaged in the business of insurance
- A valid insurance policy exists between insurer and insured
- Plaintiff is a first-party insured (or third-party claimant with standing)

### Element 2: Claim Presented
- Plaintiff presented a claim under the policy
- Notice was proper and timely
- Claim triggered the insurer's obligations under the policy

### Element 3: Unfair Claims Practice (one or more)
Enumerated practices under § 59A-16-20:

- **(A)** Misrepresenting pertinent facts or policy provisions relating to coverage
- **(B)** Failing to acknowledge and act promptly upon communications regarding claims
- **(C)** Failing to adopt and implement reasonable standards for prompt investigation of claims
- **(D)** Refusing to pay claims without conducting a reasonable investigation
- **(E)** Failing to affirm or deny coverage within a reasonable time after proof of loss
- **(F)** Not attempting in good faith to effectuate prompt, fair, and equitable settlement when liability is reasonably clear
- **(G)** Compelling insureds to institute litigation by offering substantially less than amounts ultimately recovered
- **(H)** Attempting to settle claims for less than a reasonable person would believe to be owed based on advertising materials
- **(I)** Attempting to settle on the basis of an application altered without consent
- **(J)** Making claims payments without explanation of coverage denial
- **(K)** Unreasonable delay in claim settlement
- **(L)** Failure to promptly settle one portion of a claim to influence settlement of another
- **(M)** Failure to provide reasonable explanation of denial
- **(N)** Advising claimant not to obtain counsel
- **(O)** Misleading claimant about applicable statute of limitations

### Element 4: Damages
- Actual damages caused by the unfair practice
- Consequential damages (additional costs from delay, emotional distress)
- Attorney's fees and costs
- Potential punitive damages for willful conduct

## Burden of Proof

**Plaintiff bears the burden** of proving the enumerated unfair practice
and resulting damages. Unlike common law bad faith, no requirement to prove
the insurer acted with subjective bad faith intent — objective violation
of § 59A-16-20 is sufficient. `[VERIFY-CITE]`

## Relationship to Other Claims

Unfair insurance practices claims are typically brought alongside:
- **Breach of contract** (failure to pay covered claim)
- **UPA** (§ 57-12-1 et seq.) — unfair/deceptive trade practices
- **Common law bad faith** — requires proof of bad faith intent (higher bar)
- **Declaratory judgment** — to establish coverage as threshold issue

These claims overlap but are distinct. Each provides different remedies.
The statutory claim (§ 59A-16-20) provides the most specific framework.

## Common Defense Arguments to Anticipate

- Claim investigation was reasonable and timely
- Denial was based on legitimate coverage dispute (reasonable basis)
- Payment was prompt once liability was clear
- Insured failed to cooperate or provide requested documentation
- Damages not caused by the alleged unfair practice
- No private right of action (standing issues) `[VERIFY-CITE]`

## Key Evidence Types for SUMF

- Insurance policy and claim documentation
- Claim file (adjuster notes, investigation timeline, internal memos)
- Correspondence between insured and insurer (demands, denials, offers)
- Timeline of claim handling (dates of notice, investigation steps, decisions)
- Denial letters with stated reasons
- Settlement offers vs. actual value of claim
- Expert testimony on industry claims handling standards
- Insurer's internal guidelines and procedures

## SUMF Organization

1. Insurance relationship facts (policy, parties, coverage)
2. Claim presentation facts (what was claimed, when, how)
3. Insurer's claims handling facts (chronological — what they did/didn't do)
4. Specific unfair practice facts (mapped to § 59A-16-20 subsections)
5. Damages facts (what the unfair practice cost plaintiff)
