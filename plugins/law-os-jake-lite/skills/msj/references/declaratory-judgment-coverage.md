# MSJ Theory: Declaratory Judgment — Insurance Coverage Disputes

## Theory Code: DECL-JUDGMENT

## Governing Law

- **NMSA 1978, § 44-6-1 et seq.** — Declaratory Judgment Act
- **Foundation Reserve Ins. Co. v. Mullenix**, 1982-NMSC-038 `[VERIFY-CITE]`
- **Knowles v. United Services Auto. Ass'n**, 1992-NMSC-030 `[VERIFY-CITE]`
- **Am. Gen. Fire & Cas. Co. v. Christiania Gen. Ins. Corp.**,
  1997-NMSC-019 `[VERIFY-CITE]`

## NM Standard

The Declaratory Judgment Act authorizes courts to declare the rights,
status, and other legal relations of parties under a contract (including
insurance policies) where there is an actual controversy.

In insurance coverage disputes, either the insured or the insurer may
seek a declaratory judgment to resolve whether a policy provides coverage
for a particular claim or loss.

**NM rules of insurance policy construction:**
1. Policy language is given its ordinary meaning `[VERIFY-CITE]`
2. Ambiguity is construed against the insurer (contra proferentem) `[VERIFY-CITE]`
3. Exclusions are narrowly construed `[VERIFY-CITE]`
4. The insurer bears the burden of proving an exclusion applies `[VERIFY-CITE]`

## Element Checklist

### Element 1: Actual Controversy
- A justiciable controversy exists between the parties
- The dispute is ripe (not hypothetical or moot)
- The parties have adverse interests regarding coverage

### Element 2: Valid Insurance Policy
- A valid insurance policy exists between the parties
- Identify the policy (number, effective dates, insured, insurer)
- Identify the relevant policy provisions (insuring agreement, definitions,
  conditions, exclusions)
- Quote the operative policy language with pinpoint to section/page

### Element 3: Covered Occurrence / Claim
**For insured seeking coverage:**
- The loss or claim falls within the policy's insuring agreement
- The occurrence meets the policy definition
- All conditions precedent to coverage have been satisfied
  (timely notice, cooperation, proof of loss)

**For insurer denying coverage:**
- The loss or claim does not fall within the insuring agreement, OR
- A policy exclusion applies to bar coverage

### Element 4: Policy Interpretation
- If policy language is unambiguous → interpretation is a question of law
  (favorable for MSJ)
- If ambiguous → construe against insurer (still may be decided on MSJ
  if only one reasonable interpretation exists)
- Apply NM canons of insurance policy construction

### Element 5: Exclusion Analysis (if insurer asserts exclusion)
- Identify the specific exclusion relied upon
- Quote the exclusion language with pinpoint
- Insurer bears the burden of proving the exclusion applies
- Construe exclusion narrowly
- If exception to exclusion exists, analyze whether it restores coverage

### Element 6: Duty to Defend vs. Duty to Indemnify
- **Duty to defend:** Determined by the allegations in the complaint
  (broader — if any allegation potentially falls within coverage,
  duty to defend exists) `[VERIFY-CITE]`
- **Duty to indemnify:** Determined by actual facts as established
  (narrower — only if actual facts trigger coverage)
- Can seek declaratory judgment on either or both

## Movant Considerations

**When insured is movant (seeking coverage):**
- Focus: policy language covers the claim; no exclusion applies
- Favorable if policy language is clear and insurer's denial is based on
  a strained reading

**When insurer is movant (denying coverage):**
- Focus: clear exclusion applies; or claim does not fall within insuring agreement
- Must prove exclusion applies (burden on insurer)
- Ambiguity defeats insurer's MSJ

## Common Defense Arguments to Anticipate

**If insured moves:**
- Policy exclusion applies
- Conditions precedent not met (late notice, failure to cooperate)
- Policy language is ambiguous (creates fact issue)
- Coverage was not in effect (lapse, cancellation)

**If insurer moves:**
- Unambiguous exclusion language bars coverage
- Claim does not fall within the insuring agreement
- Insured breached conditions (late notice, misrepresentation)
- Other insurance / priority of coverage

## Key Evidence Types for SUMF

- The insurance policy (full policy with endorsements and declarations)
- The underlying claim or complaint (triggering the coverage question)
- Denial letter(s) with stated bases for denial
- Correspondence between insured and insurer
- Proof of loss documentation
- Notice of claim documentation (dates, method)
- Claims file entries (adjuster notes, investigation)
- Any reservation of rights letter

## SUMF Organization

1. Policy facts (parties, policy number, effective dates, premiums paid)
2. Relevant policy language (insuring agreement, definitions, exclusions)
3. Underlying claim/occurrence facts
4. Notice and claims handling facts
5. Insurer's coverage position (denial basis)
6. Policy interpretation facts (if any extrinsic evidence relevant)
