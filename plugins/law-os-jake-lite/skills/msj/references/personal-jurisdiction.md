## Theory Code: PERSONAL-JURIS

## NM Standard

NM's long-arm statute extends personal jurisdiction to the fullest extent
permitted by due process. The analysis is therefore a constitutional due
process analysis under the Fourteenth Amendment.

For specific personal jurisdiction, the court examines:
1. Whether the defendant purposefully directed activities at the forum state
2. Whether the claim arises from those forum-directed activities
3. Whether exercising jurisdiction is reasonable

## Element Checklist

### Element 1: Long-Arm Statute
- NM: NMSA 1978, § 38-1-16 (coextensive with due process)
- Fed: Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(k)(1)(A) + state long-arm
- This element is typically satisfied if due process is met

### Element 2: Purposeful Direction / Availment
- Defendant deliberately targeted the forum state
- Defendant's contacts with the forum were not random, fortuitous, or
  attenuated
- Defendant invoked the benefits and protections of forum law
- Focus on defendant's own contacts — not plaintiff's unilateral activity

### Element 3: Arising From / Relatedness
- Plaintiff's claims arise out of or relate to defendant's forum contacts
- There is a sufficient nexus between the forum contacts and the claims
- Causal connection between forum activity and the harm alleged

### Element 4: Reasonableness
If minimum contacts are established, jurisdiction is presumptively reasonable.
Defendant may defeat jurisdiction by showing it would be unreasonable:
- Burden on defendant of litigating in the forum
- Forum state's interest in adjudicating the dispute
- Plaintiff's interest in obtaining convenient and effective relief
- Interstate judicial system's interest in efficiency
- Shared interest of states in furthering substantive social policies

## Burden of Proof

**Plaintiff bears the burden** of establishing personal jurisdiction once
defendant properly challenges it. `[VERIFY-CITE]`

**Defendant moving for MSJ** must show that undisputed facts negate
minimum contacts, or that jurisdiction would be constitutionally unreasonable.

## Common Defense Arguments to Anticipate

- Contacts were insufficient (no purposeful direction)
- Claims don't arise from the forum contacts
- Exercise of jurisdiction would be unreasonable
- General jurisdiction argument (defendant not "at home" in NM)

## Key Evidence Types for SUMF

- Evidence of defendant's contacts with NM (contracts, communications,
  visits, business operations, marketing)
- Evidence of where events giving rise to claims occurred
- Corporate registration, agent designations
- Deposition testimony on defendant's NM activities
- Website targeting, advertising in NM

## SUMF Organization

1. Defendant's identity and domicile facts
2. Defendant's NM contacts facts (chronological)
3. Facts linking the claims to the NM contacts
4. Reasonableness factor facts (if contested)
