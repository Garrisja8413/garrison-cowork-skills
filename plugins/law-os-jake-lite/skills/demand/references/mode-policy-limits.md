# DEMAND Mode: POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND Reference

## Purpose

The POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND mode produces a demand for the full policy limits that is specifically designed to establish the carrier's exposure to bad faith liability if it fails to tender. Unlike the TIME-LIMITED-DEMAND (which emphasizes the deadline mechanism), the POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND emphasizes the bad faith setup: demonstrating that liability is clear, damages exceed limits, and the carrier's failure to pay creates extracontractual exposure.

This mode is used when the primary strategic objective is to create a documented record of the carrier's unreasonable failure to settle, building the foundation for a potential bad faith claim.

---

## Part 1: Distinction from TIME-LIMITED-DEMAND

| Feature | TIME-LIMITED-DEMAND | POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Primary mechanism | Strict deadline for acceptance | Bad faith exposure documentation |
| Deadline emphasis | Prominent, enforceable | Present but secondary to bad faith setup |
| Damages presentation | Shows excess exposure | Extensive proof that damages far exceed limits |
| Bad faith language | Consequences section | Woven throughout with specific statutory references |
| Coverage confirmation | Requested if unknown | REQUIRED before demand is effective |
| Tone | Formal, urgent | Detailed, methodical, building an evidentiary record |
| Strategic goal | Force quick settlement decision | Create paper trail for bad faith claim if carrier refuses |

### 1.1 When to Use POLICY-LIMITS vs. TIME-LIMITED

Use POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND when:
- Damages clearly and dramatically exceed policy limits (e.g., limits are $100K, damages exceed $500K)
- The carrier has a history of bad faith behavior or lowball offers
- You want to build the strongest possible bad faith record
- Coverage has been confirmed and limits are known
- The case involves catastrophic injuries or wrongful death

Use TIME-LIMITED-DEMAND when:
- Damages exceed limits but not dramatically
- You want to force a quick settlement decision
- The case is approaching trial and you need resolution
- The deadline mechanism itself is the primary leverage

## Part 2: NM Bad Faith Framework for Policy Limits Demands

### 2.1 Elements of Bad Faith in NM

To establish bad faith, the plaintiff must show:

1. **The insurer owed a duty of good faith** -- exists in every insurance relationship (first-party and third-party)
2. **The insurer breached that duty** -- by failing to settle when settlement was reasonable
3. **The breach was unreasonable** -- no reasonable insurer would have refused to settle under the circumstances
4. **Damages resulted** -- excess judgment, litigation costs, emotional distress

**Key NM cases:**

| Case | Holding | Relevance |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Sloan v. State Farm, 2004-NMSC-004 | Insurer has duty of good faith; breach gives rise to tort liability | Foundation for all NM bad faith claims |
| Ambassador Ins. Co. v. St. Paul, 1984-NMSC-107 | Insurer may be liable for excess judgment if unreasonably refuses to settle within limits | Excess exposure liability |
| Dydek v. Dydek, 2012-NMCA-088 | Bad faith can include failure to investigate claims properly | Investigation duty |
| Sloan (damages) | Bad faith damages may include emotional distress, punitive damages, and attorney fees | Scope of bad faith damages |
| Charter Servs., Inc. v. Principal Mut. Life Ins. Co., 1994-NMSC-036 | Totality of circumstances test for bad faith | Evaluation standard |

### 2.2 Building the Bad Faith Record

The POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND must create a self-contained record that demonstrates:

| Element | How the Demand Establishes It |
|---------|------------------------------|
| Liability is clear | Detailed liability section with undisputed facts |
| Damages exceed limits | Itemized damages with DC support, showing multiples of limits |
| Demand was reasonable | Demand is for policy limits only (not excess of limits) |
| Demand was clear | Unambiguous terms, specific amount, specific acceptance method |
| Demand was unconditional | No hidden conditions, capable of immediate acceptance |
| Carrier had adequate time | Reasonable deadline with documentation of delivery |
| Carrier was warned of consequences | Express bad faith consequences section |
| Claim was fully documented | Comprehensive enclosures supporting every damages claim |

### 2.3 Coverage Confirmation Requirements

**CRITICAL:** A policy limits demand requires confirmed policy limits. Without confirmed limits, the demand cannot be effective.

**Coverage confirmation request language:**
```
Before or concurrent with this demand, we request that [CARRIER NAME]
confirm in writing:

1. All applicable policy limits under any policy providing coverage for
   this claim, including but not limited to:
   - Bodily injury liability limits (per person and per occurrence)
   - Umbrella or excess liability limits
   - Any other applicable coverage

2. Whether any coverage defenses are being asserted
3. Whether any reservation of rights has been issued
4. The identity of all insurers providing coverage for this claim

This information is discoverable under NMRA 1-026(B)(2) and failure to
disclose may itself constitute unfair claims practices under NMSA 59A-16-20.
```

## Part 3: Policy Limits Demand Structure

### Section 1: Introduction and Coverage Confirmation

```
DEMAND FOR POLICY LIMITS

This demand is made on behalf of [CLIENT NAME] for payment of the full
available policy limits under all applicable policies providing coverage
for the claim arising from the [INCIDENT DATE] incident involving
[INSURED NAME].

Policy Information:
  Carrier: [CARRIER NAME]
  Policy Number: [POLICY NUMBER]
  Bodily Injury Limits: $[LIMITS] per person / $[LIMITS] per occurrence
  [Umbrella/Excess: $[LIMITS] if applicable]

[If limits not confirmed: We have requested confirmation of policy limits.
This demand assumes limits of $[AMOUNT] based on [source of information].
If the actual limits differ, this demand is for the full available limits
under all applicable policies. FAILURE TO DISCLOSE ACCURATE POLICY LIMITS
WILL BE NOTED IN ANY SUBSEQUENT BAD FAITH PROCEEDING.]
```

### Section 2: Liability (Emphasis on Clarity)

**Key differences from standard demand:**
- Every liability fact must be presented as undisputed or overwhelmingly established
- Use language that triggers NMSA 59A-16-20(F): "liability is reasonably clear"
- If defendant has made any admissions (in pleadings, discovery, statements), highlight them
- If police report supports plaintiff, cite it prominently
- Address comparative fault to show it is minimal or inapplicable

**Template pattern:**
```
LIABILITY IS CLEAR AND UNDISPUTED

The evidence establishes beyond any reasonable dispute that [INSURED NAME]
was negligent and that this negligence was the direct and proximate cause
of [CLIENT NAME]'s injuries.

[FACT 1 -- strongest liability fact] (CFP Fact#XX)
[FACT 2] (CFP Fact#XX)
[FACT 3] (CFP Fact#XX)

[If police report favorable:] The investigating officer's report confirms
[KEY FINDINGS]. (CFP Fact#XX, Pinpoint: Police Report at [page]).

[If defendant admission:] [INSURED NAME] has acknowledged [ADMISSION].
(CFP Fact#XX, Source: [source]).

Under these circumstances, no reasonable insurer could dispute that liability
is clear within the meaning of NMSA 59A-16-20(F), which requires [CARRIER NAME]
to attempt in good faith to effectuate a prompt, fair settlement.
```

### Section 3: Damages Far Exceeding Policy Limits

**Key differences from standard demand:**
- Present damages as a MULTIPLE of policy limits
- Use specific dollar amounts for every category
- Show the excess exposure calculation explicitly
- Include future damages projections to increase the multiplier

**Excess exposure calculation template:**
```
DAMAGES ANALYSIS AND EXCESS EXPOSURE

Category                          Amount
-----------------------------------------
Past Medical Expenses:            $[AMOUNT]
Future Medical Expenses:          $[AMOUNT]
Past Lost Wages:                  $[AMOUNT]
Future Lost Earning Capacity:     $[AMOUNT]
Past Pain and Suffering:          $[AMOUNT]
Future Pain and Suffering:        $[AMOUNT]
Loss of Enjoyment of Life:        $[AMOUNT]
[Other applicable categories]     $[AMOUNT]
-----------------------------------------
CONSERVATIVE TOTAL:               $[TOTAL]

Available Policy Limits:          $[LIMITS]
EXCESS EXPOSURE:                  $[TOTAL - LIMITS]

[CLIENT NAME]'s damages are approximately [X] times the available
policy limits. The excess exposure to [INSURED NAME] is $[EXCESS AMOUNT].

[If DVP available:] Comparable NM verdicts in similar cases confirm
this valuation:
  - [Case 1]: $[VERDICT] ([County] County, [Year])
  - [Case 2]: $[VERDICT] ([County] County, [Year])
  - [Case 3]: $[VERDICT] ([County] County, [Year])
```

### Section 4: Bad Faith Exposure Analysis

This section is UNIQUE to the POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND. It explicitly walks the carrier through its bad faith exposure:

```
BAD FAITH EXPOSURE ANALYSIS

Under New Mexico law, [CARRIER NAME] has a duty to act in good faith
toward both its insured, [INSURED NAME], and the claimant, [CLIENT NAME].
Sloan v. State Farm, 2004-NMSC-004.

The following factors establish that this is a case requiring settlement
within policy limits:

1. LIABILITY IS CLEAR. [Summary of liability strength].

2. DAMAGES SUBSTANTIALLY EXCEED LIMITS. [CLIENT NAME]'s conservative
   damages total $[TOTAL], which is [X] times the $[LIMITS] available
   policy limits.

3. THIS DEMAND IS REASONABLE. We are demanding only the policy limits --
   a fraction of [CLIENT NAME]'s actual damages.

4. THIS DEMAND IS CLEAR AND UNCONDITIONAL. The terms for acceptance
   are set forth below and require only payment and a standard release.

Under these circumstances, failure to tender the policy limits would
constitute a violation of:

  - NMSA 59A-16-20(F): Not attempting in good faith to effectuate
    prompt, fair settlement when liability has become reasonably clear.

  - NMSA 59A-16-20(G): Compelling the claimant to institute litigation
    to recover amounts due by offering substantially less than the
    amount ultimately recovered.

Should [CARRIER NAME] fail to tender the policy limits, [CLIENT NAME]
will pursue all available remedies, including:

  - A judgment at trial that will substantially exceed the policy limits
  - Assignment of [INSURED NAME]'s bad faith claims against [CARRIER NAME]
  - Pre-judgment interest at 15% per annum (NMSA 56-8-4)
  - A separate action for bad faith, seeking extracontractual damages
    including emotional distress, punitive damages, and attorney fees
```

### Section 5: Demand Terms

Same requirements as TIME-LIMITED-DEMAND terms (see mode-time-limited.md), with these additions:

- Expressly state that the demand is for POLICY LIMITS, not a specific dollar amount (so if limits are higher than believed, the demand covers the full limits)
- Request confirmation of all available coverage (primary, umbrella, excess)
- Preserve all rights against other parties and UIM carriers
- Specify that acceptance does not require any admission of liability by the insured

### Section 6: Enclosures (Comprehensive Package)

The enclosures must be comprehensive enough to remove any claim that the carrier lacked information:

```
Enclosed Supporting Documentation:

1. Complete medical records:
   [List every provider with date ranges]

2. Medical billing summary:
   [Total billed: $[AMOUNT]]
   [Itemized by provider]

3. Wage loss documentation:
   [Employer verification letter / tax returns / pay stubs]

4. Expert reports (if available):
   [Medical expert / economist / life care planner]

5. Photographs:
   [Injury photos, property damage, scene photos]

6. Police/incident report

7. [Additional documentation as applicable]

This package contains all information necessary for [CARRIER NAME]
to evaluate this claim and tender the policy limits. Any request for
additional information should be directed to this office immediately
and will not extend the deadline for acceptance of this demand.
```

## Part 4: Strategic Considerations

### 4.1 Timing the Policy Limits Demand

| Scenario | Timing Strategy |
|----------|----------------|
| Catastrophic injury, clear liability | Send early (even pre-suit) -- carrier should tender limits quickly |
| Strong case, moderate limits | Send after treatment completion and DC analysis |
| Disputed liability but strong facts | Build record through discovery first, then send during litigation |
| Multiple defendants | Coordinate timing across all defendants/carriers |
| UIM component | Send to liability carrier first; UIM demand follows if limits tendered |

### 4.2 After the Demand is Sent

| Carrier Response | Next Step |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Tenders limits | Accept, execute release, distribute to client. Pursue UIM if applicable. |
| Counter-offers below limits | Document the counter, reject, note in bad faith record |
| Requests more time | Consider reasonable extension (document in writing) |
| Requests more information | Provide if reasonable; note any delay tactics in bad faith record |
| Denies claim | Document denial, file suit, preserve bad faith claim |
| No response by deadline | Document non-response, file suit, pursue bad faith claim |
| Disputes coverage | Request coverage position in writing, involve coverage counsel if needed |

### 4.3 Preserving the Bad Faith Record

Document EVERYTHING after sending the demand:
- Certified mail receipt / delivery confirmation
- All carrier communications (log dates, times, substance)
- Any counter-offers with dates
- Requests for extensions with responses
- Any additional information provided
- Timeline of carrier response (or non-response)

## Part 5: Policy Limits Demand Quality Checklist

- [ ] Policy limits confirmed (not estimated)
- [ ] Coverage confirmation requested or included
- [ ] Liability presented as clear and undisputed (NMSA 59A-16-20(F) trigger)
- [ ] Damages shown as multiple of policy limits with excess exposure calculated
- [ ] Bad faith exposure analysis section included with NM statutory citations
- [ ] Demand terms are clear, specific, and unconditional
- [ ] Release scope defined (insured only, preserve rights against others)
- [ ] Deadline is reasonable (21-30 days minimum)
- [ ] Deadline flagged as `[DECISION REQUIRED]` for attorney
- [ ] Acceptance method specified
- [ ] Payment method specified (trust account)
- [ ] Enclosures are comprehensive (remove "insufficient information" defense)
- [ ] All assertions traced to CFP/DC/LPB in Cite Table
- [ ] NMSA 59A-16-20 provisions cited correctly
- [ ] Sloan v. State Farm and other NM bad faith cases cited
- [ ] Pre-judgment interest referenced (NMSA 56-8-4)
- [ ] No threats, only factual statements of legal consequences
- [ ] BODY-ONLY format maintained
- [ ] Delivery method note: certified mail, return receipt requested
- [ ] Attorney has approved demand before sending
