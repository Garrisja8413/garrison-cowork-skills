# DEMAND Mode: TIME-LIMITED-DEMAND Reference

## Purpose

The TIME-LIMITED-DEMAND mode produces a demand letter with a strict, enforceable deadline for acceptance. In New Mexico, this is the equivalent of a "Stowers demand" (Texas terminology) or "excess exposure" demand. The purpose is to create a limited window for the insurance carrier to settle within policy limits, after which the carrier faces potential bad faith exposure for any excess verdict.

This is the highest-stakes demand mode. Precision in language, timing, and statutory requirements is essential. A defective time-limited demand may not trigger bad faith consequences.

---

## Part 1: Legal Foundation in New Mexico

### 1.1 NM Bad Faith Law Overview

New Mexico recognizes a cause of action against an insurance carrier for bad faith failure to settle. The legal framework:

| Authority | Principle |
|-----------|-----------|
| Sloan v. State Farm, 2004-NMSC-004 | Insurer has duty of good faith in handling claims; breach can give rise to tort liability |
| Ambassador Ins. Co. v. St. Paul Fire & Marine Ins., 1984-NMSC-107 | Insurer who fails to settle within policy limits may be liable for excess judgment |
| Chavez v. Kennelly, 2002-NMCA-049 | Bad faith requires showing insurer acted unreasonably under the circumstances |
| NMSA 59A-16-20 | Unfair Claims Practices Act -- defines unfair claim settlement practices |
| NMSA 59A-16-20(E) | Failure to affirm or deny coverage within a reasonable time |
| NMSA 59A-16-20(F) | Not attempting good faith settlement when liability is reasonably clear |
| NMSA 59A-16-20(G) | Compelling litigation by offering substantially less than amounts recovered |
| UJI 13-1716 | Measure of damages for bad faith (may include consequential damages beyond policy limits) |

### 1.2 Stowers-Equivalent Doctrine in NM

While NM does not use the term "Stowers demand" (a Texas-specific doctrine from G.A. Stowers Furniture Co. v. American Indemnity Co., 15 S.W.2d 544 (Tex. Comm'n App. 1929)), the functional equivalent exists:

**NM Principle:** When a plaintiff makes a clear, unconditional offer to settle within policy limits and the insurer unreasonably refuses, the insurer may be liable for any excess judgment above the policy limits.

**Key elements for a valid time-limited demand under NM law:**
1. The demand must be within policy limits (or equal to policy limits)
2. The demand must be clear and unconditional
3. The terms must be reasonable and capable of acceptance
4. Adequate time must be given for the insurer to evaluate and respond
5. The plaintiff must be willing to execute a release upon payment
6. The demand must clearly communicate the consequences of non-acceptance

### 1.3 Unfair Claims Practices Act (NMSA 59A-16-20)

The time-limited demand should be structured to trigger specific UCPA violations if the carrier fails to respond:

| UCPA Provision | Triggered By |
|---------------|-------------|
| 59A-16-20(B) | Failing to acknowledge communications regarding claims promptly |
| 59A-16-20(D) | Refusing to pay claims without conducting reasonable investigation |
| 59A-16-20(E) | Failing to affirm or deny coverage within a reasonable time |
| 59A-16-20(F) | Not attempting in good faith to effectuate prompt, fair settlement when liability is reasonably clear |
| 59A-16-20(G) | Compelling insured/claimant to institute litigation by offering substantially less than amount ultimately recovered |
| 59A-16-20(N) | Failing to provide a reasonable explanation for denial of a claim |

## Part 2: Time-Limited Demand Structure

### Section 1: Introduction (Heightened Formality)

**Template pattern:**
```
THIS IS A TIME-LIMITED DEMAND FOR SETTLEMENT WITHIN POLICY LIMITS.
FAILURE TO ACCEPT THIS DEMAND WITHIN THE TIME SPECIFIED MAY RESULT
IN EXPOSURE TO YOUR INSURED IN EXCESS OF POLICY LIMITS AND MAY
CONSTITUTE BAD FAITH UNDER NEW MEXICO LAW.

This firm represents [CLIENT NAME] regarding injuries sustained on
[INCIDENT DATE] in an incident involving your insured, [INSURED NAME].
This demand is made under Claim Number [CLAIM NUMBER] / Policy Number
[POLICY NUMBER].
```

**Required elements:**
- Prominent notice that this is a time-limited demand
- Clear statement of bad faith consequences
- Identification of all parties, claim number, and policy number
- If policy limits are known, state them; if unknown, request disclosure

### Section 2: Liability Summary

Same as PRE-SUIT or LITIGATION mode, but with emphasis on the clarity of liability:

**Key addition:** The liability section must establish that "liability is reasonably clear" to trigger NMSA 59A-16-20(F). Use language such as:

```
The evidence establishes that liability in this matter is clear and
undisputed [or: overwhelmingly established]. [SPECIFIC LIABILITY FACTS].
Under these circumstances, [CARRIER NAME]'s duty to effectuate a prompt,
fair settlement is triggered under NMSA 59A-16-20(F).
```

### Section 3: Injury and Treatment Narrative

Same as PRE-SUIT or LITIGATION mode. Comprehensive and fully supported by CFP.

### Section 4: Damages Exceeding Policy Limits

**Critical section for time-limited demands.** The demand must demonstrate that the claim value EXCEEDS policy limits, creating excess exposure for the insured:

```
The total damages in this case, as detailed above, range from $[DC LOW]
to $[DC HIGH]. Even using the most conservative assessment, [CLIENT NAME]'s
damages substantially exceed the $[POLICY LIMITS] policy limits available
under the subject policy.

Given the clear excess exposure, any failure to tender the policy limits
will expose [INSURED NAME] to personal liability for the difference
between the policy limits and the ultimate judgment or verdict.
```

**Required proof points:**
1. Total specials (medical + lost wages) -- specific dollar amounts
2. General damages range from DC -- supported by comparable verdicts if DVP available
3. Clear statement that total damages exceed policy limits
4. Express statement of excess exposure risk to the insured

### Section 5: Demand Terms (Precise and Unconditional)

This is the most legally significant section. Every element must be present:

**Required elements:**

```
DEMAND TERMS:

1. AMOUNT: [CLIENT NAME] demands payment of the full available policy
   limits of $[POLICY LIMITS] under [POLICY TYPE] policy number
   [POLICY NUMBER].

2. DEADLINE: This demand must be accepted in writing on or before
   [SPECIFIC DATE AND TIME], which is [NUMBER] days from the date
   of this letter. Time is of the essence.

3. RELEASE: Upon timely tender of the policy limits, [CLIENT NAME]
   will execute a full release of [INSURED NAME] only. [CLIENT NAME]
   reserves all rights against [OTHER DEFENDANTS / UNDERINSURED
   MOTORIST CARRIER / OTHER PARTIES] and does not release any claims
   against any party other than [INSURED NAME].

4. PAYMENT: Payment shall be made by [CASHIER'S CHECK / WIRE TRANSFER]
   payable to "[CLIENT NAME] and [LAW FIRM NAME], Attorneys Trust
   Account."

5. CONDITIONS: This offer is open and unconditional. No additional
   conditions, releases, or terms beyond those stated herein are
   required for acceptance.

6. ACCEPTANCE: Acceptance shall be communicated in writing to
   [ATTORNEY NAME] at [ADDRESS / EMAIL] on or before the deadline
   stated above.
```

### Section 6: Consequences of Non-Acceptance

**Template pattern:**
```
CONSEQUENCES OF NON-ACCEPTANCE:

If this demand is not accepted within the time specified above:

1. This offer is withdrawn and [CLIENT NAME] will proceed to trial
   seeking the full measure of damages, which substantially exceed
   the available policy limits.

2. [CLIENT NAME] reserves the right to assert a claim for bad faith
   against [CARRIER NAME] for failure to settle within policy limits
   when liability is reasonably clear and damages clearly exceed
   the policy limits, in violation of NMSA 59A-16-20 and the common
   law duty of good faith. See Sloan v. State Farm, 2004-NMSC-004.

3. [INSURED NAME] will be personally exposed to any judgment in excess
   of the policy limits. [CLIENT NAME] will seek to recover any excess
   judgment directly from [INSURED NAME] and/or through assignment of
   [INSURED NAME]'s bad faith claims against [CARRIER NAME].

4. [CLIENT NAME] will seek pre-judgment interest on the full judgment
   amount pursuant to NMSA 56-8-4 at the rate of 15% per annum from
   the date of this demand.
```

## Part 3: Deadline Selection

### 3.1 Reasonable Deadline Determination

The deadline must be "reasonable" to be enforceable. An unreasonably short deadline may undermine the bad faith argument.

| Factor | Guidance |
|--------|----------|
| Pre-suit demand | 30 days is standard and clearly reasonable |
| During litigation | 21-30 days is reasonable |
| Complex coverage issues | 30-45 days to allow coverage evaluation |
| Simple claim, clear liability | 14-21 days may be reasonable if claim is well-documented |
| Multiple carriers | Allow additional time for inter-carrier coordination |
| Approaching trial | 14 days may be reasonable given urgency |

**IMPORTANT:** The deadline is ALWAYS flagged as `[DECISION REQUIRED]` for attorney approval. The attorney selects the specific deadline period. The skill does not choose the deadline.

### 3.2 Deadline Language Requirements

- State a specific calendar date AND time (e.g., "5:00 PM MST on March 15, 2026")
- State the number of days from the demand letter date
- Use "time is of the essence" language
- Specify how acceptance must be communicated (in writing, to specific address/email)
- Specify that the offer is automatically withdrawn if not accepted by the deadline

## Part 4: Common Defects That Undermine Time-Limited Demands

### 4.1 Defects to Avoid

| Defect | Why It Fails | Correction |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Conditional demand | Carrier can argue offer was not unconditional | Remove all conditions except payment and release |
| Ambiguous release scope | Carrier claims uncertainty about who is released | Specify exactly who is and is not released |
| Unreasonable deadline | Carrier argues insufficient time to evaluate | Use 21-30 days minimum |
| No specific amount | Carrier claims demand was not definite | State exact dollar amount |
| Missing policy reference | Carrier claims confusion about which policy | Include policy number and limits |
| No acceptance mechanism | Carrier claims they didn't know how to accept | Specify written acceptance to specific person/address |
| Demanding more than limits | Not a policy limits demand if amount exceeds limits | Demand exactly the policy limits amount |
| Release of all claims | May inadvertently release UIM carrier or other defendants | Preserve all rights against non-settling parties |
| Threatening language | May undermine professional standing | Firm, factual, but not threatening |
| Invented consequences | Stating legal consequences not supported by NM law | Only cite actual NM bad faith law |

### 4.2 Pre-Delivery Checklist

- [ ] Policy limits amount is confirmed (not estimated)
- [ ] Demand amount equals policy limits exactly
- [ ] Deadline is a specific date and time
- [ ] Deadline period is reasonable (21-30 days minimum)
- [ ] Demand is unconditional (no hidden conditions)
- [ ] Release scope is clearly defined (who is released, who is not)
- [ ] Payment method specified (check payable to trust account)
- [ ] Acceptance method specified (written, to specific person/address)
- [ ] Consequences of non-acceptance stated clearly
- [ ] Bad faith statutes cited correctly (NMSA 59A-16-20, Sloan v. State Farm)
- [ ] Damages clearly shown to exceed policy limits
- [ ] Liability characterized as "reasonably clear"
- [ ] Pre-judgment interest reference included (NMSA 56-8-4)
- [ ] Attorney has reviewed and approved deadline period `[DECISION REQUIRED]` resolved
- [ ] BODY-ONLY format (no letterhead, date, address, signature)
- [ ] All assertions traced to CFP/DC/LPB in Cite Table
- [ ] Sent via certified mail with return receipt requested (delivery method note in Next Steps)

## Part 5: Edge Cases

### 5.1 Unknown Policy Limits

If policy limits are not confirmed:
- Send a demand requesting disclosure of policy limits first
- Reference NMRA 1-026(B)(2) (insurance agreements discoverable)
- Once limits are confirmed, send the time-limited demand
- Do NOT send a time-limited demand without confirmed limits

### 5.2 Multiple Policies / Stacked Coverage

- Address each policy separately if multiple policies apply
- Demand limits of each applicable policy
- Specify which policy is being demanded
- Consider whether umbrella/excess coverage exists
- If stacking applies (NM allows stacking of UM/UIM under certain circumstances), address stacked limits

### 5.3 Government Defendants

- Government entities may have sovereign immunity caps (NMSA 41-4-19)
- TCA caps may be LOWER than policy limits -- demand the applicable cap or limits, whichever is lower
- TCA notice must have been provided (NMSA 41-4-16(A))
- Government risk management may have different settlement authority processes

### 5.4 Expiring Statute of Limitations

If SOL is approaching:
- Time-limited demand should be sent BEFORE filing suit if possible
- If filing suit during the demand period, note in the demand that filing is to preserve the claim and does not withdraw the offer
- Filing suit does not invalidate the time-limited demand
