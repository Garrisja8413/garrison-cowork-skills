# Interrogatory Objection Validity Standards — OA-ROG Reference

**Version:** 1.0
**Applies to:** OA-ROG (Objection Analyzer — Interrogatories)

## Governing Rules

### Federal
- **FRCP 33(a)(1):** 25-interrogatory limit without leave or stipulation
- **FRCP 33(a)(2):** Interrogatories may relate to any matter under FRCP 26(b)
- **FRCP 33(b)(1)(A):** Each interrogatory must be answered separately and fully under oath
- **FRCP 33(b)(3):** Grounds for objection must be stated with specificity
- **FRCP 33(b)(4):** Answering part of an interrogatory is not waiver of objection to remainder
- **FRCP 33(c):** Answers may be used at trial to the extent allowed by the rules of evidence
- **FRCP 33(d):** Business records option — production in lieu of answer
- **FRCP 37(a)(4):** Evasive or incomplete answer treated as failure to answer

### New Mexico
- **NMRA 1-033:** Interrogatories (tracks federal rule with NM modifications)
- **NMRA 1-033(A):** Interrogatory limit may differ from federal 25-cap; check local rules
- **NMRA 1-033(B):** Response within 30 days of service

## Subpart Counting Rules

### The "Discrete Subpart" Test
A subpart counts as a separate interrogatory if:
1. It is logically independent — answering one does not necessarily answer the other
2. It requires investigation into a separate subject
3. It could stand alone as a complete interrogatory

A subpart does NOT count separately if:
- It is a natural follow-up to the main question (e.g., "If yes, explain...")
- It defines or clarifies the scope of the main question
- It seeks related details about the same core topic

### Common Subpart Patterns
| Pattern | Counts as Separate? | Reasoning |
|---------|---------------------|-----------|
| "Identify each document and state its author" | NO | Related details about same document |
| "State all facts and identify all witnesses" | YES | Separate investigations |
| "If yes, explain when, where, and how" | NO | Conditional follow-up to single question |
| "(a) List all medications, (b) List all providers, (c) List all diagnoses" | YES | Each requires separate investigation |

## Contention Interrogatory Standards

Contention interrogatories are permitted under FRCP 33(a)(2) but:
- May be deferred until close of discovery if served early
- Must relate to legal contentions or factual bases for claims/defenses
- Court has discretion to time and sequence contention interrogatories
- Answering does not waive privilege over mental impressions or legal theories

## Business Records Option (FRCP 33(d))

The responding party may produce business records instead of answering if:
1. The burden of deriving the answer is substantially the same for both parties
2. The records are specified with reasonable particularity
3. The records are made available for inspection

This option cannot be used to:
- Dump unorganized records and force the requesting party to find the answer
- Avoid answering when the responding party can easily derive the answer from its own records

## Evasive/Incomplete Answer Doctrine

Under FRCP 37(a)(4), an answer is treated as a failure to answer if:
- It addresses only part of the interrogatory without objecting to the remainder
- It gives a vague or general response when specific information was requested
- It purports to answer but actually evades the question asked
- It fails to provide information within the responding party's knowledge or control
