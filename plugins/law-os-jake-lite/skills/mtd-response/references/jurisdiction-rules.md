# Jurisdiction Rules — MTD Response Reference

## New Mexico (State Court — NMRA 1-012(B))

### Dismissal Standard — 1-012(B)(6)

- **Rule:** NMRA 1-012(B)(6) — failure to state a claim upon which relief
  can be granted
- **Standard:** Notice pleading — plaintiff must state a claim that falls
  within a recognized legal theory. The question is whether the plaintiff
  may establish a set of facts that would entitle the plaintiff to relief.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Key distinction from federal:** NM has not adopted *Twombly*/*Iqbal*
  plausibility standard. NM uses the more liberal "set of facts" standard
  derived from *Conley v. Gibson*. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Complaint allegations:** Accepted as true for purposes of the motion.
  All reasonable inferences drawn in plaintiff's favor. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Dismissal disfavored:** Motion to dismiss is a drastic remedy. Doubt
  should be resolved in favor of the complaint. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Pleading Requirements — NMRA 1-008(A)

- **Short and plain statement:** Complaint must contain "a short and plain
  statement of the claim showing that the pleader is entitled to relief."
  NMRA 1-008(A). [VERIFY-CITE]
- **No heightened pleading** (except fraud/mistake under NMRA 1-009(B)):
  NM does not require fact pleading for most claims. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Notice function:** The complaint's purpose is to give the opposing party
  fair notice of the claims and the grounds upon which they rest. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Burden Allocation

- Defendant (movant) bears the burden of establishing that the complaint
  fails to state a claim. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Where complaint can be read to support any legal theory, dismissal is
  not warranted. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Leave to Amend — NMRA 1-015(A)

- **Rule:** NMRA 1-015(A) — leave to amend "shall be freely given when
  justice so requires"
- Amendment should be denied only for: undue delay, bad faith, undue
  prejudice, or futility. [VERIFY-CITE]
- First opportunity to amend should generally be granted before dismissal
  with prejudice. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Conversion to Summary Judgment

- **Rule:** NMRA 1-012(B) — if matters outside the pleadings are presented
  to and not excluded by the court, the motion shall be treated as one for
  summary judgment under NMRA 1-056. [VERIFY-CITE]
- All parties must be given reasonable opportunity to present material
  pertinent to a summary judgment motion. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Specific Grounds — NM State Court

#### 1-012(B)(1) — Lack of Subject Matter Jurisdiction
- District courts have general jurisdiction over civil matters.
  NMSA 1978, § 38-3-1.1. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Governmental immunity under NMTCA (NMSA 1978, §§ 41-4-1 to -30)
  may raise jurisdictional questions in tort claims against government
  entities. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Plaintiff bears the burden to establish subject matter jurisdiction.
  [VERIFY-CITE]

#### 1-012(B)(2) — Lack of Personal Jurisdiction
- NM long-arm statute: NMSA 1978, § 38-1-16 — extends jurisdiction to
  the full extent permitted by due process. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Analysis collapses into due process inquiry: minimum contacts +
  fair play and substantial justice. [VERIFY-CITE]
- At motion-to-dismiss stage, plaintiff need only make a prima facie
  showing of jurisdiction. [VERIFY-CITE]

#### 1-012(B)(3) — Improper Venue
- NM venue rules: NMRA 1-010.1 — county where claim arose, where
  defendant resides, or where defendant can be found. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Improper venue is waivable if not raised in first responsive pleading
  or by motion before responsive pleading. [VERIFY-CITE]

#### 1-012(B)(4)/(5) — Process and Service Defects
- Service rules: NMRA 1-004 — service of summons and complaint.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- Insufficient service is curable; courts prefer to grant extensions
  or allow re-service rather than dismiss. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Dismissal for service defects is without prejudice. [VERIFY-CITE]

#### 1-012(B)(7) — Failure to Join Required Party
- Required party analysis: NMRA 1-019 — joinder of persons needed
  for just adjudication. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Court must determine: (a) whether party is required, (b) whether
  joinder is feasible, (c) if not feasible, whether action should
  proceed in equity and good conscience. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Response Deadlines — NM State Court

- Response deadline: controlled by NMRA 1-006 and scheduling order.
  Typically 15 days from service of motion unless otherwise ordered.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- Extensions: request in writing before deadline expires. Good cause
  standard. [VERIFY-CITE]

## Federal — D.N.M. (FRCP 12(b) + Local Rules)

### Dismissal Standard — FRCP 12(b)(6)

- **Rule:** FRCP 12(b)(6) — failure to state a claim upon which relief
  can be granted
- **Standard:** *Twombly*/*Iqbal* plausibility framework:
  - Step 1: Identify the well-pleaded factual allegations (excluding
    legal conclusions and "threadbare recitals"). [VERIFY-CITE]
  - Step 2: Determine whether the remaining factual allegations, taken
    as true, "plausibly" state a claim for relief. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Plausibility, not probability:** "Asking for plausible grounds . . .
  does not impose a probability requirement." *Twombly*, 550 U.S. at 556.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Factual content test:** "A claim has facial plausibility when the
  plaintiff pleads factual content that allows the court to draw the
  reasonable inference that the defendant is liable for the misconduct
  alleged." *Iqbal*, 556 U.S. at 678. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Pleading Requirements — FRCP 8(a)

- **FRCP 8(a)(2):** "A short and plain statement of the claim showing
  that the pleader is entitled to relief"
- **No heightened pleading** (except FRCP 9(b) — fraud/mistake):
  Federal courts follow notice pleading as refined by *Twombly*/*Iqbal*.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Well-pleaded complaint rule:** Court considers only the complaint,
  documents attached to the complaint, documents incorporated by
  reference, and matters of which the court may take judicial notice.
  [VERIFY-CITE]

### Burden Allocation

- Movant (defendant) bears the burden of establishing that plaintiff's
  complaint fails to state a claim. [VERIFY-CITE]
- 10th Circuit: "dismissal under Rule 12(b)(6) is a harsh remedy which
  must be cautiously studied, not only to effectuate the spirit of the
  liberal rules of pleading but also to protect the interests of justice."
  [VERIFY-CITE]

### Leave to Amend — FRCP 15(a)

- **Rule:** FRCP 15(a)(2) — court should freely give leave when justice
  so requires
- **10th Circuit standard:** Refusal is justified only upon a showing of
  "undue delay, undue prejudice to the opposing party, bad faith or
  dilatory motive, failure to cure deficiencies by amendments previously
  allowed, or futility of amendment." [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Dismissal without leave:** 10th Circuit disfavors dismissal with
  prejudice without granting leave to amend, particularly for the
  first complaint. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Futility standard: proposed amendment would not survive a motion to
  dismiss. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Conversion to Summary Judgment

- **Rule:** FRCP 12(d) — if matters outside the pleadings are presented
  and not excluded by the court, the motion must be treated as one for
  summary judgment under Rule 56. All parties must be given reasonable
  opportunity to present pertinent material. [VERIFY-CITE]
- The court must provide notice before converting. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Plaintiff may argue: exclude the extrinsic materials, or request
  discovery before conversion. [VERIFY-CITE]

### Specific Grounds — D.N.M. Federal Court

#### FRCP 12(b)(1) — Lack of Subject Matter Jurisdiction
- **Facial attack:** Accepts complaint allegations as true. Plaintiff
  must show that the allegations, if true, establish jurisdiction.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Factual attack:** Court may consider extrinsic evidence. No
  presumption of truthfulness attaches to complaint allegations.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Plaintiff's burden:** Plaintiff bears the burden of establishing
  subject matter jurisdiction. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Common bases: diversity (28 U.S.C. § 1332), federal question
  (28 U.S.C. § 1331), supplemental jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1367).

#### FRCP 12(b)(2) — Lack of Personal Jurisdiction
- Two-step inquiry: (1) state long-arm statute, (2) constitutional
  due process (minimum contacts). [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Plaintiff's prima facie burden:** At the motion-to-dismiss stage,
  the plaintiff need only make a prima facie showing of jurisdiction.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **Uncontroverted allegations:** The court must accept uncontroverted
  allegations in the complaint and resolve factual disputes in plaintiff's
  favor. [VERIFY-CITE]
- NM long-arm reaches to constitutional limits: NMSA 1978, § 38-1-16.
  [VERIFY-CITE]

#### FRCP 12(b)(3) — Improper Venue
- Venue statutes: 28 U.S.C. § 1391 — district where any defendant
  resides (if all in same state), where substantial part of events
  occurred, or fallback. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Court may transfer rather than dismiss: 28 U.S.C. § 1406(a).
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- Plaintiff's burden to establish venue is proper. [VERIFY-CITE]

#### FRCP 12(b)(4)/(5) — Process and Service Defects
- Service rules: FRCP 4 — service of summons and complaint.
- Courts prefer to quash service and allow re-service rather than
  dismiss. [VERIFY-CITE]
- If service defect is curable and plaintiff acts promptly, dismissal
  is inappropriate. [VERIFY-CITE]
- FRCP 4(m) — 90-day deadline for service (with good cause extension).
  [VERIFY-CITE]

#### FRCP 12(b)(7) — Failure to Join Required Party
- Required party analysis: FRCP 19 — joinder of required parties.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- Three-part test: (a) is the absent party required, (b) is joinder
  feasible, (c) if not feasible, should the action proceed in equity
  and good conscience. [VERIFY-CITE]

### D.N.M. Local Rules

#### Page Limits
- **D.N.M. LR-Civ 7.5:** Response brief limited to 24 pages.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- Excess pages require leave of court.

#### Good Faith Certification
- **D.N.M. LR-Civ 7.1(a):** Good faith conference requirement applies
  to "non-dispositive" motions. Motions to dismiss are generally
  dispositive and may be exempt, but check judge-specific requirements.
  [VERIFY-CITE]

#### Filing Requirements
- D.N.M. LR-Civ 10.1 — electronic filing requirements.
- D.N.M. LR-Civ 10.6 — format requirements (font, margins, etc.).

### Response Deadlines — D.N.M.

- Response deadline: 14 days after service of the motion (FRCP 6(d)
  adds 3 days for service by mail/electronic means). D.N.M. LR-Civ 7.4(a).
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- Extensions: D.N.M. LR-Civ 7.4(b) — stipulated extensions filed before
  deadline. Otherwise, motion required. [VERIFY-CITE]

## Jurisdiction Detection

From the Complaint or MTD filing:
- **NM state court** → NMRA 1-012(B) + NM notice pleading standard
- **D.N.M. federal court** → FRCP 12(b) + *Twombly*/*Iqbal* plausibility
- **Other federal district** → FRCP 12(b) + circuit-specific precedent

If jurisdiction is unclear: flag as `[DECISION REQUIRED]` — "Confirm
whether this case is in NM state court or federal court. The applicable
pleading standard differs significantly."

## NM vs. Federal — Key Differences Summary

| Issue | NM State Court | D.N.M. Federal Court |
|-------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Pleading standard** | Notice pleading — "set of facts" | *Twombly*/*Iqbal* plausibility |
| **Dismissal burden** | Defendant bears burden | Defendant bears burden |
| **Complaint construction** | Liberally construed | Liberally construed (but legal conclusions excluded) |
| **Leave to amend** | Freely given (NMRA 1-015(A)) | Freely given (FRCP 15(a)(2)) |
| **Conversion rule** | NMRA 1-012(B) → 1-056 | FRCP 12(d) → Rule 56 |
| **Response page limit** | Per local rule / scheduling order | 24 pages (LR-Civ 7.5) |
| **Response deadline** | ~15 days (per NMRA 1-006 / order) | 14 days + 3 if mailed (FRCP 6) |
| **Heightened pleading** | Fraud/mistake only (NMRA 1-009(B)) | Fraud/mistake only (FRCP 9(b)) |
