# MTD Response Structure — Reference

## BODY-ONLY Template

The SA shell provides: caption, case number, court, COS, signature block.
This skill drafts only the opposition body (Sections I–VI).

---

### I. INTRODUCTION

**Template:**
```
[MERGE:CaseName] respectfully opposes Defendant [Defendant name]'s
Motion to Dismiss and requests that this Court deny the motion in its
entirety.

[If 12(b)(6) / 1-012(B)(6):]
Defendant moves to dismiss [Plaintiff]'s [Complaint / Amended Complaint]
under [NMRA 1-012(B)(6) / Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6)], arguing that
[Plaintiff] has failed to state a claim upon which relief can be granted.
Defendant is wrong.

[Summary — 2-3 sentences previewing argument:]
[Plaintiff]'s Complaint [identifies the key factual allegations that
state viable claims]. Accepting these allegations as true, as this Court
must at this stage, [Plaintiff] has [plausibly stated / stated facts
sufficient to support] claims for [list claims: e.g., negligence,
premises liability, NMSA § xx-xx-x violation].

[If multiple grounds:]
Defendant raises [count] grounds for dismissal: [list grounds].
Each fails for the reasons set forth below.

[If 12(b)(1):]
Defendant challenges this Court's subject matter jurisdiction. [Brief
statement of jurisdictional basis — cite statute or constitutional
provision.]

[If 12(b)(2):]
Defendant challenges personal jurisdiction. [Brief statement of
defendant's contacts with the forum state.]
```

**Traceability:** Claim identification from Complaint. Legal standard
from LPB. Defendant's arguments from MTD.

### II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

**Template:**
```
The following facts are drawn from [Plaintiff]'s [Complaint / Amended
Complaint] and must be accepted as true for purposes of this motion.
[Cite standard from LPB — e.g., "On a motion to dismiss, the Court
accepts all well-pleaded factual allegations as true and draws all
reasonable inferences in the plaintiff's favor." LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

[Organize facts by topic/chronology to build narrative supporting
claim viability. Each fact cites a Complaint ¶.]

On [date], [description of events]. (Compl. ¶ [X].)

[Plaintiff] [description of relationship, duty, conduct]. (Compl.
¶¶ [Y]–[Z].)

As a result of [Defendant]'s [conduct], [Plaintiff] suffered [injuries/
damages described]. (Compl. ¶¶ [A]–[B].)

[If CFP PACKET available, supplement with record cites:]
[Additional factual support from CFP — DocID + Bates/Page:Line.]
```

**Traceability:** All facts from Complaint ¶ numbers. Supplemental facts
from CFP rows with Evidence_DocID + Bates. No facts from outside the
record. No argument in this section.

**Key discipline:** Do NOT include facts not in the Complaint unless they
are from the CFP packet and are judicially noticeable or referenced in
the MTD. On 12(b)(6), the universe is generally limited to the four
corners of the complaint (plus documents incorporated by reference or
subject to judicial notice).

### III. LEGAL STANDARD

**Template for 12(b)(6) / 1-012(B)(6):**
```
[Build from LPB authority. Use the following structure:]

A. Standard of Review

A motion to dismiss under [NMRA 1-012(B)(6) / Rule 12(b)(6)] tests the
legal sufficiency of the complaint. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

[If NM:]
Under New Mexico's notice pleading standard, a complaint need only
contain "a short and plain statement of the claim showing that the
pleader is entitled to relief." NMRA 1-008(A). [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]
The question is whether the plaintiff may establish a set of facts
in support of the claims that would entitle the plaintiff to relief.
[LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

All well-pleaded factual allegations in the complaint are accepted as
true and viewed in the light most favorable to the plaintiff. [LPB
LawTag + Pinpoint.]

Dismissal is appropriate only when it appears that the plaintiff can
prove no set of facts in support of the claims that would entitle the
plaintiff to relief. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

[If FED:]
Under the federal standard, a complaint must contain "enough facts to
state a claim to relief that is plausible on its face." [LPB LawTag +
Pinpoint — Bell Atl. Corp. v. Twombly, 550 U.S. 544, 570 (2007).]

A claim is facially plausible "when the plaintiff pleads factual content
that allows the court to draw the reasonable inference that the defendant
is liable for the misconduct alleged." [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint —
Ashcroft v. Iqbal, 556 U.S. 662, 678 (2009).]

The Court accepts all well-pleaded factual allegations as true and draws
all reasonable inferences in the plaintiff's favor. [LPB LawTag +
Pinpoint.]

Plausibility requires "more than a sheer possibility that a defendant
has acted unlawfully," but does not require probability. [LPB LawTag +
Pinpoint.]

B. Defendant Bears the Burden

The defendant bears the burden of demonstrating that the plaintiff's
complaint fails to state a claim. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.] Dismissal
is a drastic remedy that is disfavored. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

C. Leave to Amend

[If NM:] Even if dismissal were warranted, leave to amend should be
freely given. NMRA 1-015(A). [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

[If FED:] Even if the Court identifies a deficiency, leave to amend
should be "freely give[n] when justice so requires." Fed. R. Civ. P.
15(a)(2). [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]
```

**Template for 12(b)(1) / 1-012(B)(1) — Subject Matter Jurisdiction:**
```
A motion to dismiss for lack of subject matter jurisdiction under
[NMRA 1-012(B)(1) / Rule 12(b)(1)] may present either a facial or
factual challenge. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

A facial challenge accepts the complaint's allegations as true and
tests whether they establish jurisdiction. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

A factual challenge goes beyond the complaint, and the court may
consider extrinsic evidence. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

The plaintiff bears the burden of establishing subject matter
jurisdiction. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]
```

**Template for 12(b)(2) / 1-012(B)(2) — Personal Jurisdiction:**
```
[See MSJ Specific Personal Jurisdiction skill for detailed
jurisdictional analysis framework. Apply the same element-by-element
approach in opposition posture.]

The plaintiff bears the initial burden of making a prima facie showing
of personal jurisdiction. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

At the motion-to-dismiss stage, the plaintiff's burden is light — the
plaintiff need only make a prima facie showing by demonstrating, via
affidavit or other written materials, facts that would support
jurisdiction. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

[Remainder follows Long-Arm + Due Process framework from LPB.]
```

**Note:** Every sentence in the Legal Standard section must cite an LPB
LawTag + Pinpoint. If a rule statement lacks LPB backing, mark
`[VERIFY-CITE]`.

### IV. ARGUMENT — CLAIM-BY-CLAIM RESPONSE

**This is the core of the opposition. Each challenged claim gets its
own lettered subsection.**

**Template per claim — 12(b)(6) / 1-012(B)(6):**
```
[Letter]. [Plaintiff]'s [Claim Name] Claim States a Viable Cause of Action.

[State elements of the claim — cite LPB authority + Pinpoint:]
To state a claim for [claim type], a plaintiff must allege:
(1) [element 1], [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint];
(2) [element 2], [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint];
(3) [element 3], [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint]; and
(4) [element 4], [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint].

[If defendant challenges specific elements, address each challenged
element. If defendant makes a general insufficiency argument, walk
through all elements.]

1. [Element Name]

Defendant contends that [paraphrase defendant's argument — cite MTD
page/section]. (MTD at [page/¶].)

[Response — choose the applicable pattern:]

[If well-pleaded facts satisfy element:]
The Complaint alleges that [quote or paraphrase relevant allegations].
(Compl. ¶¶ [X]–[Y].) These allegations, accepted as true, [plausibly
establish / are sufficient to establish] [element] because [explain
how the facts map to the legal standard — cite LPB authority].

[If defendant mischaracterizes the complaint:]
Defendant misreads the Complaint. [Plaintiff] does not allege [what
defendant claims]. Rather, [Plaintiff] alleges [actual allegation with
Complaint ¶ cite]. This allegation satisfies [element] under [LPB
authority + Pinpoint].

[If defendant applies wrong standard:]
Defendant applies the wrong legal standard. [Defendant cites/argues X],
but the correct standard is [Y]. [LPB authority + Pinpoint.] Under the
correct standard, [Plaintiff]'s allegations are sufficient because
[analysis].

[If defendant argues facts must be proven:]
Defendant improperly asks this Court to weigh evidence at the pleading
stage. Whether [factual dispute] is a question of fact inappropriate
for resolution on a motion to dismiss. [LPB authority + Pinpoint.]
At this stage, [Plaintiff]'s allegations must be accepted as true.
[LPB authority + Pinpoint.]

[If defendant raises affirmative defense as dismissal ground:]
Defendant's argument is actually an affirmative defense [identify:
statute of limitations, immunity, etc.] that is not properly raised on
a motion to dismiss unless it appears on the face of the complaint.
[LPB authority + Pinpoint.] Because [the defense does not appear on the
face of the complaint / the complaint's allegations do not conclusively
establish the defense], dismissal on this ground is premature.

[Repeat for each challenged element.]

[Conclusion for this claim:]
The Complaint's allegations, taken as true and viewed in [Plaintiff]'s
favor, [plausibly state / state facts sufficient to support] a claim
for [claim type]. Defendant's motion to dismiss this claim should be
denied.
```

**Template per claim — 12(b)(1) — Subject Matter Jurisdiction:**
```
[Letter]. This Court Has Subject Matter Jurisdiction.

[Identify statutory/constitutional basis for jurisdiction:]
This Court has [general / limited] jurisdiction over this matter
pursuant to [statutory basis — e.g., NMSA 1978, § 38-3-1.1 (district
court jurisdiction); 28 U.S.C. § 1332 (diversity); 28 U.S.C. § 1331
(federal question)]. [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

[Address defendant's specific challenge:]
Defendant argues that [paraphrase — cite MTD page/¶]. (MTD at [page/¶].)

This argument fails because [analysis — cite LPB authority and
Complaint ¶s / jurisdictional facts].

[If diversity jurisdiction:]
Complete diversity exists because [Plaintiff] is a citizen of [state]
and [Defendant] is a citizen of [state]. (Compl. ¶¶ [X]–[Y].) The
amount in controversy exceeds $75,000 because [basis]. (Compl. ¶ [Z].)

[If factual challenge — may go beyond pleadings:]
[Address extrinsic evidence if applicable. Cite CFP PACKET rows.]
```

**Template per claim — 12(b)(2) — Personal Jurisdiction:**
```
[Letter]. This Court Has Personal Jurisdiction Over [Defendant].

[Follow Long-Arm + Due Process framework. Address:]
A. Long-Arm Statute
B. Constitutional Due Process
   1. Purposeful Direction/Availment
   2. Arising From / Relatedness
   3. Reasonableness (if applicable)

[For each element, cite Complaint ¶s and/or CFP rows showing
defendant's contacts with the forum state.]
```

### V. LEAVE TO AMEND (Alternative Relief)

**Template:**
```
[Include this section as alternative relief in every MTD response.]

Should this Court find any deficiency in the Complaint, [Plaintiff]
respectfully requests leave to amend.

[If NM:]
Under NMRA 1-015(A), leave to amend "shall be freely given when
justice so requires." [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.] Amendment should be
permitted "unless there are good reasons to the contrary, such as
undue delay, bad faith, undue prejudice to the opposing party, or
futility of the amendment." [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.]

[If FED:]
Under Fed. R. Civ. P. 15(a)(2), leave to amend should be "freely
give[n] when justice so requires." [LPB LawTag + Pinpoint.] The
Tenth Circuit has held that "[r]efusal of leave to amend is generally
only justified upon a showing of undue delay, undue prejudice to the
opposing party, bad faith or dilatory motive, failure to cure deficiencies
by amendments previously allowed, or futility of amendment." [LPB LawTag
+ Pinpoint.]

None of these factors is present here. [Address each briefly if
defendant has argued futility or delay.]

[If specific curable deficiencies identified:]
[Plaintiff] can cure any identified deficiency by [describe specific
additional facts or legal theories that could be pleaded]. This
demonstrates that amendment would not be futile.

[If this is the first complaint:]
This is [Plaintiff]'s [first / second] complaint. No prior amendment
has been allowed or denied. [Plaintiff] should be given at least one
opportunity to amend before dismissal with prejudice. [LPB LawTag +
Pinpoint.]
```

### VI. CONCLUSION

**Template:**
```
WHEREFORE, [MERGE:CaseName] respectfully requests that this Court:

1. Deny [Defendant]'s Motion to Dismiss in its entirety;

2. In the alternative, if the Court finds any claim deficient, grant
   [Plaintiff] leave to amend the Complaint within [N] days; and

3. Grant such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.
```

## Exhibit and Record Strategy

The opposition should reference record materials:

| Reference Type | Format | Source |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Complaint allegations | (Compl. ¶ [X]) | Uploaded complaint |
| Defendant's MTD arguments | (MTD at [page/section]) | Uploaded MTD |
| Case facts (if CFP available) | (CFP DocID + Bates/Page:Line) | CFP PACKET |
| Declarations/affidavits (12(b)(1)/(2)) | (Decl. of [Name] ¶ [X]) | Uploaded exhibit |

For 12(b)(6) motions, the response generally relies only on the complaint,
documents incorporated by reference, and matters subject to judicial notice.
Flag any reliance on extrinsic materials: `[DECISION REQUIRED] — Consider
whether reliance on [document] outside the complaint may trigger conversion
to MSJ under NMRA 1-012(B) / FRCP 12(d).`

## Response to Common Defendant Arguments

### "Conclusory Allegations"
```
Defendant labels [Plaintiff]'s allegations as "conclusory," but this
characterization is incorrect. [Plaintiff] alleges specific facts:
[list factual allegations with Complaint ¶ cites]. These are factual
allegations, not legal conclusions, and must be accepted as true.
[LPB authority + Pinpoint.]
```

### "Threadbare Recitals"
```
Defendant argues that [Plaintiff]'s Complaint merely recites the
elements of a cause of action without factual support. In fact, the
Complaint pleads [specific facts beyond elements — cite Complaint ¶s].
These facts move [Plaintiff]'s claims from the conceivable to the
plausible. [LPB authority + Pinpoint.]
```

### "Insufficient Factual Detail"
```
Defendant demands a level of factual specificity not required at the
pleading stage. [Cite LPB authority establishing that discovery, not
pleading, is the vehicle for factual development.] The purpose of a
motion to dismiss is to test the legal sufficiency of the claim, not
to require proof of the claim. [LPB authority + Pinpoint.]
```

### "Affirmative Defense on Face of Complaint"
```
Defendant raises [affirmative defense — e.g., statute of limitations,
immunity] as a basis for dismissal. An affirmative defense may warrant
dismissal only when it is apparent on the face of the complaint and
admits of no exceptions. [LPB authority + Pinpoint.]

Here, the Complaint does not conclusively establish [the defense]
because [analysis — e.g., tolling, discovery rule, exceptions].
Resolution of this defense requires factual development beyond the
pleading stage. [LPB authority + Pinpoint.]
```

### "Matters Outside the Pleadings"
```
Defendant attaches [exhibit/declaration] to the Motion to Dismiss.
Because this material is outside the pleadings, the Court has two
options: (1) exclude the material and decide the motion on the
pleadings alone, or (2) convert the motion to one for summary judgment
under [NMRA 1-012(B) / FRCP 12(d)] and give the parties opportunity
for discovery. [LPB authority + Pinpoint.]

[DECISION REQUIRED] — [Plaintiff] should [argue for exclusion / argue
for conversion with discovery opportunity / address the extrinsic
evidence on the merits]. Attorney must decide.
```
