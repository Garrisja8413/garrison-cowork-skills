## NMRA Rules Index — Motion to Dismiss & Pleading Standards

### NMRA 1-007 — Pleadings Allowed; Form of Motions
- 1-007(B): Motions and other papers — motions shall state with
  particularity the grounds and the relief sought. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-008 — General Rules of Pleading
- **1-008(A):** Claims for relief — a pleading that states a claim for
  relief shall contain a short and plain statement of the claim showing
  that the pleader is entitled to relief. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(A)(1):** A short and plain statement of the grounds for the
  court's jurisdiction. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(A)(2):** A short and plain statement of the claim showing
  that the pleader is entitled to relief. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(A)(3):** A demand for the relief sought. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(E):** Pleading to be concise and direct; consistency — each
  averment shall be simple, concise, and direct. A party may set forth
  two or more statements alternatively or hypothetically. A party may
  state as many separate claims or defenses regardless of consistency.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(F):** Construction of pleadings — all pleadings shall be
  construed so as to do justice. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-009 — Pleading Special Matters
- **1-009(B):** Fraud, mistake, condition of mind — circumstances
  constituting fraud or mistake shall be stated with particularity.
  Malice, intent, knowledge, and other condition of mind may be
  averred generally. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-009(C):** Conditions precedent — sufficient to aver generally
  that all conditions precedent have been performed or occurred.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-009(G):** Special damages — items of special damage shall be
  specifically stated. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-010 — Form of Pleadings
- **1-010(A):** Caption — every pleading shall contain a caption with
  the name of the court, title of the action, file number, and
  designation. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-010(B):** Paragraphs; separate statements — averments shall be
  made in numbered paragraphs. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-012 — Defenses and Objections
- **1-012(B):** Every defense to a claim for relief shall be asserted
  in the responsive pleading, except the following defenses may be
  raised by motion: [VERIFY-CITE]
  - (1) lack of jurisdiction over the subject matter
  - (2) lack of jurisdiction over the person
  - (3) improper venue
  - (4) insufficiency of process
  - (5) insufficiency of service of process
  - (6) failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted
  - (7) failure to join a party under Rule 1-019
- **1-012(B) (conversion clause):** If on a motion asserting the
  defense numbered (6) matters outside the pleading are presented to
  and not excluded by the court, the motion shall be treated as one
  for summary judgment and disposed of as provided in Rule 1-056.
  All parties shall be given reasonable opportunity to present all
  material made pertinent to such a motion. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-012(G):** Consolidation of defenses in motion — a party who
  makes a motion under this rule may join with it any other motions
  then available. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-012(H):** Waiver or preservation of certain defenses —
  - (1) A defense of lack of personal jurisdiction, improper venue,
    insufficiency of process, or insufficiency of service of process
    is waived if not raised by motion under this rule or in a
    responsive pleading. [VERIFY-CITE]
  - (2) A defense of failure to state a claim, failure to join
    indispensable party, or lack of subject matter jurisdiction is
    NOT waived and may be raised at any time. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-015 — Amended and Supplemental Pleadings
- **1-015(A):** Amendments — a party may amend the party's pleading
  once as a matter of course at any time before a responsive pleading
  is served. Otherwise, a party may amend the party's pleading only
  by leave of court or by written consent of the adverse party; and
  leave shall be freely given when justice so requires. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-015(C):** Relation back of amendments — an amendment relates
  back to the date of the original pleading when the claim or defense
  asserted in the amended pleading arose out of the conduct, transaction,
  or occurrence set forth in the original pleading. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-019 — Joinder of Persons Needed for Just Adjudication
- **1-019(A):** Persons to be joined if feasible — person shall be
  joined if (1) complete relief cannot be accorded among existing
  parties without the absent person, or (2) the absent person claims
  an interest that could impair the ability to protect that interest
  or subject existing parties to double liability. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-019(B):** Determination by court whenever joinder not feasible —
  court considers: (1) extent of prejudice from non-joinder, (2)
  ability to lessen prejudice by protective provisions, (3) adequacy
  of judgment without absent party, (4) whether plaintiff has adequate
  remedy if dismissed. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-056 — Summary Judgment
- **1-056(C):** Standard — judgment shall be rendered if the pleadings,
  depositions, answers to interrogatories, and admissions on file,
  together with the affidavits, show that there is no genuine issue
  as to any material fact and that the moving party is entitled to
  judgment as a matter of law. [VERIFY-CITE]
- Referenced when MTD converts under 1-012(B) conversion clause.

## Key NM Case Law Framework

### Dismissal Standard — 1-012(B)(6)

**Principle:** A motion to dismiss for failure to state a claim tests the
legal sufficiency of the complaint. The complaint should not be dismissed
unless it appears that the plaintiff can prove no set of facts in support
of the claims that would entitle the plaintiff to relief.

- [NM case holding that dismissal is a drastic remedy to be used

### Notice Pleading Standard — NMRA 1-008(A)

**Principle:** New Mexico follows a notice pleading standard. The complaint
need only provide fair notice of the nature of the claim and the grounds
upon which it rests. The complaint is not required to state facts
constituting a cause of action.

- [NM case on notice pleading — complaint need only give fair notice]
  [VERIFY-CITE]

  plausibility standard] [VERIFY-CITE]

### Leave to Amend — NMRA 1-015(A)

**Principle:** Leave to amend shall be freely given when justice so
requires. Denial of leave to amend is appropriate only for undue delay,
bad faith, undue prejudice to the opposing party, or futility.

### Heightened Pleading — Fraud (NMRA 1-009(B))

**Principle:** Claims sounding in fraud or mistake must be pleaded with
particularity — who, what, when, where, how. Other claims need only
meet the general notice pleading standard.

### Subject Matter Jurisdiction — 1-012(B)(1)

**Principle:** NM district courts have general jurisdiction over civil
matters. The Tort Claims Act (NMSA 1978, §§ 41-4-1 to -30) provides
a limited waiver of sovereign immunity for government entities,
creating jurisdictional questions in cases against the state or its
subdivisions.

**Principle:** NM's long-arm statute (NMSA 1978, § 38-1-16) extends
jurisdiction to the full extent permitted by constitutional due process.

### Conversion to Summary Judgment

**Principle:** When matters outside the pleadings are presented on a
1-012(B)(6) motion and not excluded by the court, the motion converts
to summary judgment. All parties must receive reasonable opportunity

**Principle:** An affirmative defense may be raised on a motion to
dismiss only when the defense appears on the face of the complaint
and admits of no exception. If the complaint's allegations do not

## Authority Hierarchy (MTD Response)

When citing authority in the opposition brief:

1. **NMSC** — New Mexico Supreme Court (binding in NM state court)
   Format: `[Case Name], [Year]-NMSC-[###], ¶ [##]`
2. **NMCOA** — New Mexico Court of Appeals (binding unless overruled)
   Format: `[Case Name], [Year]-NMCA-[###], ¶ [##]`
3. **NMRA** — New Mexico Rules Annotated (binding procedural rules)
   Format: `NMRA 1-[###]([subsection])`
4. **NMSA** — New Mexico Statutes Annotated (binding substantive law)
   Format: `NMSA 1978, § [xx]-[xx]-[x]`
5. **UJI** — Uniform Jury Instructions (authoritative on elements)
   Format: `UJI 13-[####] NMRA`
6. **10th Circuit** — persuasive only for state court; controlling for D.N.M.
   Format: `[Case Name], [F.3d/F.4th cite], [pinpoint] (10th Cir. [Year])`
7. **SCOTUS** — controlling on federal constitutional issues
   Format: `[Case Name], [U.S. cite], [pinpoint] ([Year])`
8. **D.N.M.** — persuasive for state court; persuasive/instructive for D.N.M.
   Format: `[Case Name], [F. Supp. cite], [pinpoint] (D.N.M. [Year])`
9. **Other circuits** — least weight; use only when NM/10th Cir. silent
   Always mark: `persuasive only — [Circuit]`

All case citations must include pinpoint paragraph or page number.
All citations `[VERIFY-CITE]` until verified through LPB or Lexis.
