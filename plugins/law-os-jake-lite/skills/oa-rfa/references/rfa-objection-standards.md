# RFA Objection Validity Standards — OA-RFA Reference

**Version:** 1.0
**Applies to:** OA-RFA (Objection Analyzer — Requests for Admission)

## Governing Rules

### Federal
- **FRCP 36(a)(1)-(6):** Request for admission procedure
- **FRCP 36(a)(3):** Deemed admitted if not timely answered
- **FRCP 36(a)(4):** Insufficiency of answer or objection — court may order admission or determine sufficiency
- **FRCP 36(a)(5):** Objection must state grounds with specificity
- **FRCP 36(b):** Withdrawal or amendment of admissions requires court leave
- **FRCP 36(a)(2):** 25-request limit without leave or stipulation

### New Mexico
- **NMRA 1-036:** Requests for admission (tracks federal rule with NM modifications)
- **NMRA 1-036(A):** No numerical limit in NM state court (unlike federal 25-request cap)
- **NMRA 1-036(B):** Response within 30 days of service

## Deemed Admission Doctrine

An RFA is deemed admitted if:
1. The responding party fails to serve a written answer or objection within 30 days (FRCP 36(a)(3))
2. The response is not signed by the party or attorney
3. The response fails to specifically admit, deny, or state reasons for inability to admit/deny

**Key cases:**
- A deemed admission is a judicial admission — it conclusively establishes the matter
- Withdrawal requires showing: (a) presentation of the merits will be served, AND (b) the requesting party will not be prejudiced (FRCP 36(b))
- Courts apply a two-part test balancing merits presentation against prejudice

## Objection Sufficiency Requirements

A valid RFA objection must:
1. **State the ground specifically** — boilerplate insufficient (FRCP 36(a)(5))
2. **Be timely filed** — within 30 days of service
3. **Not be used to avoid an answer** — an objection to part of an RFA requires admission/denial of the non-objectionable part
4. **Be signed** by the attorney or party

## Response Requirements (for ANALYZE-INCOMING mode)

A valid RFA response must either:
- **Admit** the matter
- **Deny** the matter (or deny the part not admitted and qualify/explain)
- **State in detail** why the party cannot truthfully admit or deny
- **Object** with specificity to the request

A response that fails to comply may result in the matter being deemed admitted.
