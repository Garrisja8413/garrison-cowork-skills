# IKP INTERVIEW — Comprehensive Intake Interview

## Purpose

Gather the granular facts needed for an attorney to evaluate liability,
damages, and insurance coverage. This interview typically takes 30–45 minutes
and should be conducted in a single session when possible.

## Before Starting

- Confirm you have completed CAPTURE and SCREEN modes
- Have the lead record open in front of you
- Confirm SOL calculation is complete and logged
- If SOL is CRITICAL or URGENT, note this to the attorney before proceeding

## Section A: Incident Narrative & Liability

### A.1 Step-by-Step Narrative

> "I'd like you to walk me through exactly what happened, from the beginning.
> Take your time — every detail matters."

**Capture:**
- What the client was doing immediately before the incident
- How the incident began
- Sequence of events during the incident
- What happened immediately after
- Client's own words (note direct quotes when possible)

### A.2 Location & Conditions

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Exact location | "Where exactly did this happen? What intersection, address, or business?" | Street name, cross streets, highway mile marker |
| City/County | "What city and county?" | Jurisdiction determination |
| Lighting | "What were the lighting conditions?" | Daylight, dusk, dark, streetlights |
| Weather | "What was the weather like?" | Rain, snow, ice, fog, clear |
| Road conditions | "How were the road conditions?" | Wet, dry, construction, potholes |
| Traffic | "How was the traffic at the time?" | Heavy, light, stop-and-go |

### A.3 Police / Incident Report

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Report filed? | "Was a police report or incident report filed?" | YES/NO |
| Report number | "Do you have the report number?" | May not have yet |
| Agency | "Which department responded? Police, sheriff, state police?" | Responding agency name |
| Officers | "Do you remember the officer's name or badge number?" | For report retrieval |
| Citations | "Was anyone given a ticket or citation?" | Important for liability |
| Arrest | "Was anyone arrested at the scene?" | DUI, reckless driving |

### A.4 Witnesses & Evidence

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Witnesses | "Were there any witnesses? Passengers? Bystanders?" | Names and contact info |
| Witness statements | "Did any witnesses talk to you or the police about what they saw?" | Note what witnesses reportedly said |
| Dashcam | "Do you or the other driver have a dashcam?" | Critical evidence |
| Bodycam | "Were police wearing body cameras?" | FOIA/records request needed |
| Surveillance | "Were there any security cameras nearby? Gas station, business, traffic camera?" | Preservation letter may be needed |
| Photos | "Did you take any photos at the scene?" | Request copies |
| Phone records | "Were you or the other driver using a phone at the time?" | Distracted driving evidence |

### A.5 Other Party Information

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Defendant name | "Do you have the other person's name?" | Full legal name |
| Defendant address | "Do you have their address?" | If exchanged at scene |
| Defendant phone | "Did you get their phone number?" | If exchanged |
| Defendant employer | "Were they working/driving for a company at the time?" | Respondeat superior |
| Vehicle info | "What type of vehicle were they driving? Make, model, color, plate?" | For MVC |
| Commercial vehicle | "Was it a commercial truck, delivery van, rideshare, or company vehicle?" | Higher coverage, federal regs |

## Section B: Medical & Injury Assessment

### B.1 Immediate Treatment

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Ambulance | "Did you take an ambulance from the scene?" | Gap analysis — ambulance refusal is defense argument |
| First treatment | "Where were you first treated? ER, Urgent Care, or your doctor?" | Facility name |
| Treatment date | "When was your first medical visit for these injuries?" | Should match DOI or within days |
| Admitted | "Were you admitted to the hospital? For how long?" | Severity indicator |

### B.2 Injuries & Diagnoses

> "I need to know every injury you were diagnosed with, from head to toe."

| Body Area | Common PI Injuries | Ask Specifically |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Head/Brain | Concussion, TBI, skull fracture | "Any headaches, dizziness, memory problems, confusion?" |
| Neck | Cervical strain/sprain, disc herniation, whiplash | "Any neck pain, stiffness, numbness in arms?" |
| Back | Lumbar strain, disc herniation, compression fracture | "Any back pain, numbness in legs, difficulty walking?" |
| Shoulder | Rotator cuff tear, labrum tear, dislocation | "Any shoulder pain, limited range of motion?" |
| Knee | Meniscus tear, ACL/MCL injury, fracture | "Any knee pain, swelling, instability?" |
| Extremities | Fractures, sprains, lacerations | "Any broken bones? Cuts requiring stitches?" |
| Internal | Organ damage, internal bleeding | "Any internal injuries diagnosed?" |
| Psychological | PTSD, anxiety, depression, sleep issues | "Any anxiety, nightmares, difficulty sleeping, fear of driving?" |

### B.3 Current Treatment & Providers

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Current pain level | "On a scale of 1–10, what's your pain level today?" | Baseline for tracking |
| Current providers | "Who are you currently seeing for treatment?" | List ALL providers |
| PCP | "Who is your primary care doctor?" | Name and practice |
| Specialists | "Are you seeing any specialists? Orthopedic, neurologist, pain management?" | Name each |
| Chiropractor | "Are you seeing a chiropractor?" | Name and frequency |
| Physical therapy | "Are you doing physical therapy?" | Name and frequency |
| Medications | "What medications were you prescribed for your injuries?" | List medications |
| Surgery | "Has any doctor recommended surgery?" | Procedure name, doctor |
| Future treatment | "Has your doctor told you what treatment you'll need going forward?" | Prognosis indication |

### B.4 Pre-Existing Conditions (CRITICAL)

> "I need to ask about your medical history before this incident. This is very
> important — the other side WILL investigate your medical background, so it's
> much better for us to know about everything upfront."

| Data Point | Question | Why It Matters |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Same body parts | "Have you ever had injuries or treatment to the same body parts BEFORE this incident?" | Defense eggshell plaintiff / aggravation argument |
| Prior accidents | "Have you ever been in another car accident or had another injury claim?" | Prior claims history |
| Prior surgeries | "Have you had any surgeries before this incident?" | Distinguish pre-existing from new |
| Chronic conditions | "Do you have any chronic pain conditions, arthritis, fibromyalgia, or degenerative issues?" | May complicate causation |
| Workers' comp | "Have you ever filed a workers' compensation claim?" | Prior injury history |
| Disability | "Are you currently receiving disability benefits for any reason?" | Pre-existing vs. new |

**Document the client's exact words.** Their transparency now prevents
devastating surprises during discovery or at deposition.

### B.5 Impact on Daily Life

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Work status | "Are you able to work? Have you missed any work?" | Lost wages calculation |
| Employer | "Who is your employer? What do you do?" | Wage documentation |
| Income | "What is your approximate income?" | Damages calculation |
| Daily activities | "What can't you do now that you could do before?" | Pain and suffering |
| Hobbies/recreation | "How has this affected your hobbies, exercise, or recreation?" | Loss of enjoyment |
| Household tasks | "Are you able to do housework, yard work, cooking, childcare?" | Loss of household services |
| Sleep | "How has this affected your sleep?" | Quality of life |
| Relationships | "How has this affected your relationships or family life?" | Loss of consortium potential |

## Section C: Insurance & Sources of Recovery

### C.1 Defendant's Insurance

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Insurance company | "Do you have the other party's insurance company name?" | From exchange at scene or police report |
| Policy number | "Do you have the policy number?" | May not be available |
| Claim number | "Has a claim already been opened? What's the claim number?" | If adjuster has called |
| Adjuster name | "Has an adjuster contacted you? What was their name?" | Note: instruct not to speak with them |
| Policy limits | "Do you know their policy limits?" | Rarely known at intake |

### C.2 Client's Auto Insurance (MVC Cases)

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Carrier | "Who is your auto insurance company?" | Get exact company name |
| Policy number | "What's your policy number?" | From insurance card |
| UM/UIM | "Do you have Uninsured or Underinsured Motorist coverage?" | May not know — order declarations page |
| UM/UIM limits | "Do you know the limits?" | If known |
| MedPay | "Do you have Medical Payments coverage?" | $5K–$25K typical in NM |
| PIP | "Do you have Personal Injury Protection?" | PIP state requirements |
| Collision | "Do you have collision/comprehensive coverage?" | For property damage |
| Rental | "Do you have rental car coverage?" | For transportation during repair |

### C.3 Client's Health Insurance

| Data Point | Question | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Type | "Do you have health insurance?" | Private, Medicare, Medicaid, VA/Tricare, none |
| Carrier | "What health insurance company?" | BCBS, UHC, Aetna, etc. |
| Plan type | "Is it through your employer or individual?" | Group vs. individual (ERISA implications) |
| Medicare | "Are you on Medicare?" | Medicare Secondary Payer Act — lien obligations |
| Medicaid | "Are you on Medicaid?" | State lien obligations |
| VA/Tricare | "Do you receive VA or Tricare benefits?" | Federal lien obligations |
| No insurance | "If you don't have health insurance, have you been able to get treatment?" | May need LOP referral |

## Interview Completion

### Completeness Check

Before ending the interview, verify all sections have at least partial data:

- [ ] Incident narrative (who, what, when, where, how)
- [ ] Police report information
- [ ] Witnesses identified (or confirmed none)
- [ ] Evidence identified (video, photos)
- [ ] All injuries documented
- [ ] All current providers listed
- [ ] Pre-existing conditions asked and documented
- [ ] Impact on daily life recorded
- [ ] Defendant insurance information
- [ ] Client auto insurance information
- [ ] Client health insurance information
