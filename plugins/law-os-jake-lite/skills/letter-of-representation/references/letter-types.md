# Letter Types Guide

Detailed guidance for each type of letter this skill can draft.

---

## 1. Defendant Notice Letter of Representation (LOR)

**Use for:** Defendants and adverse entities to be placed on formal notice of claim and preservation obligations

**Required elements:**
1. Date + service method (Certified Mail RR#, Email, Fax, U.S. Mail)
2. Recipient address block
3. RE: line with Claimant, Date of Incident, Location
4. Representation statement + no-contact directive
5. Revocation/disavowal paragraph
6. Brief incident summary (2-6 sentences, sanitized)
7. Put defendant on notice of the claim and factual basis
8. Demand preservation of evidence (vehicles, surveillance footage, ESI, and physical evidence)
9. Spoliation warning with specific categories to preserve
10. Request defendant insurance information
11. Request defendant counsel contact information if represented
12. Professional close
13. Signature block

**Standard opening:**
```
This office represents [MERGE:ClientInitials] regarding an incident that occurred on [MERGE:AccidentDate], at [LOCATION DESCRIPTION]. [1-2 sentence incident summary]. Please direct all further communications to this office and do not contact my client about this matter.
```

**Revocation/disavowal paragraph (always include):**
```
My client revokes prior medical authorizations and disavows previous settlement agreements, releases of liability, and written statements pursuant to NMSA §41-1-1.
```

**Alternative revocation/disavowal (longer form):**
```
My client also disavows any previous settlement agreement, release of liability, and/or any written statements made, pursuant to NMSA §41-1-1. I am assembling the information and documentation necessary to evaluate this claim.
```

**Request for handling:**
```
Contact this office to confirm handling of this claim within fifteen (15) days. Also, provide the following:
```

**Professional close:**
```
If for any reason you wish to discuss this claim or the above requests, please do not hesitate to call. Thank you. I look forward to working with you.
```

**Signature block:**
```
Sincerely,

[ATTORNEY NAME]

[ATTORNEY INITIALS]/[PARALEGAL INITIALS]
```

## 2. Witness Cooperation Letter

**Use for:** Witnesses with potential knowledge of the incident

**Tone requirements:** Non-adversarial and cooperative

**Required elements:**
1. Date + service method
2. Recipient address block
3. RE: line with incident/date/location
4. Neutral incident identification
5. Statement of witness potential knowledge
6. Request for voluntary cooperation and interview availability
7. Firm contact information for scheduling
8. Professional close
9. Signature block

**Hard rules:**
- Do **not** include preservation demands unless the witness controls relevant evidence
- Do **not** use accusatory language

---

## 3. Insurer Claim Notice Letter

**Use for:** Insurance carriers receiving claim notice and coverage demands

**Required elements:**
1. Identify insured, policy number (if known), and date of loss
2. Put insurer on notice of the claim
3. Demand acknowledgment of coverage and opening of claim
4. Request complete copy of applicable policy (declarations, endorsements, amendments)
5. Request adjuster assignment and contact information
6. Reference applicable insurance regulations and bad faith standards
7. For UM/UIM matters: request UM/UIM provisions, rejection forms, and offer/rejection process documentation

## 4. Tort Claim Notice

**Use for:** Government entities (City, County, State agencies, public bodies)

**Statutory basis:** NMSA §41-4-1 et seq. (New Mexico Tort Claims Act)

**Critical requirements:**
- Must include: time, place, and circumstances of loss/injury
- Must be sent to proper government entity
- Subject to strict deadlines (verify before drafting)

**Required elements:**
1. Date
2. Service method: "By Mail and Fax to [FAX NUMBER]" or "By Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested RR# [NUMBER]"
3. Recipient address (government entity + Office of Risk Management if applicable)
4. RE: line with Claimant, Date of Incident, Location
5. Tort Claims Act notice paragraph
6. "Description of Incident" section
7. "Description of Injuries" section  
8. Professional close
9. Signature block

**Opening paragraph (use this format):**
```
Please be advised that this correspondence serves as, and is intended to give, the State of New Mexico and [ENTITY NAME] (hereinafter "[ABBREVIATION]") notice of a tort claim(s) under the New Mexico Tort Claims Act, NMSA 1978, §§ 41-4-1 et seq., and [if applicable: the New Mexico Civil Rights Act, NMSA 1978, §§ 41-4A-1 et seq.].
```

**Alternative opening (simpler):**
```
Pursuant to the New Mexico Tort Claims Act, NMSA §41-4-1, et seq., [PL-NAME] provides notice of [his/her/its] claim against [ENTITY NAME] and each employees, agents or affiliates of these agencies, for the violation of the New Mexico Tort Claims Act.
```

**Description of Incident:**
```
Description of Incident

On [DATE], [INCIDENT FACTS - 3-8 sentences describing what happened, who was involved, and the tortious conduct].
```

**Description of Injuries:**
```
Description of Injuries

As a result of [ENTITY NAME]'s tortious conduct, [CLIENT] suffered [INJURY SUMMARY - 1-3 sentences].
```

**Preservation notice (optional but recommended):**
Include standard preservation/spoliation language and applicable categories from request-library.md. Add paragraph:
```
In addition, this letter serves as our formal request that you maintain all records pertinent to this incident for future review, including the preservation of all electronic and paper documentation; all correspondence relating to this incident; all contracts or agreements between any of the above-referenced entities, their agents, or their employees; all photographs of the scene and any individuals involved in or present at the scene of the incident, either before or after; all incident or investigative reports and/or any witness statements you may have in your possession or available to you; all policies and procedures given to or otherwise communicated by you to your employees involved in this incident; all written policies and procedures, employment handbooks, and/or employment manuals in effect at the time of this incident; and any and all materials or documents in your possession that is in any way related to this incident.

This letter also notifies you of a potential claim for spoliation of evidence should you fail to preserve such records.
```

**Professional close:**
```
If for any reason you wish to discuss this claim or the above requests, please do not hesitate to contact this office. Thank you. I look forward to working with you.
```

## 5. Civil Rights Act Notice

**Use for:** Law enforcement agencies (police departments, sheriff's offices, certified law enforcement officers)

**Statutory basis:** NMSA §§ 41-4A-1 to 41-4A-13 (New Mexico Civil Rights Act)

**Required elements:**
1. Date
2. Service method: "By Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested RR# [NUMBER]" AND "by email to [EMAIL]"
3. Recipient address (law enforcement agency or department address)
4. Subject line: "Notice of Civil Rights Act Claims"
5. Opening paragraph with statutory reference
6. "Time, Place, and Circumstances of the Loss(es) or Injury(ies)" section
7. Bill of rights deprivation language
8. "Actual Notice of Occurrence(s)" section
9. Request to confirm receipt
12. Professional close
13. Signature block

**Opening paragraph (use this format):**
```
Dear [AGENCY NAME]:

[CLIENT NAME(S)] ("Claimant(s)") claims damages from an act or omission of a certified law enforcement officer under the New Mexico Civil Rights Act NMSA §§ 41-4A-1 to 41-4A-13 ("Civil Rights Act"). For purposes of the Civil Rights Act, Claimant(s) present(s) this written notice stating the time, place and circumstances of the loss(es) or injury(ies).
```

**Time, Place, and Circumstances section:**
```
Time, Place, and Circumstances of the Loss(es) or Injury(ies)

[ENTITY NAME] or person acting on behalf of, under color of or within the course and scope of the authority of [ENTITY NAME] subjected or caused to be subjected Claimant(s) to deprivation of rights, privileges or immunities secured pursuant to the bill of rights of the constitution of New Mexico.

On [DATE], [INCIDENT FACTS - 3-10 sentences describing the conduct and constitutional deprivation].

Claimant(s) suffered a deprivation of rights, privileges or immunities pursuant to the bill of rights of the constitution of New Mexico due to acts or omissions of [ENTITY NAME] or person acting on behalf of, under color of or within the course and scope of the authority of [ENTITY NAME], for which it is liable for actual damages. Claimant(s) may also be entitled to equitable or injunctive relief.
```

**Actual Notice section:**
```
Actual Notice of Occurrence(s)

Further, [ENTITY NAME] had actual notice of the occurrences. If [ENTITY NAME] disputes actual notice or believes this notice to be untimely, please state the basis of your assertions and beliefs in writing.
```

**Request to confirm receipt:**
```
Please Confirm Receipt of this Notice

Please confirm receipt of this Notice of Civil Rights Act Claims in writing by no later than [7-15 days from sending]. If you do not believe that you are the proper recipient for this notice, or that this notice should be provided to another person, please let this office know in writing when you confirm receipt. If you have any other questions, concerns, or comments concerning this notice, please do not hesitate to contact us.
```

**Professional close:**
```
Thank you.

Sincerely,

[ATTORNEY NAME]

[ATTORNEY INITIALS]/[PARALEGAL INITIALS]
```

## 6. Follow-Up Letter (No Response)

**Use for:** When initial letter sent but no response received within deadline

**Tone:** Professional but firmer; emphasize deadline passed and continuing efforts

**Required elements:**
1. Date
2. Service method (same as original + may add additional methods)
3. Recipient address
4. RE: line + "FOLLOW-UP" or "SECOND REQUEST"
5. Reference to original letter by date
6. Note that deadline has passed
7. Reiterate key missing items
8. Set new deadline (shorter, e.g., 7-10 days)
9. May mention continuing good faith efforts or preservation of rights
10. Professional close

**Opening:**
```
On [ORIGINAL DATE], this office sent you a Letter of Representation regarding [CLAIMANT] and the incident that occurred on [INCIDENT DATE]. [IF APPLICABLE: A copy is enclosed for your reference.]

As of this date, we have not received a response to our letter. The [15]-day deadline for response has passed.
```

**Reiteration of requests:**
```
We again request that you:

• Confirm handling of this claim
• Provide the documents requested in our original letter, including:
  - [LIST KEY CATEGORIES: e.g., incident reports, video surveillance, insurance policy information]
• [IF APPLICABLE: Admit that your insured is liable for this incident]
• Preserve all evidence as detailed in our original letter
```

**New deadline:**
```
Please respond to this letter within [7-10] days. We remain committed to resolving this matter in good faith but must protect our client's rights.
```

**Alternative for multiple non-responses (firmer):**
```
This is our [second/third] attempt to obtain a response from you. If we do not receive a response within [7] days, we will have no choice but to [proceed with litigation/file a formal complaint/etc.].
```

## 7. Follow-Up Letter (Insufficient Response)

**Use for:** When initial response received but missing key items or incomplete

**Tone:** Professional, constructive, specific about what's missing

**Required elements:**
1. Date
2. Recipient address
3. RE: line + "FOLLOW-UP" or "SUPPLEMENTAL REQUEST"
4. Acknowledge response received
5. Identify specifically what's missing or incomplete
6. Re-request missing items
7. Set deadline (7-14 days)
8. Professional close

**Opening:**
```
Thank you for your [DATE] response to our Letter of Representation regarding [CLAIMANT] and the incident on [INCIDENT DATE].

However, your response was incomplete. Specifically, we have not received the following:
```

**List missing items:**
```
• [SPECIFIC ITEM 1: e.g., Video surveillance footage from 5:00 AM to 6:00 AM on the date of incident]
• [SPECIFIC ITEM 2: e.g., Incident report prepared by [NAME] on [DATE]]
• [SPECIFIC ITEM 3: e.g., Insurance policy declaration page]
```

**Re-request:**
```
Please provide these items within [7-14] days. [IF APPLICABLE: If any of these items do not exist or are not in your possession, please advise us in writing.]
```

## Letter Selection Decision Tree

Use this to determine which letter type to draft:

```
1. Is the recipient a government entity or law enforcement?
   YES → Is it about law enforcement conduct/constitutional rights?
         YES → Civil Rights Act Notice
         NO → Tort Claim Notice
   NO → Continue to 2

2. Is this a follow-up to a previous letter?
   YES → Did they respond?
         YES → Follow-Up (Insufficient Response)
         NO → Follow-Up (No Response)
   NO → Continue to 3

3. Standard Letter of Representation
```

## Special Variations

### Letter to Insurer (No Defendant Info Yet)

When you don't know who the insurer is, send to defendant with softer language:

**Opening:**
```
This office represents [CLIENT] regarding an incident that occurred on [DATE] at [LOCATION]. [INCIDENT SUMMARY].

Please direct all further communications to this office and do not contact my client about this matter. Please forward this letter to your insurance carrier immediately and have them contact this office within fifteen (15) days.
```

### Letter to Registered Agent

When serving via registered agent for service of process:

**Add after opening:**
```
This letter is being sent to you in your capacity as registered agent for service of process for [ENTITY NAME]. Please forward this immediately to [ENTITY NAME] and its insurance carrier.
```

### Letter with Settlement Discussion

Some letters may include openness to early resolution:

**Add before close:**
```
[CLIENT] is open to early resolution of [his/her/their] claims. We welcome any offers of settlement at this time and are willing to engage in good faith settlement negotiations.
```

## Final Formatting Notes

1. **Spacing:** Single space within sections, double space between sections
2. **Indentation:** Use tabs for RE: line formatting
3. **Signature block:** Leave 3-4 blank lines between "Sincerely," and typed name
4. **Page breaks:** Avoid orphaned signature blocks (keep on same page as closing paragraph)
5. **Letterhead:** Assume firm letterhead at top (don't include in draft)

## 7. Recipient-Specific LOR Protocols

### Defendant Recipients (Spoliation-Forward)

When recipient is a defendant business, property owner, or represented tortfeasor:
- Use firmer preservation framing.
- Include the full standard preservation/spoliation block.
- Add incident-specific categories likely to disappear quickly (video, logs, device data).
- State that deletion/overwrite after notice may support spoliation remedies.

### Witness Recipients (Cooperative Tone)

When recipient is a non-party witness or custodian of relevant facts:
- Use a cooperative, non-accusatory tone.
- Request preservation and voluntary coordination for statement/interview.
- Avoid liability-admission asks and avoid overbroad demands.
- Keep requests narrow: photos, communications, and contemporaneous notes.

### Insurer Recipients (UM/UIM Requests)

When recipient is first-party carrier or UM/UIM adjuster:
- Include UM/UIM-specific request set:
  - Complete policy declarations and endorsements
  - Stacking/anti-stacking language
  - Offset/credit position and calculation basis
  - Exhaustion prerequisites and proof requirements
  - Claim handling timeline and assigned adjuster information
- Ask for written coverage position by deadline.
