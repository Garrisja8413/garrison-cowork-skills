# Document Request Library

This library contains standardized request and preservation language that must be used VERBATIM in Letters of Representation. The same language will be reused word-for-word in discovery requests to prevent spoliation defense arguments.

**CRITICAL RULE:** Copy entire request blocks exactly as written. Do not modify, paraphrase, or abbreviate.

---

## Basic Document Requests

Use for all letters unless specifically omitted:

```
• Reports. Reports, accident reports, incident reports and documents taken or prepared by anyone investigating or inspecting the scene or the incident in this matter.

• Photos, Video, Images. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things depicting any place, object or individual concerning the incident or the injuries, or any other matter relating to this claim.

• Statements. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things, revealing statements by any person related to the incident or the injuries, or any other matter relating to this claim.

• Admission of liability. Please admit that your insured is liable for this incident.
```

**Notes:**
- "Admission of liability" bullet is optional—check with attorney
- Can be shortened to just first 3 bullets if admission not requested

## Standard Preservation/Spoliation Notice

Use this complete block for all letters with preservation language:

```
Additionally, preserve all potentially relevant evidence and records in this case without any additions, alterations or tampering. If any potential evidence has been added to, altered, tampered with, destroyed or misplaced, then document such and provide this office of the explanatory documentation. Any alteration, loss or destruction of evidence may result in a finding of liability for spoliation of evidence.

Specifically, preserve (and notify your insured to preserve) the following items of evidence in their original and unaltered format:
```

**Then add applicable preservation categories below (next sections).**

**Always end with this closing:**

```
You are hereby on notice that New Mexico law and discovery rules prohibit the spoliation or alteration or destruction of evidence.[FOOTNOTE] The above items must be maintained in their original condition and not altered or destroyed in any way as litigation is pending. You must take every reasonable step to preserve the above evidence until the final resolution of this litigation.
```

**Footnote text:**
```
[FOOTNOTE] See Segura v. K-Mart Corp., 2003-NMCA-013, 133 N.M. 192 and Restaurant Management Co. V. Kidde-Fenwal, Inc., 1999-NMCA-101, 127 N.M. 708.
```

## Core Preservation Categories

### Communications

```
• Communications. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning communications related to the incident, or the injuries, or any other matter relating to this claim. This request includes traditional communications and those through social media.
```

### Reports

```
• Reports. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things prepared concerning the incident, or the injuries, or any other matter relating to this claim.
```

### Business Documents

```
• Business Documents as it relates to this claim, including but not limited to receipts, invoices, bills, letters, facsimiles, e-mails, financial data, bank statements, checks, and other documentation discussing or reflecting such business.
```

### Identification of Witnesses

```
• Identification of Witnesses present at the scene on the date of incident (including but not limited to employee log sheets, time cards, payroll checks and stubs, or other data.)
```

### Insurance Claims File

```
• Insurance Claims File. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things contained in the claims file(s) maintained by your insurance carrier, or claims administrator, related to the incident, or the injuries, or any other matter relating to this claim.
```

## Premises Liability / Property-Specific Categories

### Premises Ownership and Occupancy

```
• Premises Ownership and Occupancy. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things showing ownership or occupancy of the premises where the incident occurred, including any title documents, contracts, lease or rental agreements, or other forms of documentation demonstrating ownership or occupancy.
```

### Employee Time Logs

```
• Employee Time Logs. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning employee work time, including time sheets, logs, payroll or other data listing individuals employed for any time during the six months preceding the incident, until 7 days following the incident.
```

### Manager Contracts

```
• Manager Contracts. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning the employment of the manager of the premises, including contracts, employment applications, position statements, and any other documents outlining or articulating the duties of the manager.
```

### Safety Documentation

```
• Safety Documentation. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning policies and procedures related to safety, and employee training thereon, and all communications concerning premises safety in place for the 2 years prior to the incident until the present.
```

### Maintenance Duties

```
• Maintenance Duties. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning or setting forth the responsibilities of any employee, contractor or other agent responsible for maintaining the premises, including contracts, indemnification agreements, employment manuals, employment applications, position statements, and any other documents outlining or articulating said responsibilities.
```

### Maintenance Records

```
• Maintenance Records. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning the maintenance, supervision and repair of the premises, from five years before the incident to the present, including but not limited to log sheets or other documentation of inspection, mopping, sweeping, cleaning, construction, repairs, and any other maintenance and repair records
```

### Safety Meeting Minutes

```
• Safety Meeting Minutes. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning minutes, memos, correspondence, notes or other documentation of any meetings on safety following the incident in this matter.
```

### Previous Complaints

```
• Previous Complaints. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning all incidents concerning claims for injuries, falls or otherwise, on any area of your premises from five years before the incident to the present.
```

### Code Violations

```
• Code Violations. All documents, electronically stored information, and other things concerning any notice, within the past three years, that any area within the subject premises violated any applicable safety code: building, fire or health.
```

## Premises Security / Violent Crime Categories

### Cellphone or Mobile Device

```
• Cellphone or Mobile Device. The cellular telephone or other mobile device (whether hand-held or vehicle-based) available to [IDENTIFY PERSON] on the date of the incident for inspection and data download. Do not delete or alter any data on the cell phone prior to its production.
```

### Cellphone or Mobile Device Bills and Records

```
• Cellphone or Mobile Device Bills and Records. All documents concerning the usage of any cellular telephone or other mobile device (whether hand-held or vehicle-based) by [IDENTIFY PERSON] on the date of the incident. This request is limited to use of the device from the day prior to the incident to 7 days after the incident.
```

### Social Media Postings and Direct Messages

```
• Social Media Postings and Direct Messages. All social media postings depicting, referring to, or otherwise related to this incident, the parties, or their injuries.
```

### Documents Identifying Witnesses

```
• Documents Identifying Witnesses. All documents revealing the identity of witnesses with personal knowledge of facts relevant to the claims or defenses in this matter, including witnesses present at the scene on the date of incident and insurance adjusters, employees, investigators, and others who have spoken to witnesses about this matter. This request includes employee log sheets, timecards, payroll checks and stubs, or other data.
```

## Short-Term Rental / Platform Categories

### Policy Declaration Pages

```
• Policy Declaration Pages. The declaration page for each insurance policy that may potentially cover claims arising from this incident. This request includes any excess policies which may be applicable.
```

### Platform Communications

```
• Communications and Correspondence. All correspondence, communications, and other similar documents concerning related to the incident, or the injuries or damages, or any other matter relating to this claim. This request includes traditional communications and those through social media or other means.
```

## Car Crash Categories

### Vehicle Information

```
• Vehicle Information. All documents concerning the ownership, registration, insurance, maintenance, and inspection of the vehicle(s) involved in this incident.
```

### Driver Information

```
• Driver Information. All documents concerning the driver(s) of the vehicle(s) involved in this incident, including but not limited to driver's licenses, driving records, employment records, and any documents concerning the scope of employment or agency relationship at the time of the incident.
```

### Alcohol/Drug Testing

```
• Alcohol/Drug Testing. All documents concerning any alcohol or drug testing conducted on any driver or occupant involved in this incident, including but not limited to test results, chain of custody documents, and laboratory reports.
```

### Electronic Data Recorders (EDR/Black Box)

```
• Electronic Data Recorders. All data from any electronic data recorder (EDR), event data recorder, or similar device installed in any vehicle involved in this incident, including but not limited to speed, braking, acceleration, steering, and collision data.
```

## Dram Shop / Alcohol Overservice Categories

### Point of Sale Records

```
• Point of Sale Records. All point of sale (POS) records, receipts, tabs, or similar documents showing alcoholic beverage sales and service on the date of the incident, including but not limited to transaction logs, itemized receipts, and server records.
```

### Server Training and Licensing

```
• Server Training and Licensing. All documents concerning the training, certification, and licensing of servers, bartenders, and managers regarding responsible alcohol service, including but not limited to training materials, certificates, and licensing records.
```

### Alcohol Service Policies

```
• Alcohol Service Policies. All policies and procedures concerning the service of alcoholic beverages, including but not limited to policies on checking identification, recognizing intoxication, refusing service, and safe transportation options.
```

### Video Surveillance (Dram Shop)

```
• Video Surveillance. All video surveillance footage from the establishment from [TIME RANGE] on the date of the incident, including but not limited to footage from bar areas, seating areas, entry/exit points, and parking areas.
```

## Slip/Trip/Fall Categories

### Floor Maintenance Logs

```
• Floor Maintenance Logs. All documents concerning the cleaning, mopping, sweeping, waxing, or other maintenance of floors in the area where the incident occurred, from 30 days before the incident to the present.
```

### Inspection Logs

```
• Inspection Logs. All documents concerning inspections of the area where the incident occurred, including but not limited to safety inspection logs, manager walkthrough logs, and maintenance request logs, from 30 days before the incident to the present.
```

### Weather Records (if outdoor)

```
• Weather Records. All documents concerning weather conditions on the date and time of the incident, including but not limited to internal weather logs, forecasts, or alerts received by the property owner or manager.
```

### Lighting Records (if relevant)

```
• Lighting Records. All documents concerning the lighting in the area where the incident occurred, including but not limited to maintenance records, inspection logs, and work orders for lighting repairs or replacements, from one year before the incident to the present.
```

## Usage Guidelines

**When drafting an LOR:**

1. **Start with Basic Document Requests** (always include unless specifically omitted)

2. **Add Standard Preservation/Spoliation Notice** (opening paragraph)

3. **Select applicable preservation categories** based on incident type:
   - **All letters:** Communications, Reports, Business Documents, Identification of Witnesses, Insurance Claims File
   - **Premises liability:** Add premises-specific categories (ownership, employee logs, manager contracts, safety docs, maintenance, complaints, code violations)
   - **Violent crime/security:** Add security-specific categories (cellphone, social media, witness identification)
   - **Short-term rental:** Add platform categories (policy dec pages, platform communications)
   - **Car crash:** Add vehicle categories (vehicle info, driver info, EDR data, alcohol testing if applicable)
   - **Dram shop:** Add alcohol categories (POS records, training, policies, video)
   - **Slip/fall:** Add premises + slip/fall categories (floor logs, inspection logs, weather, lighting)

4. **Close with spoliation case law paragraph** (always include)

5. **Customize bracketed placeholders** (e.g., [IDENTIFY PERSON], [TIME RANGE])

**DO NOT:**
- Modify request language (use verbatim)
- Combine bullets into one
- Paraphrase or simplify
- Omit preservation categories that apply
- Skip the spoliation closing paragraph

**Remember:** These exact words will be reused in formal discovery requests.
