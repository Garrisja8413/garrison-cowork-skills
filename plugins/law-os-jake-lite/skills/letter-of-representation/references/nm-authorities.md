# New Mexico Authorities Reference

New Mexico-specific case law, statutes, and rules relevant to Letters of Representation.

---

## Core Statutes

### NMSA §41-1-1 - Revocation of Settlement/Release

**Full citation:** NMSA 1978, § 41-1-1

**Use:** Include revocation/disavowal language in standard LORs

**Standard language to include:**
```
My client revokes prior medical authorizations and disavows previous settlement agreements, releases of liability, and written statements pursuant to NMSA §41-1-1.
```

**Alternative language:**
```
My client also disavows any previous settlement agreement, release of liability, and/or any written statements made, pursuant to NMSA §41-1-1.
```

**Purpose:** Protects client from settlements made before representation; invalidates prior releases

---

### NMSA §§ 41-4-1 et seq. - Tort Claims Act

**Full citation:** New Mexico Tort Claims Act, NMSA 1978, §§ 41-4-1 to 41-4-20

**Use:** Governs notices to government entities (City, County, State agencies)

**Key requirements:**
- Written notice required with time, place, circumstances
- Strict deadlines apply (verify before drafting)
- Notice must go to proper government entity

**Standard opening language:**
```
Pursuant to the New Mexico Tort Claims Act, NMSA §41-4-1, et seq., [CLIENT NAME] provides notice of [his/her/its] claim against [ENTITY NAME] and each employees, agents or affiliates of these agencies, for the violation of the New Mexico Tort Claims Act.
```

**Alternative opening (more formal):**
```
Please be advised that this correspondence serves as, and is intended to give, the State of New Mexico and [ENTITY NAME] notice of a tort claim(s) under the New Mexico Tort Claims Act, NMSA 1978, §§ 41-4-1 et seq.
```

---

### NMSA §§ 41-4A-1 to 41-4A-13 - Civil Rights Act

**Full citation:** New Mexico Civil Rights Act, NMSA 1978, §§ 41-4A-1 to 41-4A-13

**Use:** Governs notices for claims against law enforcement/certified officers

**Key requirements:**
- Written notice stating time, place, circumstances
- Must identify constitutional rights deprived
- Notice to law enforcement agency/department

**Standard opening language:**
```
[CLIENT NAME(S)] ("Claimant(s)") claims damages from an act or omission of a certified law enforcement officer under the New Mexico Civil Rights Act NMSA §§ 41-4A-1 to 41-4A-13 ("Civil Rights Act"). For purposes of the Civil Rights Act, Claimant(s) present(s) this written notice stating the time, place and circumstances of the loss(es) or injury(ies).
```

**Constitutional deprivation language:**
```
[ENTITY NAME] or person acting on behalf of, under color of or within the course and scope of the authority of [ENTITY NAME] subjected or caused to be subjected Claimant(s) to deprivation of rights, privileges or immunities secured pursuant to the bill of rights of the constitution of New Mexico.
```

## Spoliation of Evidence Case Law

### Segura v. K-Mart Corp.

**Full citation:** Segura v. K-Mart Corp., 2003-NMCA-013, 133 N.M. 192

**Use:** Cite in spoliation/preservation notice paragraphs

**Holding:** Spoliation of evidence can support adverse inference or separate tort claim

**Standard footnote:**
```
See Segura v. K-Mart Corp., 2003-NMCA-013, 133 N.M. 192 and Restaurant Management Co. V. Kidde-Fenwal, Inc., 1999-NMCA-101, 127 N.M. 708.
```

### Restaurant Management Co. v. Kidde-Fenwal, Inc.

**Full citation:** Restaurant Management Co. V. Kidde-Fenwal, Inc., 1999-NMCA-101, 127 N.M. 708

**Use:** Cite in spoliation/preservation notice paragraphs (paired with Segura)

**Holding:** Spoliation of evidence can support independent tort claim

**Standard preservation closing paragraph:**
```
You are hereby on notice that New Mexico law and discovery rules prohibit the spoliation or alteration or destruction of evidence.[1] The above items must be maintained in their original condition and not altered or destroyed in any way as litigation is pending. You must take every reasonable step to preserve the above evidence until the final resolution of this litigation.

[1] See Segura v. K-Mart Corp., 2003-NMCA-013, 133 N.M. 192 and Restaurant Management Co. V. Kidde-Fenwal, Inc., 1999-NMCA-101, 127 N.M. 708.
```

## Professional Conduct Rules

### Rule 16-104 NMRA - Competence; Communication

**Full citation:** Rule 16-104, New Mexico Rules of Professional Conduct

**Key requirements:**
- Keep clients informed
- Consult with clients about means to achieve objectives
- Professional liability insurance disclosure requirements (if applicable)

**Relevance to LORs:**
- Letters must reflect client's informed decisions
- Communications must be accurate and non-misleading
- Transparency in all professional communications

### D.N.M. LR-Civ. 83.9 - Creed of Professionalism

**Full citation:** D.N.M. Local Rule - Civil 83.9

**Requirement:** Attorneys in District of New Mexico must adhere to Rules of Professional Conduct and "Creed of Professionalism of the New Mexico Bench and Bar"

**Relevance to LORs:**
- Maintain professional tone
- Candor and civility
- Avoid unnecessary legal jargon

## Additional Authorities (Use When Applicable)

### Belanger v. Allstate Fire & Cas. Ins. Co.

**Full citation:** Belanger v. Allstate Fire & Cas. Ins. Co., 588 F. Supp. 3d 1249

**Topic:** Insurance transparency; duty to disclose material facts about policies

**Use:** When dealing with insurance coverage disputes or requesting policy information

**Key principle:** Insurers have duty to disclose material facts; attorneys should avoid withholding information that could mislead recipient

### Apodaca v. Young Am. Ins. Co.

**Full citation:** Apodaca v. Young Am. Ins. Co., 702 F. Supp. 3d 1094

**Topic:** Negligent misrepresentation

**Key principle:** Misrepresentations (intentional or negligent) require proof of material misstatement, reliance, and intent to induce reliance

**Relevance to LORs:** Ensure all statements are truthful and not misleading; avoid false claims about client's injuries or circumstances

**Full citation:** NMSA 1978, § 29-1-1

**Use:** Support preservation requests in LORs; potential basis for spoliation claim

**Note:** Also cite in Tort Claim Notices when requesting evidence preservation

## Standard Citation Format

When drafting letters, use these citation formats:

**In text (first reference):**
- "pursuant to NMSA §41-1-1"
- "under the New Mexico Tort Claims Act, NMSA §41-4-1 et seq."
- "under the New Mexico Civil Rights Act, NMSA §§ 41-4A-1 to 41-4A-13"

**In footnotes:**
```
[1] See Segura v. K-Mart Corp., 2003-NMCA-013, 133 N.M. 192 and Restaurant Management Co. V. Kidde-Fenwal, Inc., 1999-NMCA-101, 127 N.M. 708.
```

**In preservation paragraphs:**
- Integrate citations into text: "You are hereby on notice that New Mexico law and discovery rules prohibit the spoliation or alteration or destruction of evidence."
- Follow with footnote containing case citations

## Verification Requirements

**CRITICAL:** Always mark `[VERIFY-CITE]` when:
- Citing cases not listed in Legal Authorities Pack
- Referencing statutes without specific section numbers
- Applying rules to novel fact patterns
- Making claims about legal standards without support

**Before sending letter:**
- Verify all statute citations in Lexis+ AI
- Confirm case law is still good law (not overruled/distinguished)
- Check pinpoints for quoted language
- Ensure jurisdiction is correct (NM state vs. federal)

## When to Cite vs. When to Omit

**Always cite:**
- NMSA §41-1-1 in revocation/disavowal
- NMSA §41-4-1 et seq. in Tort Claim Notices
- NMSA §§41-4A-1 et seq. in Civil Rights Act Notices
- Segura and Restaurant Management in spoliation warnings

**Generally omit (unless strengthening argument):**
- Professional conduct rules (16-104, LR-Civ. 83.9) - follow them but don't cite
- General discovery rules - implied in preservation language
- Insurance coverage cases - unless dealing with coverage dispute

**Use judgment:**
- Case law supporting specific requests (e.g., requesting video in premises security case)
- Statutes supporting specific preservation categories
- Authority for "admission of liability" request

## Jurisdiction Notes

**New Mexico state practice:**
- Second Judicial District Court (Albuquerque) - most common venue in examples
- First, Thirteenth Judicial Districts also referenced
- State district court rules apply

**Federal practice (D.N.M.):**
- D.N.M. Local Rules apply
- May have different notice requirements
- Verify jurisdiction before citing state-only authorities
