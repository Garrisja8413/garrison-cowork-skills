## NMRA Pleading Rules Index (Complaint-Relevant)

### NMRA 1-003 — Commencement of Action
- **1-003:** A civil action is commenced by filing a complaint with the
  court. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-007 — Pleadings Allowed; Form of Motions
- **1-007(A):** Pleadings allowed: complaint and answer; reply to
  counterclaim; answer to cross-claim; third-party complaint and answer.
  No other pleading shall be allowed. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-008 — General Rules of Pleading
- **1-008(A):** Claims for relief — "A pleading which sets forth a claim
  for relief shall contain (1) a short and plain statement of the claim
  showing that the pleader is entitled to relief, and (2) a demand for
  judgment for the relief to which the pleader deems himself entitled."
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(B):** Denials — party shall state in short and plain terms the
  party's defenses to each claim asserted. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(C):** Affirmative defenses — enumerated list. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(D):** Effect of failure to deny — averments are admitted when
  not denied. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(E):** Pleading to be concise and direct; consistency — a party
  may set forth alternative or hypothetical claims regardless of
  consistency. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-009 — Pleading Special Matters
- **1-009(A):** Capacity — not necessary to aver capacity of a party to
  sue or be sued except to the extent required to show jurisdiction.
  [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-009(B):** Fraud, mistake, condition of mind — circumstances
  constituting fraud or mistake shall be stated with particularity.
  Malice, intent, knowledge, and other condition of mind may be averred
  generally. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-009(C):** Conditions precedent — sufficient to aver generally that
  all conditions precedent have been performed or occurred. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-009(G):** Special damages — items of special damage shall be
  specifically stated. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-010 — Form of Pleadings
- **1-010(A):** Caption — every pleading shall contain a caption with the
  name of the court, the title of the action, the file number, and a
  designation of the pleading. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-010(B):** Paragraphs; separate statements — all averments shall be
  made in numbered paragraphs, each limited as far as practicable to a
  single set of circumstances. Each claim or defense based on a separate
  transaction or occurrence shall be stated in a separate count.
  [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-011 — Signing of Pleadings; Sanctions
- **1-011(A):** Every pleading shall be signed by at least one attorney
  of record. By presenting a pleading, the attorney certifies that:
  (1) it is not for improper purpose; (2) claims are warranted by
  existing law or nonfrivolous argument; (3) factual contentions have
  evidentiary support or will likely have support after discovery;
  (4) denials are warranted on evidence. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-012 — Defenses and Objections
- **1-012(B):** How presented — defenses must be asserted in responsive
  pleading. Certain defenses may be raised by motion: (1) lack of
  jurisdiction over subject matter; (2) lack of jurisdiction over person;
  (3) improper venue; (4) insufficiency of process; (5) insufficiency of
  service; (6) failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted;
  (7) failure to join an indispensable party. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-012(B)(6):** Failure to state a claim — this is the primary
  defense the complaint must survive. The complaint must state facts
  sufficient to state a claim for relief. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-013 — Counterclaim and Cross-Claim
- Defendant may assert counterclaims (compulsory and permissive) in the
  Answer. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-014 — Third-Party Practice
- Defendant may implead third parties. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-015 — Amended and Supplemental Pleadings
- **1-015(A):** Amendment as a matter of course — within 10 days after
  service (if no responsive pleading required) or before responsive
  pleading is served. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-015(B):** Amendment by leave of court — leave shall be freely given
  when justice so requires. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-015(C):** Relation back — amendment relates back to the date of the
  original pleading when the claim arose out of the same conduct,
  transaction, or occurrence. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-015(C)(3):** Relation back for Doe defendants — amendment changing
  the party relates back if: (i) same transaction/occurrence, (ii) party
  received notice within service period, (iii) party knew or should have
  known the action would have been brought against them. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-017 — Parties Plaintiff and Defendant
- **1-017(A):** Real party in interest — every action shall be prosecuted
  in the name of the real party in interest. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-018 — Joinder of Claims and Remedies
- A party may join as many claims as the party has against an opposing
  party. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-019 — Joinder of Persons Needed for Just Adjudication
- Persons shall be joined as parties if complete relief cannot be
  accorded among existing parties or the absent person's interest would
  be impaired. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-020 — Permissive Joinder of Parties
- Persons may be joined as plaintiffs or defendants if claims arise from
  the same transaction/occurrence and common questions of law or fact.
  [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-038 — Jury Trial of Right
- **1-038(A):** Right to jury trial preserved. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-038(B):** Demand — must be served not later than 10 days after
  service of the last pleading directed to the triable issue. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-038(D):** Waiver — failure to demand = waiver. [VERIFY-CITE]

## Authority Hierarchy (Complaint Drafting)

When citing authority in the complaint and Cite Table:

1. **NMSA statutes** — controlling for statutory claims
   Format: `NMSA § [section]`
2. **NMRA rules** — controlling for procedural requirements
   Format: `NMRA 1-[###]([subsection])`
3. **NMSC** — New Mexico Supreme Court (binding)
   Format: `[Case Name], [Year]-NMSC-[###], ¶ [##]`
4. **NMCOA** — New Mexico Court of Appeals (binding unless overruled)
   Format: `[Case Name], [Year]-NMCA-[###], ¶ [##]`
5. **NM UJI** — Uniform Jury Instructions (authoritative for elements)
   Format: `UJI 13-[####]`
6. **10th Circuit** — persuasive for state court; controlling for federal
   Format: `[Case Name], [F.3d/F.4th cite], [pinpoint] (10th Cir. [Year])`
7. **SCOTUS** — controlling for federal constitutional issues
   Format: `[Case Name], [U.S. cite], [pinpoint] ([Year])`

All case citations must include pinpoint paragraph or page number.
