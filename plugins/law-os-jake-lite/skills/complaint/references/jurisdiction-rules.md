# Jurisdiction Rules — Complaint Reference

## New Mexico State Court

### Court System

- **District Courts:** General jurisdiction over civil matters
- **13 Judicial Districts** covering all 33 counties
- **No minimum amount in controversy** for district court jurisdiction
- **Magistrate courts** have limited jurisdiction (up to $10,000);
  PI claims exceeding this amount file in district court

### Pleading Standard: Notice Pleading

**Rule:** NMRA 1-008(A) — "A pleading which sets forth a claim for relief
shall contain a short and plain statement of the claim showing that the
pleader is entitled to relief."

**Standard:** Notice pleading — the complaint must give the defendant
fair notice of what the claim is and the grounds upon which it rests.

**What this means for complaint drafting:**
- Factual allegations need not be detailed evidence — but must identify
  the transaction, occurrence, and basis for liability
- Legal conclusions alone are insufficient — must plead some supporting facts
- Each element of the cause of action should be addressed
- Specific evidentiary facts are helpful but not strictly required
- The complaint should be sufficient to survive a motion to dismiss for
  failure to state a claim (NMRA 1-012(B)(6))

### Numbered Paragraphs

**NMRA 1-010(B):** "All averments of claim or defense shall be made in
numbered paragraphs, the contents of each of which shall be limited as
far as practicable to a statement of a single set of circumstances."

This aligns with the micro-decomposition principle — one fact per paragraph.

### Verification

**When required:**
- Quiet title actions
- Injunctions (NMRA 1-065)
- Specific statutory claims

**When NOT required (default for PI):**
- Standard negligence / PI complaints
- The SA shell includes verification pages only when flagged

### Alternative Pleading

**NMRA 1-008(E):** "A party may set forth two or more statements of a
claim or defense alternatively or hypothetically. A party may state as
many separate claims or defenses regardless of consistency."

This permits pleading both direct negligence and vicarious liability
against the same defendant, or negligence and strict liability for the
same incident.

### Doe Defendants

**NM permits Doe defendants.** When the identity of a responsible party
is unknown, the complaint may name "Doe" defendants.

**Relation-back:** NMRA 1-015(C) permits amendment to substitute the
true name of a Doe defendant, and the amendment relates back to the
original filing date if:
- The claim arose from the same transaction/occurrence
- The real party received notice within the service period
- The real party knew or should have known the action would have been
  brought against them but for the mistake in identity

### Tort Claims Act — Government Entity Defendants

**NMSA §§ 41-4-1 through 41-4-27 (New Mexico Tort Claims Act)**

**Notice requirement:** NMSA § 41-4-16(A) — written notice must be given
within 90 days after the occurrence giving rise to the claim. The notice
must state the time, place, and circumstances of the loss or injury.

**What the complaint must allege:**
```
Plaintiff provided timely written notice of this claim to [Entity]
on [date], in compliance with NMSA § 41-4-16(A). More than ninety
(90) days have elapsed since the giving of said notice, and [Entity]
has [denied the claim / failed to act upon the claim within the
statutory period].
```

**Filing deadline:** Within two years after the date of the occurrence
(NMSA § 41-4-15(A)), but the 90-day notice must be given first.

**Waiver of immunity:** The complaint must identify the specific waiver
of sovereign immunity that applies (NMSA §§ 41-4-5 through 41-4-12).
Common waivers for PI:
- § 41-4-6: Operation of motor vehicles, aircraft, watercraft
- § 41-4-9: Buildings, public parks, machinery, equipment
- § 41-4-10: Health care facilities
- § 41-4-11: Highways and streets
- § 41-4-12: Law enforcement (for Civil Rights Act claims)

**Damages cap:** $750,000 per occurrence for claims against government
entities (NMSA § 41-4-19(A)), with exceptions.

### NM Civil Rights Act — Law Enforcement Defendants

**NMSA §§ 41-4A-1 through 41-4A-13 (New Mexico Civil Rights Act)**

**Notice requirement:** NMSA § 41-4A-12 — written notice required before
filing suit.

**What the complaint must allege:**
- The specific constitutional right violated
- The specific conduct of the law enforcement officer
- That timely notice was provided under NMSA § 41-4A-12
- That the officer was acting under color of law

### Jury Demand

**NMRA 1-038(B):** "Any party may demand a trial by jury of any issue
triable of right by a jury by serving upon the other parties a demand
therefor in writing at any time after the commencement of the action and
not later than ten (10) days after the service of the last pleading
directed to such issue."

**Best practice:** Include the jury demand in the complaint itself.

### Damages — Ad Damnum Clause

**NM does not require** a specific dollar amount in the ad damnum clause
for PI claims. "In an amount to be proven at trial" or "in excess of
the jurisdictional minimum" is sufficient.

**Exception:** Medical malpractice — specific damages amounts may be
required under NMSA § 41-5-1 et seq.

## Federal Court — District of New Mexico (D.N.M.)

### Pleading Standard: Twombly/Iqbal Plausibility

**Rule:** FRCP 8(a)(2) — "A pleading that states a claim for relief must
contain a short and plain statement of the claim showing that the pleader
is entitled to relief."

**Standard:** Plausibility pleading under *Bell Atlantic Corp. v. Twombly*,
550 U.S. 544 (2007) and *Ashcroft v. Iqbal*, 556 U.S. 662 (2009).
[VERIFY-CITE]

**Two-step analysis:**
1. **Identify conclusory statements** — legal conclusions not entitled to
   presumption of truth
2. **Assess remaining factual allegations** — do they plausibly state a
   claim for relief?

**What this means for complaint drafting:**
- Must plead facts, not just legal labels or conclusions
- Each element of the cause of action needs factual support
- "Formulaic recitation of the elements" is insufficient
- Facts must "nudge claims across the line from conceivable to plausible"
- More specificity required than NM state notice pleading

**Practical impact on this skill:**
- Federal mode produces more detailed factual paragraphs
- Each element allegation includes specific facts (not just legal conclusion)
- The complaint self-checks against the Iqbal two-step

### Subject Matter Jurisdiction

#### Diversity Jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1332)

**Must allege:**
- **Citizenship** (not residency) of each party
  - Individuals: state of domicile
  - Corporations: state of incorporation AND principal place of business
  - LLCs: citizenship of every member
- **Complete diversity:** no plaintiff shares citizenship with any defendant
- **Amount in controversy:** exceeds $75,000, exclusive of interest and costs

**Template:**
```
¶ [#].  Plaintiff is a citizen of the State of New Mexico.

¶ [#].  Defendant [Corp] is a [State] corporation with its principal
        place of business in [State], and is therefore a citizen of
        [State(s)].

¶ [#].  Complete diversity of citizenship exists between Plaintiff
        and all Defendants.

¶ [#].  The amount in controversy exceeds $75,000, exclusive of
        interest and costs.
```

#### Federal Question Jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1331)

**Must allege:**
- The specific federal statute or constitutional provision creating
  the right of action
- Facts establishing the federal claim

**Common federal claims in PI context:**
- 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (civil rights — state actors)
- 42 U.S.C. § 1985 (conspiracy to deprive civil rights)
- FELA (Federal Employers' Liability Act)
- Jones Act
- FTCA (Federal Tort Claims Act — against federal government)

### Personal Jurisdiction

**Must allege basis for personal jurisdiction over each defendant:**
- **General jurisdiction:** Defendant domiciled in NM or "at home" in NM
  (corporations: state of incorporation or PPB)
- **Specific jurisdiction:** Defendant's contacts with NM related to the claim
  (minimum contacts + fair play)

### Venue

**28 U.S.C. § 1391(b) — Venue proper in:**
1. District where any defendant resides (if all in same state)
2. District where a substantial part of events occurred
3. If no other district, district where any defendant is subject to
   personal jurisdiction

### Jury Demand

**FRCP 38(b):** "On any issue triable of right by a jury, a party may
demand a jury trial by serving the other parties with a written demand —
which may be included in a pleading — no later than 14 days after the
last pleading directed to the issue is served."

**Best practice:** Include in the complaint. Failure to timely demand
waives the right (FRCP 38(d)).

### Doe Defendants in Federal Court

**Disfavored.** Federal courts generally do not permit Doe defendants:
- Cannot use Does to manufacture or destroy diversity
- Must identify defendants with enough specificity for service
- If identity truly unknown, request expedited discovery to identify

**If used, must explain:**
```
The true names and identities of Defendants Does 1 through [N] are
unknown to Plaintiff at this time. Plaintiff will seek to amend this
Complaint to identify these Defendants once their identities are
ascertained through discovery.
```

### FTCA — Federal Government Defendants

**Federal Tort Claims Act (28 U.S.C. §§ 1346(b), 2671-2680)**

**Administrative exhaustion required:** Must file administrative claim
with the relevant federal agency and either receive denial or wait six
months before filing suit.

**What the complaint must allege:**
- Administrative claim filed with [Agency] on [date]
- Final denial received on [date] OR six months elapsed without action
- The United States is the proper defendant (not the agency or individual)
- The specific negligent act of the government employee
- That the employee was acting within the scope of employment

## Jurisdiction Detection

**From intake_info or CFP:**
- `NM` or `State` → NM state court rules (NMRA 1-008)
- `FED`, `D.N.M.`, or `Federal` → Federal rules (FRCP 8) + D.N.M. local rules
- `TCA` or government entity defendant → Tort Claims Act requirements
- `CRA` or law enforcement defendant → Civil Rights Act requirements
- `FTCA` or federal government defendant → Federal Tort Claims Act requirements

**If jurisdiction unclear:**
Flag `[DECISION REQUIRED]` — "Court selection not specified. NM state court
is the default for NM PI claims. Federal court requires diversity of citizenship
or a federal question. Please specify the desired forum."

| Feature | NM State | Federal (D.N.M.) |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| Pleading standard | Notice | Plausibility (Twombly/Iqbal) |
| Specificity required | Fair notice | Factual plausibility |

| Venue | County where COA arose | District where events occurred |
| Doe defendants | Permitted (relation-back) | Disfavored |

| Damages amount | Not required in complaint | Amount in controversy for diversity |
| Verification | Rarely required for PI | Rarely required for PI |
| Government entity | TCA notice (90 days) | FTCA admin claim (6 months) |
| Sanctions regime | NMRA 1-011 (no safe harbor specified) | FRCP 11 (21-day safe harbor) |
