# DPW Phase 3: Valuation & Client Authority — Reference

## Purpose

The attorney evaluates the risk, establishes the "bottom line," and secures
ethical clearance from the client to make a formal offer. This phase produces
three critical deliverables:

1. **Valuation Matrix** — the attorney's assessment of case value
2. **Estimated Settlement Statement (ESS)** — a mock disbursement showing
   the client exactly how a settlement would be divided
3. **Settlement Authority Directive** — the client's written authorization
   to demand a specific amount with a defined walk-away number

Phase 3 has three steps:
- Step 5: The Valuation Matrix
- Step 6: The Estimated Settlement Statement (ESS)
- Step 7: Procure Written Client Authority

---

## Part 1: The Valuation Matrix (Step 5)

### 1.1 Purpose

The Lead Attorney reviews the compiled file (CFP, DVP, DC, DRO outputs) and
assesses the total case value across three categories of damages, factoring in
state-specific caps, multipliers, and risk adjustments.

### 1.2 Valuation Components

#### Special Damages (Hard Costs)

These are objectively verifiable economic losses:

| Category | Source | Calculation |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Past medical expenses** | DVP medical bills (gross charges) | Sum of all verified medical bills |
| **Future medical expenses** | Life care plan OR treating physician projection | Present value of future treatment costs |
| **Past lost earnings** | Wage verification + employment records | Documented wages lost from date of injury to date of demand |
| **Future lost earning capacity** | Vocational expert OR economist | Present value of diminished future earning capacity |
| **Past out-of-pocket expenses** | Receipts and documentation | All verified hard costs from Phase 2, Step 4 |
| **Future out-of-pocket expenses** | Expert projections | Ongoing assistive devices, home modifications, transportation |

#### General Damages (Human Toll)

These are subjective, non-economic damages:

| Category | Assessment Method | Key Factors |
|----------|------------------|-------------|
| **Pain and suffering** | Per diem or multiplier analysis | Duration, intensity, permanence of pain |
| **Mental anguish / emotional distress** | Client testimony + treating provider records | PTSD diagnosis, anxiety, depression, sleep disruption |
| **Loss of enjoyment of life** | Before/after comparison | Hobbies lost, activities limited, quality of life reduction |
| **Disfigurement / scarring** | Photo documentation + medical records | Visibility, permanence, age of client, social impact |
| **Loss of consortium** | Spouse testimony + counseling records | Impact on marriage, intimacy, family relationships |
| **Physical impairment** | Impairment rating + functional capacity eval | Permanent restrictions, disability percentage |
| **Loss of liberty** (Civil Rights) | Detention records + client declaration | Duration of wrongful detention, conditions |
| **Humiliation / stigma** (Civil Rights) | Client declaration + witness statements | Public nature of incident, community impact |

#### Statutory Multipliers, Caps, and Enhancements

**CRITICAL:** State-specific rules dramatically affect the demand number. The
attorney MUST apply the correct modifiers:

| Modifier Type | NM Application | Statute / Authority |
|--------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Med Mal cap** | $600,000 cap on non-economic damages (as adjusted) | NMSA §41-5-6 (Medical Malpractice Act) |
| **Punitive damages** | No statutory cap in NM; subject to due process limits | BMW v. Gore, 517 U.S. 559 (1996); State Farm v. Campbell, 538 U.S. 408 (2003) |
| **Consumer Protection treble damages** | Court SHALL award treble damages for willful UPA violations | NMSA §57-12-10(B) |
| **Consumer Protection attorney fees** | Mandatory fee-shifting to prevailing plaintiff | NMSA §57-12-10(C) |
| **Civil Rights attorney fees** | Mandatory fee-shifting under NM Civil Rights Act | NMSA §41-4A-12 |
| **Civil Rights — no QI** | Qualified immunity BANNED under NM Civil Rights Act | NMSA §41-4A-4 |
| **Pre-judgment interest** | Accrues from date of demand if demand is reasonable | NMSA §56-8-4 |
| **Wrongful death damages** | No cap; includes loss of companionship, guidance, comfort | NMSA §41-2-1 et seq. |

### 1.3 Risk Adjustment

The valuation is not just what damages are worth — it is what a jury would
likely award, discounted by litigation risk:

| Risk Factor | Impact on Valuation | How to Assess |
|------------|--------------------|-|
| **Liability strength** | Strong liability → higher demand; disputed → discount | PCM gap analysis |
| **Comparative fault** | NM pure comparative — discount by plaintiff's fault % | CFP liability facts |
| **Venue / jury pool** | Urban NM counties trend plaintiff-favorable | DRO venue analysis |
| **Prior medical history** | Pre-existing conditions reduce causation argument | CFP medical history |
| **Witness credibility** | Strong plaintiff witnesses → higher; impeachable → discount | CFP witness assessment |
| **Defendant sympathy** | Sympathetic defendant → jury may reduce award | Case-specific |
| **Insurance coverage** | Demand calibrated to available coverage layers | ICM coverage map |

## Part 2: The Estimated Settlement Statement — ESS (Step 6)

### 2.1 Purpose

The paralegal drafts a mock ESS showing the client exactly how a settlement
would be divided at the target demand amount. This is NOT a billing statement —
it is a projection tool that helps the client understand what they would
actually receive.

### 2.2 ESS Requirements

The ESS must show:
1. **Gross demand amount** — the number being demanded
2. **Attorney fees** — calculated per the retainer agreement (typically 33.3%
   pre-suit, 40% post-filing, 45% post-discovery)
3. **Case expenses** — all costs advanced by the firm (filing fees, expert fees,
   medical records costs, deposition costs, etc.)
4. **Medical liens** — all known lien amounts from Phase 2, Step 3
5. **Government liens** — Medicare, Medicaid, VA, IHS (separately itemized)
6. **Letters of Protection** — all LOP balances
7. **Net to client** — what the client actually takes home

### 2.3 ESS Template

```
ESTIMATED SETTLEMENT STATEMENT
===============================
Case: [CASE_CODE]
Client: [CLIENT_NAME]
Date: [DATE]
Prepared by: [PARALEGAL_NAME]
Reviewed by: [ATTORNEY_NAME]

THIS IS AN ESTIMATE ONLY. Actual disbursement may differ based on
final lien amounts, negotiated reductions, and settlement terms.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gross Settlement / Demand Amount:        $[AMOUNT]

Less: Attorney Fees
  [X]% per retainer agreement:          ($[AMOUNT])

Less: Case Expenses Advanced:
  Filing fees:                          ($[AMOUNT])
  Medical records:                      ($[AMOUNT])
  Expert fees:                          ($[AMOUNT])
  Deposition costs:                     ($[AMOUNT])
  Other:                                ($[AMOUNT])
  TOTAL EXPENSES:                       ($[AMOUNT])

Less: Medical Liens:
  Medicare (MSPRC):                     ($[AMOUNT])
  Medicaid (NM HSD):                    ($[AMOUNT])
  [Health Plan]:                        ($[AMOUNT])
  TOTAL MEDICAL LIENS:                  ($[AMOUNT])

Less: Letters of Protection:
  Dr. [Name]:                           ($[AMOUNT])
  [Facility]:                           ($[AMOUNT])
  TOTAL LOPs:                           ($[AMOUNT])

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

If settled at walk-away number ($[AMOUNT]):
  Estimated Net to Client:               $[AMOUNT]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

### 2.4 ESS Preparation Rules

1. **Show two scenarios:** ESS at the demand amount AND ESS at the walk-away
   number, so the client understands the range of likely outcomes.
2. **Use current lien balances:** Update all lien amounts from Phase 2 before
   drafting the ESS. Stale numbers mislead the client.
3. **Note uncertainty:** If any lien balance is estimated (e.g., Medicare MSPRC
   has not yet provided a final conditional payment letter), clearly mark it
   as "ESTIMATED — Final amount pending."
4. **Include fee tier:** If the fee percentage changes based on case stage
   (pre-suit vs. post-filing), show the applicable tier.
5. **Do not promise:** The ESS header must include a disclaimer that this is
   an estimate only and actual disbursement may differ.

## Part 3: Written Client Authority (Step 7)

### 3.1 Ethical Requirement

**Strict Ethical Rule: Never send a demand without the client's written
authorization.** This is both an ethical obligation and a malpractice shield.

Under NM Rules of Professional Conduct:
- **Rule 16-102(A):** The lawyer shall abide by the client's decisions
  concerning the objectives of representation, including whether to settle.
- **Rule 16-104(A):** The lawyer shall promptly inform the client of any
  settlement offer and explain the matter sufficiently for the client to make
  an informed decision.
- **Rule 16-104(B):** The lawyer shall explain a matter to the extent reasonably
  necessary for the client to make informed decisions.

### 3.2 Settlement Authority Directive

The attorney reviews the ESS with the client (in person, by phone, or by video —
never just by email) and executes a Settlement Authority Directive that:

1. **Authorizes the demand amount:** "I authorize the firm to demand $X."
2. **Establishes the walk-away number:** "My minimum acceptable settlement
   is $X."
3. **Acknowledges lien obligations:** "I understand that medical liens
   totaling approximately $X must be satisfied from any settlement."
4. **Acknowledges the ESS is an estimate:** "I understand the estimated
   net to me is approximately $X and may change."
5. **Is signed and dated by the client.**

### 3.3 Settlement Authority Directive Template

```
SETTLEMENT AUTHORITY DIRECTIVE
===============================
Case: [CASE_CODE]
Client: [CLIENT_NAME]
Date: [DATE]

I, [CLIENT_NAME], have reviewed the Estimated Settlement Statement
prepared by [FIRM_NAME] dated [ESS_DATE] for my claim against
[DEFENDANT(S)].

I understand:
1. The firm recommends demanding $[DEMAND_AMOUNT] from
   [INSURER/DEFENDANT].
2. My estimated net recovery at this demand amount, after attorney
   fees, costs, and lien satisfaction, is approximately $[NET_AMOUNT].
3. Medical liens totaling approximately $[LIEN_TOTAL] must be
   satisfied from any settlement proceeds.
4. The estimated amounts may change based on final lien calculations
   and negotiation outcomes.

I HEREBY AUTHORIZE [FIRM_NAME] to:
  ☐ Demand $[DEMAND_AMOUNT] on my behalf
  ☐ Negotiate settlement of my claim
  ☐ My minimum acceptable settlement ("walk-away") is $[WALK_AWAY]

If negotiations reach impasse, I understand the firm will recommend
filing a lawsuit, which I will discuss with my attorney before
proceeding.

_________________________________    _______________
Client Signature                      Date

_________________________________    _______________
Attorney Signature                    Date

[DECISION REQUIRED: Client must review ESS and sign this directive
before DPW proceeds to Phase 4]
```

### 3.4 Client Conference Checklist

Before requesting the client's signature, the attorney must discuss:

| Topic | Discussion Point |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Case strength** | Honest assessment of liability and damages strength |
| **Demand rationale** | Why this demand number (reference DRO anchoring analysis) |
| **Expected range** | Realistic settlement range (not just the demand number) |
| **Walk-away reasoning** | Why the recommended walk-away is appropriate |
| **ESS breakdown** | Line-by-line review of fees, costs, liens, and net |
| **Timeline** | Expected response time from adjuster/defense counsel |
| **If demand fails** | Next steps — lawsuit filing, costs, timeline |
| **Risks** | Litigation risks if case goes to trial |
| **Liens** | Explanation of mandatory lien obligations |
| **Client questions** | Answer ALL client questions before requesting signature |

### 3.5 Special Authority Situations

| Situation | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Client is a minor** | Guardian ad litem or next friend must sign. Court approval may be required for settlement. |
| **Client is incapacitated** | Legal guardian or conservator must sign. Court approval typically required. |
| **Multiple clients** | Each client signs a separate authority directive for their individual claim. |
| **Wrongful death** | Personal representative of the estate signs. All statutory beneficiaries should be notified. |
| **Client unreachable** | Do NOT send the demand. Document all attempts to reach the client. Wait. |

---

## Part 4: Phase 3 Completion Gate

Phase 3 is complete when ALL three steps are done:

| Step | Pass Criteria |
|------|--------------|
| Valuation Matrix (Step 5) | Attorney has reviewed file and established Low/Mid/High ranges |
| ESS (Step 6) | Mock disbursement drafted at demand and walk-away levels |
| Client Authority (Step 7) | Client has signed Settlement Authority Directive |

**If client authority is not obtained:** DPW CANNOT proceed to Phase 4.
This is an inviolable ethical gate. No exceptions.
