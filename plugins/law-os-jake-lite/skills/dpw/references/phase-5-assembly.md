# DPW Phase 5: Assembly & Transmission — Reference

## Purpose

Deliver a professional, unassailable demand package that leaves the defense with
no excuses for delay. Phase 5 transforms the drafted demand letter and supporting
evidence into a polished, navigable digital demand brochure — then transmits it
via dual methods that legally prove receipt for bad faith deadline enforcement.

Phase 5 has two steps:
- Step 11: The Digital Demand Brochure
- Step 12: Final Quality Control & Transmission

---

## Part 1: The Digital Demand Brochure (Step 11)

### 1.1 Bates Stamping Protocol

**Every exhibit in the demand package must be Bates-stamped** with sequential
identification numbers. Bates stamping serves three purposes:
1. Creates a verifiable record of what was transmitted
2. Enables precise cross-referencing from the demand letter to exhibits
3. Prevents the adjuster from claiming exhibits were "missing" or "incomplete"

**Bates numbering convention:**

| Exhibit Type | Prefix | Example Range |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| Medical records | MEDS_ | MEDS_0001 – MEDS_0450 |
| Medical bills | BILLS_ | BILLS_0001 – BILLS_0075 |
| Imaging / radiology | IMG_ | IMG_0001 – IMG_0025 |
| Expert reports | EXPERT_ | EXPERT_0001 – EXPERT_0020 |
| Photographs (injury) | PHOTO_ | PHOTO_0001 – PHOTO_0040 |
| Photographs (property) | PROP_ | PROP_0001 – PROP_0015 |
| Wage / employment records | WAGE_ | WAGE_0001 – WAGE_0010 |
| Government records (IPRA/FOIA) | GOV_ | GOV_0001 – GOV_0200 |
| Video / bodycam | VIDEO_ | VIDEO_0001 – VIDEO_0005 |
| Correspondence | CORR_ | CORR_0001 – CORR_0030 |
| Other exhibits | EX_ | EX_0001 – EX_0050 |

**Bates stamping rules:**
- Sequential within each prefix category
- Stamp appears on the lower-right corner of each page
- Stamp is visible but does not obscure document content
- Multi-page documents receive continuous numbering (e.g., a 15-page ER record
  is MEDS_0001 through MEDS_0015)
- Include a master Bates index as an exhibit appendix

### 1.3 Hyperlinking Protocol

**When the demand letter mentions a crucial finding, link that sentence directly
to the Bates-stamped exhibit.** The reader should be able to click from any
factual assertion in the demand to the supporting evidence.

**Implementation:**

| Demand Letter Reference | Hyperlink Target |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| "The MRI dated [DATE] revealed a disc herniation at L4-L5..." | Link to IMG_0001 (MRI report page) |
| "Dr. [NAME] confirmed the standard of care was breached..." | Link to EXPERT_0001 (affidavit first page) |
| "The bodycam footage at timestamp [XX:XX] shows..." | Link to VIDEO_0001 (or specific timestamp reference) |
| "Medical expenses totaling $[AMOUNT]..." | Link to BILLS_0001 (billing summary) |
| "Ms. [NAME] endured three epidural steroid injections..." | Link to MEDS_0XXX (injection procedure notes) |

**Hyperlinking rules:**
- Every factual assertion in Sections 2-5 of the demand letter should have at
  least one hyperlink to a supporting exhibit
- Use descriptive hyperlink text (not "click here")
- Test all hyperlinks before transmission
- In the print version (certified mail), include parenthetical Bates references:
  "(See MEDS_0026 at p.3)" instead of hyperlinks

### 1.4 Visual Integration

**Embed compelling visuals directly into the body of the demand letter.** Do not
relegate them to an appendix where the adjuster may never look. The reader
should SEE the damage while reading about it.

| Visual Type | Where to Embed | Purpose |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **Property damage photos** | Section 2 (Liability Narrative) | Show the force of impact / severity of incident |
| **Injury photographs** | Section 3 (Medical Chronology) — at the relevant treatment stage | Show the human cost of the defendant's conduct |
| **Surgical scar photos** | Section 3 — after describing the surgery | Make the permanent disfigurement visible |
| **X-rays showing hardware** | Section 3 — after describing the surgical intervention | Show metal rods, screws, plates in the body |
| **MRI images** | Section 3 — after describing the diagnostic findings | Show disc herniations, tears, structural damage |
| **Bodycam stills** | Section 2 (for civil rights) — at the key moments | Show the constitutional violation happening |
| **Before/after photos** | Section 4 (Human Toll) | Show quality of life impact |

**Visual embedding rules:**
- Images must be high-resolution and clearly labeled
- Include a brief caption below each image: "[PHOTO_0003: Surgical scar,
  left knee, 6 weeks post-operation]"
- Do not use gratuitously graphic images — impactful but professional
- Ensure images are appropriately sized (do not distort aspect ratios)
- Cross-reference each embedded image to its Bates-stamped full-resolution
  exhibit in the appendix

## Part 2: Final Quality Control & Transmission (Step 12)

### 2.1 Quality Control Checklist

The Lead Attorney performs a final review before transmission. The following
checklist must be completed with ALL items passing:

| # | QC Check | How to Verify | Status |
|---|----------|--------------|--------|
| 1 | **Typos and grammar** | Full read-through of demand letter body | PASS/FAIL |
| 2 | **Tone appropriate** | Professional, firm, not threatening; suitable for mediator/judge review | PASS/FAIL |
| 3 | **Mathematical accuracy** | Verify every dollar amount: medical bills total, lien amounts, lost wages, demand number. Cross-check against DVP/DC output. | PASS/FAIL |
| 4 | **Demand amount matches authority** | Confirm demand amount matches the amount authorized in the Settlement Authority Directive (Phase 3, Step 7) | PASS/FAIL |
| 5 | **Client name / case details correct** | Verify all names, dates, claim numbers, policy numbers | PASS/FAIL |
| 6 | **All exhibits Bates-stamped** | Verify sequential Bates numbers; no gaps; master index complete | PASS/FAIL |
| 7 | **Hyperlinks functional** | Click-test every hyperlink in the digital version | PASS/FAIL |
| 8 | **Visuals embedded correctly** | Images display properly; captions accurate; no distortion | PASS/FAIL |
| 9 | **Bad faith trigger language included** | Verify language per Phase 4, Step 10 requirements | PASS/FAIL |
| 10 | **Deadline date specified** | Confirm specific date + time + timezone; calculate from send date | PASS/FAIL |
| 11 | **Enclosures list complete** | Every exhibit in the appendix is listed in the enclosures list | PASS/FAIL |
| 12 | **Merge tokens resolved** | No unresolved merge tokens (e.g., [CLIENT_NAME]) remain in the document | PASS/FAIL |
| 13 | **SDC posture compliance** | No UNDISCLOSED items revealed without SDC authorization | PASS/FAIL |
| 14 | **Client authority on file** | Signed Settlement Authority Directive in the case file | PASS/FAIL |
| 15 | **Lien obligations noted** | All known liens accounted for in the settlement structure | PASS/FAIL |

### 2.2 Common QC Failures

**Mathematical errors are the #1 reason adjusters delay responses.** Common
errors to check:

| Error Type | How It Happens | How to Prevent |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Medical bills don't sum** | Individual provider amounts don't add to stated total | Verify with calculator; cross-check against DVP |
| **Wrong fee percentage** | Used pre-suit rate but case is in litigation | Check retainer agreement fee tier |
| **Lien amount changed** | Used stale lien balance; lien holder updated amount | Re-confirm all lien balances within 7 days of sending |
| **Lost wages math error** | Weekly rate × weeks doesn't equal stated total | Verify wage calculation independently |
| **Demand exceeds policy limits** | Demanded $500K against a $100K policy (may be intentional for excess claim, but must be deliberate) | Verify against ICM policy limits |

### 2.3 Transmission Protocol

The demand package must be transmitted via **two methods simultaneously:**

#### Method 1: Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested (USPS)

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Why certified mail** | Legally proves receipt for bad faith deadline enforcement. The green card (return receipt) is evidence that the carrier received the demand on a specific date. |
| **Addressed to** | The assigned claims adjuster by name, at the carrier's claims office address. Also send a copy to defense counsel if counsel has appeared. |
| **Contents** | Full demand letter + all exhibits (printed version with Bates references instead of hyperlinks) |
| **Tracking** | Record the USPS tracking number in the case file and CAL system |
| **Return receipt** | When the green card is returned, scan it and add to the case file. This is critical evidence for bad faith claims. |

#### Method 2: Electronic Delivery

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Email** | Send to the adjuster's email address AND the adjuster's supervisor's email (if known). CC defense counsel if applicable. |
| **Subject line** | "FORMAL DEMAND — [Client Name] v. [Insured Name] — Claim #[NUMBER] — Response Required by [DEADLINE DATE]" |
| **Attachments** | Full demand letter (PDF) + all exhibits (PDF). If the package exceeds email size limits, use a secure file-sharing link. |
| **Read receipt** | Request read receipt on the email. |
| **Portal** | If the carrier uses an electronic claims portal (e.g., Verisk, ISO), upload the demand through the portal as well. |

### 2.4 Post-Transmission Actions

Immediately after transmission:
1. **Record in CMS:** Log the demand as sent with date, method, and tracking info
2. **Docket deadline:** Enter the response deadline in CAL:
   - Event: DEMAND-DEADLINE
   - Date: [Deadline date from demand letter]
   - Alert: 5 days before deadline
3. **Docket nudge:** Enter the 15-day nudge in CAL:
   - Event: DEMAND-NUDGE
   - Date: 15 days after transmission
   - Alert: Day of
4. **Docket expiration:** Enter the drop-dead date:
   - Event: DEMAND-EXPIRATION
   - Date: Deadline date
   - Alert: Day of, with escalation to Lead Attorney
5. **Confirm with client:** Send the client a brief communication confirming
   the demand was sent, the amount demanded, and the deadline for response
6. **File the certified mail receipt** and tracking number in the case file
