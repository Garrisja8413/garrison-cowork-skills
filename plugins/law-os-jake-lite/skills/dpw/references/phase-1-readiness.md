# DPW Phase 1: Demand Readiness — Reference

## Purpose

Ensure the case file is completely mature before asking for money. Premature
demands leave money on the table. Demanding with missing records gives adjusters
an excuse to delay. This phase prevents both failure modes by enforcing
practice-area-specific readiness triggers.

The Case Manager moves a file into "Demand Prep" only when one of the defined
triggers is met. Until a trigger fires, the file stays in active case management.

---

## Part 1: Practice-Area Readiness Triggers

### 1.1 Personal Injury & Premises Liability — The MMI Trigger

**Trigger:** The client reaches Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI) and is released
from care, OR a formal Life Care Plan for future medicals is finalized by an
expert.

**Why MMI matters:** Demanding before MMI means the demand under-values future
treatment. Adjusters will argue the claimant's condition may improve, justifying
a lower offer. Waiting for MMI eliminates this argument and locks in the full
scope of past and future damages.

**Evaluation criteria:**

| Criterion | How to Verify | Status Values |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Treating physician has released patient from active care | Discharge letter / final visit note | MET / NOT MET |
| Patient is not scheduled for future surgeries or procedures | Treatment plan review | MET / NOT MET |
| Permanent impairment rating issued (if applicable) | IME or treating physician report | MET / NOT MET / N/A |
| Life Care Plan finalized (for catastrophic injuries) | Expert life care planner report | MET / NOT MET / N/A |
| All referrals completed | Medical records cross-reference | MET / NOT MET |

**Fast-track exception:** If MMI is not reached but the statute of limitations is
within 6 months, demand immediately and note in the demand that treatment is
ongoing and future damages will be supplemented.

### 1.2 Personal Injury & Med Mal — The Limits Trigger

**Trigger:** The known medical bills (e.g., $150,000) drastically exceed the
known insurance policy limits (e.g., $25,000). Do not wait for MMI — demand
the policy limits immediately.

**Why this overrides MMI:** When damages clearly exceed policy limits, waiting
for MMI provides no additional leverage. The carrier's exposure is capped at
policy limits regardless of how high the damages climb. Immediate demand:
- Recovers money for the client faster
- Begins the bad faith clock (carrier must respond reasonably under NM UCP Act)
- Preserves excess/umbrella policy claims
- Frees the attorney to pursue the tortfeasor personally or find additional coverage

**Evaluation criteria:**

| Criterion | How to Verify | Status Values |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Known medical bills exceed 3× policy limits | DVP medical bills total vs. ICM policy limits | MET / NOT MET |
| Policy limits confirmed via ICM or dec page | Insurance coverage declaration page | MET / NOT MET |
| No known excess/umbrella policy | ICM excess/umbrella search complete | MET / NOT MET / UNKNOWN |
| Liability is clear or very strong | CFP liability assessment + LPB duty/breach analysis | MET / NOT MET |

**If umbrella/excess exists:** Do NOT fast-track. Full demand preparation is
required to maximize recovery across all layers of coverage.

### 1.3 Medical Malpractice — The Expert Affidavit Trigger

**Trigger:** An out-of-state Board-Certified expert has provided a favorable
written affidavit confirming the standard of care was breached and caused the
injury.

**Why the expert affidavit is mandatory:** Med mal claims are expert-dependent.
Without a credible expert opinion, the demand has no teeth. An out-of-state
board-certified expert provides maximum credibility because:
- No local bias or fear of retaliation from the defendant doctor/hospital
- Board certification demonstrates unimpeachable subject-matter expertise
- Written affidavit is a pre-litigation exhibit that signals the firm is ready
  to file a Medical Review Commission application

**Evaluation criteria:**

| Criterion | How to Verify | Status Values |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Expert is board-certified in the relevant specialty | CV + board certification verification | MET / NOT MET |
| Expert is licensed out-of-state (no local entanglements) | License verification | MET / NOT MET |
| Written affidavit addresses standard of care | Affidavit text review | MET / NOT MET |
| Affidavit establishes breach of standard | Affidavit text review | MET / NOT MET |
| Affidavit establishes causation to a reasonable medical probability | Affidavit text review | MET / NOT MET |
| Affidavit identifies the specific injuries caused | Affidavit text review | MET / NOT MET |

### 1.4 Civil Rights / Human Rights — The Records & Resolution Trigger

**Trigger:** All IPRA/FOIA public records and bodycam footage are received, the
underlying criminal case (if any) is resolved to clear Heck v. Humphrey hurdles,
and economic/emotional damages are calculated.

**Why all three sub-triggers must be met:**

- **IPRA/FOIA records:** The government defendant's own records are the most
  powerful evidence. Without bodycam footage, internal affairs files, and
  policy/training records, the demand cannot paint the full picture of
  institutional negligence or constitutional malice.

- **Heck v. Humphrey clearance:** If the plaintiff has an underlying criminal
  case, Heck v. Humphrey (512 U.S. 477 (1994)) bars §1983 claims that would
  necessarily imply the invalidity of the criminal conviction. The criminal
  case must be resolved (acquittal, dismissal, or favorable plea) before the
  civil rights demand can proceed. Under the NM Civil Rights Act (NMSA
  §41-4A-1 et seq.), qualified immunity is banned — but Heck still applies to
  federal §1983 claims and analogous state claims.

- **Damages calculation:** Civil rights damages include economic losses (medical,
  lost wages) plus emotional distress, humiliation, and loss of liberty. These
  must be calculated before the demand can state a number.

**Evaluation criteria:**

| Criterion | How to Verify | Status Values |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| IPRA/FOIA request submitted | Request letter on file | MET / NOT MET |
| IPRA/FOIA response received (complete) | Response package reviewed | MET / NOT MET |
| Bodycam footage received and reviewed | Video files in case file | MET / NOT MET / N/A |
| Internal affairs files received (if applicable) | IA response on file | MET / NOT MET / N/A |
| Criminal case resolved (if applicable) | Court records / docket check | MET / NOT MET / N/A |
| Heck v. Humphrey analysis completed | Attorney memo on file | MET / NOT MET / N/A |
| Economic damages calculated | DC output available | MET / NOT MET |
| Emotional distress damages assessed | Client declaration / expert report | MET / NOT MET |

### 1.5 Consumer Protection — The Forensic Audit Trigger

**Trigger:** The forensic audit of the fraudulent contracts is complete, and the
statutory pre-suit notice period is ready to be triggered.

**Why both sub-triggers must be met:**

- **Forensic audit:** Consumer protection claims (UPA, TILA, RESPA, FDCPA)
  require precise identification of the fraudulent or deceptive acts. A forensic
  audit of the contracts, disclosures, and transaction history identifies every
  statutory violation — each of which may carry its own damages multiplier or
  statutory penalty.

- **Pre-suit notice:** Many consumer protection statutes (including the NM
  Unfair Practices Act, NMSA §57-12-10) require a pre-suit demand/notice giving
  the defendant an opportunity to cure. The demand letter itself may serve as
  this notice, but it must be drafted with the statutory notice requirements in
  mind and the notice period must be calendared.

**Evaluation criteria:**

| Criterion | How to Verify | Status Values |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Forensic contract audit complete | Audit report on file | MET / NOT MET |
| All statutory violations identified | Audit report cross-referenced with LPB | MET / NOT MET |
| Actual damages calculated | DC output for each violation | MET / NOT MET |
| Statutory multipliers identified | LPB treble damages / penalty provisions | MET / NOT MET |
| Pre-suit notice requirements identified | LPB notice provisions | MET / NOT MET |
| Notice period calendared | CAL entry for notice expiration | MET / NOT MET |

## Part 2: Readiness Assessment Process

### Step 1: Identify Practice Area

Determine the case's primary practice area from the case record. If the case
spans multiple practice areas (e.g., a civil rights case with medical
malpractice elements), evaluate against ALL applicable triggers. The most
restrictive trigger set controls.

### Step 2: Evaluate Each Trigger Criterion

For each criterion in the applicable trigger set:
1. Check the case file for the required documentation/evidence
2. Mark each criterion as MET, NOT MET, or N/A
3. For NOT MET criteria, identify the specific gap action needed

### Step 3: Determine Readiness Verdict

| Verdict | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **READY** | All required criteria are MET (N/A criteria excluded) |
| **NOT READY** | One or more required criteria are NOT MET |
| **FAST-TRACK** | Limits Trigger met — skip to Phase 3 for policy limits demand |
| **READY WITH EXCEPTIONS** | Attorney overrides NOT MET criteria with documented justification |

### Step 4: Generate Gap Action Items

For each NOT MET criterion, generate a specific, actionable item:
- What is missing
- Who is responsible for obtaining it
- Estimated time to obtain
- Which downstream skill produces it (if applicable)

## Part 3: Fast-Track Protocol (Policy Limits Demand)

When the Limits Trigger is met, the standard DPW workflow compresses:

| Standard Phase | Fast-Track Equivalent |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Phase 1: READINESS | Limits Trigger confirmed |
| Phase 2: AUDIT | Abbreviated — verify medical bills total and policy limits only |
| Phase 3: AUTHORITY | ESS at policy limits + client authority for limits demand |
| Phase 4: DRAFT | Route to Demand skill in POLICY-LIMITS-DEMAND mode |
| Phase 5: ASSEMBLE | Abbreviated package — key medical records and bills only |
| Phase 6: ESCALATE | Shortened deadline (15 days typical for policy limits) |

The fast-track does NOT skip client authority (Phase 3, Step 7). The client
must always authorize the demand amount, even when it equals policy limits.
