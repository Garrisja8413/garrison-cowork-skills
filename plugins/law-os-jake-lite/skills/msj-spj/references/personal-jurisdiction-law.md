## Minimum Contacts / Purposeful Direction Micro-Propositions

| MicroProp_ID | Proposition | Expected Authority | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| MC-01 | Due process requires that defendant have "minimum contacts" with the forum state | Int'l Shoe Co. v. Washington, 326 U.S. 310, 316 (1945) | Foundational |
| MC-02 | The contacts must be such that maintenance of the suit does not offend traditional notions of fair play and substantial justice | Int'l Shoe, 326 U.S. at 316 | |
| MC-03 | The defendant must have purposefully directed activities at residents of the forum | Burger King Corp. v. Rudzewicz, 471 U.S. 462, 472 (1985) | |
| MC-04 | Purposeful availment ensures defendant's conduct creates a substantial connection with the forum state | Burger King, 471 U.S. at 475 | |
| MC-05 | Random, fortuitous, or attenuated contacts do not support jurisdiction | Burger King, 471 U.S. at 475 | |
| MC-06 | Unilateral activity of another party or third person is not attributable to the defendant | Helicopteros Nacionales de Colombia, S.A. v. Hall, 466 U.S. 408, 417 (1984) | |

## Calder Effects Test Micro-Propositions (Intentional Torts)

| MicroProp_ID | Proposition | Expected Authority | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| CALDER-01 | For intentional torts, jurisdiction exists where defendant: (1) committed an intentional act, (2) expressly aimed at the forum state, (3) causing harm defendant knew would be felt in the forum | Calder v. Jones, 465 U.S. 783, 788-89 (1984) | |
| CALDER-02 | Mere knowledge that plaintiff resides in the forum, without more, is insufficient | Walden v. Fiore, 571 U.S. 277, 289 (2014) | |
| CALDER-03 | The focus must be on defendant's contacts with the forum, not plaintiff's connections to the forum | Walden, 571 U.S. at 284 | Critical |

| MicroProp_ID | Proposition | Expected Authority | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
