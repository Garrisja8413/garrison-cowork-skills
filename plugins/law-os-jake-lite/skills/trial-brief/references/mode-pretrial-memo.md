# TRIAL-BRIEF — Pretrial Memorandum Format and Requirements

## Purpose

This reference defines the pretrial memorandum format for the TRIAL-BRIEF
skill. A pretrial memorandum is a structured document filed in advance of
trial that addresses specific topics required by the court's scheduling
order or local rule. It differs from a standard trial brief in that it
follows a court-mandated format and covers logistics as well as substance.

## When to Use Pretrial Memo Mode

Use this format when:
- The court's scheduling order specifically requires a "pretrial memorandum"
  or "pretrial statement"
- The court uses the term "trial memorandum" in a scheduling context
  (as distinct from a substantive trial brief)
- Local Rule or District Court Form requires specific pretrial disclosures
- A joint pretrial order is being prepared with opposing counsel

## Pretrial Memorandum vs. Standard Trial Brief

| Feature | Standard Trial Brief | Pretrial Memorandum |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Purpose | Persuasive argument on the merits | Informational disclosure and trial logistics |
| Tone | Advocacy / persuasive | Neutral / informational (some advocacy in legal issues section) |
| Audience | Judge (substantive review) | Judge (case management) |
| Format | Attorney's discretion (within NML) | Court-prescribed sections |
| Filing | Per scheduling order or courtesy | Per scheduling order (mandatory deadlines) |
| Joint? | No (each side files separately) | Sometimes joint (joint pretrial order) |

### Required by NMRA 1-016.1 (Pretrial Procedures)

conferences and orders. The court may require parties to submit pretrial

**Section-by-Section Template**:

```
PLAINTIFF'S PRETRIAL MEMORANDUM

1. NATURE OF THE ACTION

[Brief statement of claims and defenses remaining for trial]
[Causes of action: list with statutory/common law basis]
[Defenses raised: list key affirmative defenses]

2. BASIS FOR JURISDICTION AND VENUE

[Jurisdictional basis — NM state court subject matter jurisdiction]
[Venue — county where filed and basis per NMSA 38-3-1]

3. STIPULATED FACTS

The parties stipulate to the following facts:
[List all facts agreed upon by both sides]
[If none: "The parties have not reached agreement on stipulated facts.
Plaintiff proposes the following stipulations: [list]"]

4. CONTESTED ISSUES OF FACT

The following factual issues remain contested:
a. [Issue 1 — e.g., "Whether defendant ran the red light"]
b. [Issue 2 — e.g., "The extent of plaintiff's future medical needs"]
c. [Issue 3]

5. CONTESTED ISSUES OF LAW

The following legal issues remain contested:
a. [Issue 1 — e.g., "Whether negligence per se applies"]
b. [Issue 2 — e.g., "Whether punitive damages are warranted"]
c. [Issue 3]

6. PLAINTIFF'S EXHIBIT LIST

| Ex. # | Description | Bates Range | Objection Status |
|-------|-------------|------------|------------------|
| 1 | Police Report | 0001-0005 | No objection expected |
| 2 | Medical Records — [Provider] | 0100-0250 | Hearsay objection anticipated |

[Note: Exhibits must be pre-marked per court order]

7. PLAINTIFF'S WITNESS LIST

| # | Name | Role | Estimated Time | Topics |
|---|------|------|---------------|--------|
| 1 | [Plaintiff name] | Party | 2 hours | Incident, injuries, damages |
| 2 | [Treating physician] | Expert | 1 hour | Causation, treatment, prognosis |

[For each witness: whether testimony is live or by deposition designation]

8. DEPOSITION DESIGNATIONS

[List depositions to be offered at trial with page:line designations]
[Status of counter-designations from opposing party]

9. PROPOSED JURY INSTRUCTIONS

[Reference to separately filed proposed jury instructions]
[List of contested instructions with brief argument for each]
[Cross-reference to JURY-INSTRUCTIONS skill output]

10. PROPOSED VERDICT FORM

[Reference to separately filed proposed verdict form]
[If contested: identify specific verdict form questions in dispute]

11. MOTIONS IN LIMINE

[Status of all pending motions in limine]
[Summary of any motions to be filed]

| MIL # | Topic | Filed By | Status | Ruling |
|-------|-------|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | [Topic] | Plaintiff | Pending | — |
| 2 | [Topic] | Defendant | Pending | — |

12. ESTIMATED TRIAL TIME

Plaintiff estimates [X] days for trial.
Plaintiff's case-in-chief: [X] days
Defendant's case (estimated): [X] days
Rebuttal: [X] hours

13. SETTLEMENT STATUS

[Required in many courts]
[General statement — e.g., "The parties have been unable to reach
settlement. Plaintiff's last demand was [amount/general range].
Defendant's last offer was [amount/general range]."]
[Or: "Settlement discussions are ongoing."]

14. SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS

[AV equipment needs]
[Interpreter requirements]
[Accessibility accommodations]
[Sequestration of witnesses requested: YES/NO]
[Bench copies of exhibits required by court: YES/NO]
```

## D.N.M. (Federal) Pretrial Memorandum

### Required by D.N.M. Local Rule 16.3

The U.S. District Court for the District of New Mexico has specific pretrial
order requirements under Local Rule 16.3. `[VERIFY-CITE]`

**Key Differences from State Court**:

1. **Joint Pretrial Order**: D.N.M. typically requires a joint pretrial
   order signed by all counsel, not separate memoranda.
2. **Format**: The court provides a form pretrial order that must be used.
3. **Deadline**: Usually 30 days before trial (check scheduling order).
4. **Contents**: Similar to state court but includes:
   - Jurisdictional statement (federal question or diversity)
   - Amendments to pleadings (if any)
   - Witness and exhibit lists with objections noted
   - Stipulations (all agreed facts)
   - Contested issues (fact and law)
   - Proposed findings of fact and conclusions of law (bench trial only)

**D.N.M. Joint Pretrial Order Template**:

```
JOINT PRETRIAL ORDER

I. JURISDICTIONAL STATEMENT
[Federal question / diversity / supplemental jurisdiction]

II. NATURE OF ACTION
[Joint statement of claims and defenses]

III. UNCONTESTED FACTS
[All facts agreed upon — these are binding stipulations]

IV. CONTESTED ISSUES OF FACT
A. Plaintiff's Contested Facts: [list]
B. Defendant's Contested Facts: [list]

V. CONTESTED ISSUES OF LAW
A. Plaintiff's Legal Contentions: [list with authority]
B. Defendant's Legal Contentions: [list with authority]

VI. PLAINTIFF'S EXHIBITS
[List with objection column]

VII. DEFENDANT'S EXHIBITS
[List with objection column]

VIII. PLAINTIFF'S WITNESSES
[List with expected testimony summary]

IX. DEFENDANT'S WITNESSES
[List with expected testimony summary]

X. JURY INSTRUCTIONS
[Status of proposed instructions — agreement/disagreement]

XI. ESTIMATED TRIAL TIME
[Joint estimate]

XII. POSSIBILITY OF SETTLEMENT
[Joint statement]
```

## Pack Integration for Pretrial Memoranda

### CFP PACKET Usage
- Stipulated facts: Extract facts both sides can agree on (high confidence,
  documentary support, no dispute)
- Contested fact issues: Extract facts where the parties disagree
  (use CFP rows with conflicting defense positions)
- Exhibit list: Use CFP exhibit references
- Witness testimony summaries: Use CFP witness entries

### LPB PACKET Usage
- Legal issues: Extract contested legal questions from LPB
- Jury instruction disputes: Cross-reference with JURY-INSTRUCTIONS output
- Foundation and admissibility: Use LPB evidentiary rule entries

### PCM Usage
- Element mapping informs the contested issues of fact (which elements
  are disputed vs. undisputed)
- Gap analysis identifies areas where stipulation would help

### DC Usage
- Damages categories inform the settlement status section
- Expert witness disclosures tie to DC calculations

## Common Pitfalls in Pretrial Memoranda

1. **Missing exhibits**: Exhibits not listed in the pretrial memorandum
   may be excluded at trial. Include every exhibit, even if objection
   is anticipated.
2. **Vague witness summaries**: "Will testify about the incident" is
   insufficient. Specify expected testimony topics so the court and
   opposing counsel can prepare.
3. **Overlooking stipulations**: Failing to propose stipulations wastes
   trial time on undisputed facts. Proactively identify facts suitable
   for stipulation.
4. **Inconsistent exhibit numbers**: Exhibit numbers in the pretrial memo
   must match the actual exhibit markings. Use consistent numbering.
5. **Missing deadlines**: Pretrial memorandum deadlines are firm. Late
   filing can result in sanctions or continuance. Flag the deadline
   prominently.
6. **Settlement status oversharing**: Do not disclose specific settlement
   offers unless required by the court. Use general ranges if permitted.

## Output Adjustments for Pretrial Memo Mode

When producing a pretrial memorandum instead of a standard trial brief:

1. **Tone shift**: Reduce advocacy; increase informational clarity
2. **Format compliance**: Follow the court's prescribed format exactly
3. **Joint order awareness**: If producing for a joint pretrial order,
   identify sections that require opposing counsel input as
   `[OPPOSING COUNSEL INPUT REQUIRED]`
4. **Deadline prominence**: Include the filing deadline in the header
5. **Exhibit/witness completeness**: These lists are binding -- ensure
   nothing is omitted
6. **BODY-ONLY still applies**: SA shell provides caption and signature;
   the skill provides the substantive content sections
