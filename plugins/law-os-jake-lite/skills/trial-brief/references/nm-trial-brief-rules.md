# NM Trial Brief Filing Requirements and Formatting Rules

## Purpose

This reference consolidates New Mexico-specific rules governing trial brief
filing, formatting, and content requirements for both NM state courts and
the U.S. District Court for the District of New Mexico (D.N.M.). The
TRIAL-BRIEF skill must comply with these rules in all outputs.

### Filing Requirements

1. **Timing**: Trial briefs are typically due per the court's scheduling
   order or pretrial order. Common deadlines:
   - 10-14 days before trial (most NM districts)
   - 30 days before trial (some complex cases)
   - Per specific court order (check every case)

2. **Service**: Serve on all parties per NMRA 1-005.
   - Electronic service if parties are registered for e-filing
   - Mail service requires additional time (3 days per NMRA 1-006(E))

3. **Filing Method**:
   - E-filing via Odyssey File & Serve (most NM courts)
   - Paper filing in courts not yet on e-filing system
   - Courtesy bench copy may be required — check judge preferences

4. **Certificate of Service**: Required on all filings.
   (Provided by SA shell — not part of BODY-ONLY output)

### Formatting Requirements (NMRA 1-007.1)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Paper size | 8.5 x 11 inches (letter) |
| Margins | 1 inch on all sides |
| Font | Times New Roman or similar serif font, 12 point |
| Spacing | Double-spaced body text; single-spaced block quotes and footnotes |
| Page numbers | Bottom center or bottom right |
| Line numbers | Not required in NM state court (unlike some federal courts) |
| Caption | Required — provided by SA shell |
| Signature | Required — provided by SA shell |

### Page Limits

NM state courts generally do not impose strict page limits on trial briefs
unless specified in the scheduling order. However, follow these guidelines:

- Trial briefs exceeding 30 pages should include a table of contents
  and table of authorities
- Conciseness is valued — verbose briefs lose judicial attention
- The NML formatting skill applies house standards for length and density

### NM Authority Hierarchy

When citing authority in a trial brief for NM state court, follow this
hierarchy (NM-first per NML standards):

1. NM Supreme Court decisions (binding)
2. NM Court of Appeals decisions (binding unless conflicting with NMSC)
3. NM UJI Civil Committee Commentary (persuasive)
4. NMSA statutes (binding for statutory issues)
5. 10th Circuit decisions applying NM law (persuasive)
6. Other NM authorities (AG opinions, treatises)
7. Restatements (persuasive, frequently cited by NM courts)
8. Other jurisdictions (persuasive, last resort)

For federal court (D.N.M.):
1. U.S. Supreme Court (binding on federal questions)
2. 10th Circuit (binding)
3. D.N.M. decisions (persuasive, same court)
4. NM Supreme Court (binding on state law questions via Erie)
5. Other circuits (persuasive)

page limits. Follow the 25-page memorandum guideline unless the court
orders otherwise. Exceed only with leave of court.

## Content Requirements by Court

### Mandatory Content (Both Courts)

1. Statement of the case (procedural posture)
2. Statement of facts (supported by record citations)
3. Legal standards / elements of claims
4. Argument (application of facts to law)
5. Relief requested

### Commonly Required by Scheduling Order

1. Exhibit list with pre-marked exhibit numbers
2. Witness list with estimated testimony time
3. Proposed jury instructions (often filed separately)
4. Proposed verdict form (often filed separately)
5. Motions in limine (often filed separately with brief deadlines)
6. Deposition designations and counter-designations
7. Settlement conference report

### Judge-Specific Requirements

Different judges in NM have varying requirements for trial briefs. Common
variations include:

- **Trial brief vs. pretrial memorandum**: Some judges want both (substantive
  brief + logistics memo); others want one combined document
- **Bench copies**: Some judges require bench copies of the trial brief
  and all cited cases
- **Courtesy copies**: Some judges prefer electronic courtesy copies
  via email
- **Witness and exhibit binders**: Some judges require organized binders
  for the court's use during trial

The TRIAL-BRIEF skill should flag judge-specific requirements as
`[DECISION REQUIRED]` when the behavioral layer (AIP judge profile)
identifies specific preferences.

## Common Filing Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Missing certificate of service** — SA shell provides this; confirm
   it is present in final document
2. **Incorrect case number** — SA merge token; verify before filing
3. **Wrong judge** — Verify judge assignment has not changed
4. **Expired authority** — LPB Shepard Gate catches this, but verify
5. **Exhibit number mismatch** — Exhibit numbers in brief must match
   pretrial order exhibit list
6. **Witness not disclosed** — Cannot call a witness not disclosed per
   NMRA 1-026(E) (or Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(a)(3))
7. **Late filing** — Check scheduling order deadline and build in buffer
8. **Unsealed confidential information** — Verify no sealed or
   confidential information is in the public filing
