# DEPO-NOTICE Reference: FRCP 30 — Federal Deposition Rules (D.N.M.)

## Purpose

This reference provides the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure governing
depositions upon oral examination (FRCP 30) as applied to deposition notice
drafting in the United States District Court for the District of New Mexico
(D.N.M.). For NM state court depositions, see `nmra-1-030-rules.md`.

---

## 1. FRCP 30 — Overview and Structure

| Section | Subject | Key Content |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 30(a) | When a deposition may be taken | Leave of court requirements, 10-deposition limit |
| 30(b) | Notice of the deposition | Content requirements, 30(b)(6) corporate depositions |
| 30(c) | Examination and cross-examination | Conduct, objections, participation |
| 30(d) | Duration; sanction; motion to terminate | 7-hour limit, termination procedures |
| 30(e) | Review by the witness | 30-day review period, changes |
| 30(f) | Certification and delivery | Filing and storage of transcript |
| 30(g) | Failure to attend | Sanctions for noticing party failure |

## 2. FRCP 30(a) — When a Deposition May Be Taken

### General Rule (30(a)(1))

A party may, by oral questions, depose any person, including a party, without
leave of court except as provided in Rule 30(a)(2). The deponent's attendance
may be compelled by subpoena under Rule 45.

### Leave of Court Required (30(a)(2))

A party must obtain **leave of court**, and the court must grant leave to the
extent consistent with Rule 26(b)(1) and (2), if:

1. **The parties have not stipulated** to the deposition, and:
   - The deposition would result in more than **10 depositions** being taken
     by the plaintiffs, defendants, or third-party defendants (30(a)(2)(A)(i))
   - The deponent has **already been deposed** in the case (30(a)(2)(A)(ii))
   - The party seeks to take a deposition **before the time specified in
     Rule 26(d)** (before the parties' Rule 26(f) conference) (30(a)(2)(A)(iii))

2. The deponent is **confined in prison** (30(a)(2)(B))

### 10-Deposition Limit

**Critical rule for federal practice:**
- Each "side" (plaintiffs collectively, defendants collectively) is limited
  to **10 depositions** without leave of court or stipulation
- The limit counts depositions, not deponents (a 30(b)(6) deposition counts
  as one deposition regardless of how many designees testify)
- Stipulation between the parties can modify this limit
- Court order can increase or decrease the limit

**Drafting implication:** If this is the 9th or 10th deposition for the side,
flag: `[DECISION REQUIRED: This is deposition [N] of 10 allowed under FRCP
30(a)(2)(A)(i). Consider whether leave of court is needed for additional
depositions.]`

## 3. FRCP 30(b) — Notice of the Deposition

### FRCP 30(b)(1) — Notice Requirements

A party who wants to depose a person by oral questions must give **reasonable
written notice** to every other party. The notice must state:

1. The **time and place** of the deposition
2. The **name and address** of the deponent, if known
3. If the name is not known, a **general description** sufficient to identify
   the person or the particular class or group to which the person belongs

### Reasonable Notice Standard in D.N.M.

The D.N.M. Local Rules (D.N.M.LR-Cv) do not specify a minimum notice period.
Federal practice standards:

| Notice Period | Assessment |
|---------------|------------|
| 14+ days | Presumptively reasonable |
| 10-13 days | Acceptable in most cases; may be challenged in complex matters |
| 7-9 days | Short; opposing party may object |
| < 7 days | Unreasonable absent agreement or court order |

**D.N.M. practice notes:**
- D.N.M. judges generally expect 14 days minimum notice
- The scheduling order may impose different requirements
- Local practice favors cooperation in scheduling; meet-and-confer before
  noticing is strongly encouraged
- D.N.M.LR-Cv 30.1 requires parties to confer regarding scheduling before
  filing motions related to depositions

### FRCP 30(b)(2) — Producing Documents

The notice to a party deponent may be accompanied by a request under
Rule 34 to produce documents and tangible things at the deposition.

**For non-party deponents:** Document production requires a **subpoena duces
tecum** under Rule 45(a)(1)(A)(iii). See FRCP 45 section below.

### FRCP 30(b)(3) — Method of Recording

The party who notices the deposition must state in the notice the method
for recording the testimony. The method may be:
- Sound
- Sound-and-visual
- Stenographic

Unless the court orders otherwise, the testimony may be recorded by the
method stated in the notice. Any party may arrange for a transcript or
recording at that party's expense.

### FRCP 30(b)(4) — Remote Depositions

The parties may stipulate — or the court may on motion order — that a
deposition be taken by telephone or other remote means. For purposes of
the rules, the deposition is taken at the place where the deponent answers
questions.

### FRCP 30(b)(6) — Corporate Depositions

In the notice, a party may name as the deponent a public or private
corporation, a partnership, an association, a governmental agency, or
other entity and must describe with **reasonable particularity** the matters
for examination.

The named organization must then designate one or more officers, directors,
managing agents, or other persons who consent to testify on its behalf.
The designated persons must testify about information known or reasonably
available to the organization.

See `mode-corporate.md` for detailed 30(b)(6) guidance.

## 4. FRCP 30(d) — Duration Limit

### 7-Hour / 1-Day Rule (30(d)(1))

Unless otherwise stipulated or ordered by the court, a deposition is limited
to **1 day of 7 hours**.

**Calculating the 7 hours:**
- Only time spent on the record counts toward the limit
- Breaks, lunch, and off-the-record discussions do not count
- Time spent by all examining parties counts against the total
  (not 7 hours per party)

**Extending the time:**
- By stipulation of the parties
- By court order for good cause, consistent with Rule 26(b)(1) and (2)
- Good cause factors: witness evasion, need for translation, complexity of
  issues, number of topics (30(b)(6)), volume of documents

**Drafting implication:** Always state in the notice that the deposition is
"expected to last no more than 7 hours pursuant to FRCP 30(d)(1)" or, if
the attorney anticipates needing more time, flag:
`[DECISION REQUIRED: Deposition may exceed 7-hour limit. Seek stipulation
or court order for additional time.]`

### Motion to Terminate or Limit (30(d)(3))

At any time during a deposition, the deponent or a party may move to
terminate or limit the deposition on the ground that it is being conducted
in bad faith or in a manner that unreasonably annoys, embarrasses, or
oppresses the deponent or a party.

## 5. FRCP 45 — Subpoenas (Non-Party Witnesses)

### When a Subpoena Is Required

A subpoena under Rule 45 is required to:
1. Compel a **non-party's attendance** at a deposition
2. Compel a **non-party's production** of documents at a deposition
3. Compel attendance at a deposition in a location beyond the non-party's
   normal commute

**Party witnesses** may be compelled to attend by notice alone (to counsel).

### Geographic Limitations (45(c)(1)(A))

A subpoena may command a person to attend a deposition only within:
1. **100 miles** of where the person resides, is employed, or regularly
   transacts business in person; or
2. Within the **state** where the person resides, is employed, or regularly
   transacts business, if the person is a party or party's officer AND
   the command only requires attendance (no document production beyond
   that available under Rule 34)

**Geographic analysis for D.N.M. cases:**

| Deponent Location | Subpoena Permissible? | Notes |
|-------------------|-----------------------|-------|
| Within 100 mi of depo location | Yes | Standard |
| In NM but > 100 mi from depo location | Party: Yes; Non-party: No (move depo) | Non-party: change location or seek court order |
| Out of state | Issue subpoena from district where deponent resides | Must use FRCP 45(a)(2) |
| Foreign country | Hague Convention / Letters rogatory | Complex; flag `[DECISION REQUIRED]` |

### Subpoena Service (45(b))

- Personal service required (45(b)(1))
- Fees: Attendance fee + mileage per 28 U.S.C. Section 1821 ($40/day plus
  travel allowance) — must be **tendered with service**
- Notice of subpoena served on all parties before service on witness (45(a)(4))

### Subpoena Compliance Deadline

- A subpoena commanding production of documents must allow a reasonable time
  to comply (at least 14 days under most courts' interpretation)
- A motion to quash or modify must be filed **promptly**, typically within
  14 days of service or before the compliance date

## 6. FRCP 26(d) — Discovery Timing

### Rule 26(f) Conference Prerequisite

Under FRCP 26(d)(1), a party may not seek discovery from any source before
the parties have conferred as required by Rule 26(f), except:
- By stipulation
- By court order
- As provided in Rules 26(d)(2), 30(a)(2)(A)(iii)

**Drafting implication:** If the Rule 26(f) conference has not yet occurred,
the deposition notice requires leave of court. Flag:
`[VERIFY: Has the Rule 26(f) conference occurred? If not, leave of court
is required under FRCP 30(a)(2)(A)(iii).]`

## 7. D.N.M. Local Rules — Deposition-Specific

### D.N.M.LR-Cv 16.1 — Scheduling

- The scheduling order controls discovery deadlines, including the deadline
  to complete depositions
- All depositions must be **completed** by the discovery deadline (not merely
  noticed)
- Flag: `[VERIFY: Discovery deadline per scheduling order]`

### D.N.M.LR-Cv 26.1 — Discovery Cooperation

- Parties are expected to cooperate in scheduling depositions
- Meet-and-confer is required before filing motions related to deposition
  disputes
- The court takes discovery cooperation seriously; sanctions for gamesmanship

### D.N.M.LR-Cv 30.1 — Depositions

- Specific local requirements for deposition conduct and scheduling
- Parties must confer in good faith regarding scheduling
- Video depositions must comply with local technical requirements

### D.N.M.LR-Cv 37.1 — Discovery Motions

- A discovery motion must certify that the movant has in good faith conferred
  or attempted to confer with the opposing party
- Applies to motions to compel deposition attendance or testimony

## 8. Expert Depositions — Federal Rules

### FRCP 26(a)(2) — Expert Disclosures

Expert depositions have additional timing requirements:

1. Expert report must be disclosed **at least 90 days** before trial (26(a)(2)(D)(i))
2. Rebuttal expert report: **30 days** after the other party's expert disclosure
3. Expert depositions may not be taken until **after** the expert report is disclosed

**Drafting implication for expert deposition notices:**
- Confirm expert report has been disclosed before noticing deposition
- Reference the expert report in the notice
- Flag: `[VERIFY: Expert report disclosed per FRCP 26(a)(2)(D)]`

### FRCP 26(b)(4) — Expert Discovery Scope

- A party may depose any person identified as an expert whose opinions may
  be presented at trial
- The deposition may cover: opinions, bases for opinions, methodology,
  qualifications, data considered, compensation
- Draft rule experts (consulting, non-testifying) are not discoverable absent
  exceptional circumstances

## 9. Key Differences: FRCP 30 vs. NMRA 1-030

| Feature | FRCP 30 (Federal) | NMRA 1-030 (NM State) |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Deposition limit** | 10 per side (30(a)(2)(A)(i)) | No limit |
| **Duration limit** | 7 hours / 1 day (30(d)(1)) | No presumptive limit |
| **Pre-discovery conference** | Required (26(f)) before depositions | No equivalent requirement |
| **Subpoena geographic range** | 100 miles (FRCP 45(c)(1)(A)) | Statewide (NMRA 1-045) |
| **Witness fees** | $40/day + travel (28 U.S.C. 1821) | $10/day + mileage (NMSA 38-6-4) |
| **Recording method** | Any method stated in notice (30(b)(3)) | Any method stated in notice (1-030(B)(3)) |
| **Corporate depo** | 30(b)(6) — reasonable particularity | 1-030(B)(6) — reasonable particularity |
| **Objections** | Form waived if not made; substance preserved | Same framework |
| **Review period** | 30 days (30(e)(1)) | 30 days (1-030(E)) |

**Drafting instruction:** Always check which court the case is in before
drafting. The differences in deposition limits and duration limits are
significant and affect notice content.
