# DEPO-NOTICE Reference: NMRA 1-030 — Depositions Upon Oral Examination

## Purpose

This reference provides the New Mexico Rules of Civil Procedure governing
depositions upon oral examination (NMRA 1-030) as applied to deposition notice
drafting. This is the controlling rule for all state court depositions in
New Mexico district courts. For federal cases in the District of New Mexico,
see `frcp-30-rules.md`.

---

## 1. NMRA 1-030 — Overview

Rule 1-030 NMRA governs depositions upon oral examination in New Mexico state
courts. It is modeled on former FRCP 30 but has diverged in several important
ways, particularly regarding deposition limits and duration.

### Full Rule Structure

| Section | Subject | Key Content |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 1-030(A) | When depositions may be taken | Any time after commencement of action; leave required in limited circumstances |
| 1-030(B) | Notice of examination | Content and service requirements for deposition notice |
| 1-030(C) | Examination and cross-examination | Conduct of the deposition |
| 1-030(D) | Duration and manner | Motion to terminate or limit |
| 1-030(E) | Review by witness | Right to review and sign transcript |
| 1-030(F) | Certification and delivery | Court reporter obligations |
| 1-030(G) | Failure to attend | Sanctions for noticing party failure |

## 2. NMRA 1-030(A) — When Depositions May Be Taken

### General Rule

A party may take the deposition of any person, including a party, without leave
of court **at any time after commencement of the action**, except as provided below.

### Leave of Court Required

Leave of court is required if:
1. The deponent is **confined in prison**
2. The case is subject to a **scheduling order** that restricts discovery timing
3. A **protective order** has been entered limiting depositions

### No Deposition Limit in NM

**Critical distinction from federal practice:** New Mexico has **no** numerical
limit on depositions. Unlike FRCP 30(a)(2)(A)(i) (10-deposition limit per side),
New Mexico permits unlimited depositions subject only to proportionality under
NMRA 1-026(B) and the court's general power to limit discovery.

**Drafting implication:** Do not include language about "leave of court" for
exceeding a deposition limit in NM state court notices. This limit does not
exist in NM practice.

## 3. NMRA 1-030(B) — Notice of Examination

### NMRA 1-030(B)(1) — Notice Requirements

A party desiring to take a deposition upon oral examination shall give
**reasonable notice in writing** to every other party to the action.

**Notice must state:**
1. The **time and place** of the deposition
2. The **name and address** of each person to be examined, if known
3. If the name is not known, a **general description sufficient to identify**
   the person or the particular class or group to which the person belongs
4. The matters on which the person is to be examined (if 30(b)(6) designation)

### What Constitutes "Reasonable Notice"

NMRA 1-030 does not define "reasonable." New Mexico practice establishes:

| Factor | Standard |
|--------|----------|
| **Customary minimum** | 14 calendar days before the deposition |
| **Short notice** | Less than 14 days; may draw objection |
| **Emergency/expedited** | Requires agreement of counsel or court order |
| **Complex case / duces tecum** | 21-30 days is more reasonable |
| **Out-of-state deponent** | Additional time for travel arrangements |
| **Expert witness** | Coordinate with expert's schedule; 30 days common |

**NM case law on reasonable notice:**
- Courts have discretion to determine reasonableness based on the totality
  of circumstances
- Relevant factors: complexity, distance, amount of preparation needed,
  whether duces tecum is attached, prior scheduling discussions
- Motions for protective order under NMRA 1-026(C) are the remedy for
  unreasonable notice

### NMRA 1-030(B)(2) — Duces Tecum (Production at Deposition)

The notice to a deponent who is a **party** may be accompanied by a request
for the production of documents and tangible things at the deposition, made
in compliance with NMRA 1-034.

**For non-party deponents:** Document production must be compelled by
**subpoena duces tecum** under NMRA 1-045. The deposition notice alone is
insufficient to compel a non-party to produce documents.

### NMRA 1-030(B)(3) — Method of Recording

The party taking the deposition shall state in the notice the method by
which the testimony shall be recorded. The method may be:

1. **Stenographic** (court reporter transcription) — default
2. **Sound** (audio recording)
3. **Sound-and-visual** (video recording)
4. **Any combination** of the above

Unless the court orders otherwise, a deposition may be recorded by any
method stated in the notice. The testimony shall be recorded by the officer
before whom the deposition is taken, or by a person acting under the
officer's direction and in the officer's presence.

**Additional recording at another party's expense:**
Any party may arrange for a transcription of the deposition from an
alternative recording method, at that party's expense.

### NMRA 1-030(B)(4) — Deposition by Remote Means

The parties may stipulate, or the court may order, that a deposition be
taken by telephone or other remote means. For purposes of the rules, a
deposition taken by remote means is considered taken at the place where
the deponent is to answer questions.

### NMRA 1-030(B)(6) — Corporate Depositions

A party may name as the deponent a public or private corporation, a
partnership, an association, a governmental agency, or other entity and
must describe with **reasonable particularity** the matters for examination.

The named organization must then designate one or more officers, directors,
managing agents, or other persons to testify on its behalf about information
known or reasonably available to the organization.

See `mode-corporate.md` for detailed 30(b)(6) guidance.

## 4. NMRA 1-030(C) — Examination and Cross-Examination

### Conduct of Examination

- Examination and cross-examination proceed as at trial
- Governed by NMRA 11-611 (mode and order of interrogation)
- The court reporter administers the oath
- All objections must be stated concisely and in a non-argumentative manner

### Objections During Deposition

- Objections to form must be made during the deposition or they are **waived**
  (NMRA 1-032(D)(3)(a))
- Objections to competence, relevance, and materiality are **preserved**
  until trial (NMRA 1-032(D)(3)(b))
- "Objection, form" is sufficient to preserve form objections
- Speaking objections and coaching are prohibited

### Instructions Not to Answer

Under NMRA 1-030(D), a deponent may be instructed not to answer **only**:
1. To preserve a **privilege**
2. To enforce a **court-ordered limitation** on discovery
3. To present a motion to **terminate or limit** under NMRA 1-030(D)

---

## 5. NMRA 1-030(D) — Duration and Termination

### No Presumptive Duration Limit

**Critical distinction from federal practice:** New Mexico has **no** 7-hour
presumptive duration limit. Unlike FRCP 30(d)(1), which limits depositions to
"1 day of 7 hours," NM places no default time cap on depositions.

**Practical implications:**
- Include an estimated duration in the notice as a professional courtesy
- Unreasonably long depositions may be challenged by motion for protective
  order under NMRA 1-026(C)
- The court may limit duration at the request of any party or the deponent

### Motion to Terminate or Limit

At any time during a deposition, any party or the deponent may move to
terminate or limit the deposition on the ground that it is being conducted
in bad faith or in a manner that unreasonably annoys, embarrasses, or
oppresses the deponent or party.

The court may:
1. Order the deposition terminated
2. Limit the scope and manner of the deposition
3. Order the deposition conducted in a different manner
4. Award expenses and attorney's fees

## 6. NMRA 1-030(E) — Review and Signing

### Witness Review Rights

- The deponent **or any party** may request review of the transcript before
  it is filed
- If review is requested, the deponent has **30 days** from notification
  to review and sign
- Changes in form or substance must be noted with reasons
- If the deponent does not sign within 30 days, the officer shall sign and
  note the waiver

### Errata Sheet

- Changes to the transcript are made via errata sheet, not by altering the
  transcript itself
- The errata sheet becomes part of the deposition record
- Substantive changes (not just typographical) may affect the evidentiary
  value of the testimony

## 7. Related NM Rules

### NMRA 1-026 — General Discovery Provisions

- NMRA 1-026(B): Scope of discovery — proportionality factors
- NMRA 1-026(C): Protective orders — remedy for oppressive discovery
- NMRA 1-026(D): Timing of discovery — unless otherwise ordered, discovery
  may begin after commencement of the action

### NMRA 1-028 — Persons Before Whom Depositions May Be Taken

- Within NM: Any person authorized to administer oaths under NM law
- Outside NM: As provided by the law of the place where the deposition is taken
- Disqualification: No deposition before a person who is a relative, employee,
  attorney, or has a financial interest in the action

### NMRA 1-032 — Use of Depositions at Trial

- NMRA 1-032(A)(1): Impeachment — any deposition may be used to impeach
- NMRA 1-032(A)(2): Adverse party — deposition of a party may be used for
  any purpose by an adverse party
- NMRA 1-032(A)(3): Unavailable witness — if witness is unavailable, deposition
  may be used as substantive evidence
- NMRA 1-032(A)(4): Exceptional circumstances — catch-all for interests of justice

### NMRA 1-037 — Failure to Cooperate in Discovery

- NMRA 1-037(A): Motion to compel — if deponent refuses to answer
- NMRA 1-037(B): Sanctions for failure to comply with order
- NMRA 1-037(D): Failure to attend deposition — expenses and sanctions

### NMRA 1-045 — Subpoena

- NMRA 1-045(A): Forms of subpoena — attendance, production, or both
- NMRA 1-045(B): Service — personal service required
- NMRA 1-045(C): Protection of non-party — motion to quash or modify
- **Statewide subpoena power:** NM subpoenas may compel attendance anywhere
  within the state

## 8. NM-Specific Statutory References

### NMSA 1978 Section 38-6-4 — Witness Fees

- Witnesses are entitled to a fee of $10.00 per day plus mileage
- Must be tendered with subpoena for non-party witnesses
- Failure to tender witness fees renders subpoena unenforceable

### NMSA 1978 Section 38-6-1 — Competency of Witnesses

- All persons are competent to testify unless otherwise provided by law
- Applies to deposition testimony as well as trial testimony

### Uniform Interstate Depositions and Discovery Act (NM)

- NMSA 1978 Sections 38-14-1 to 38-14-9
- Governs depositions of NM witnesses in out-of-state actions
- Governs depositions of out-of-state witnesses for NM actions
- A party must domesticate an out-of-state subpoena through NM district court

```
[ ] No deposition count limit (NM has none)
```
