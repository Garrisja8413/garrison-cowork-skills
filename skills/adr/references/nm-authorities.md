## NMRA Pleading Rules Index

### NMRA 1-007 — Pleadings Allowed; Form of Motions
- 1-007(A): Pleadings — complaint and answer; reply to counterclaim;
  answer to cross-claim; third-party complaint and answer. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-008 — General Rules of Pleading
- **1-008(A):** Short and plain statement of the claim showing the pleader
  is entitled to relief. Applies to affirmative defenses. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(B):** Denials — party shall state in short and plain terms the
  party's defenses to each claim asserted. Party shall admit or deny the
  averments. When a pleader intends in good faith to deny only a part of an
  averment, the pleader shall specify so much of it as is true and deny the
  remainder. A denial shall fairly meet the substance of the averments denied.
  When a party is without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief
  as to the truth of an averment, the party shall so state and this has the
  effect of a denial. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(C):** Affirmative defenses — party shall set forth affirmatively:
  accord and satisfaction, arbitration and award, assumption of risk,
  contributory negligence, discharge in bankruptcy, duress, estoppel,
  failure of consideration, fraud, illegality, injury by fellow servant,
  laches, license, payment, release, res judicata, statute of frauds,
  statute of limitations, waiver, and any other matter constituting an
  avoidance or affirmative defense. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(D):** Effect of failure to deny — averments in a pleading to which
  a responsive pleading is required, other than those as to the amount of
  damage, are admitted when not denied in the responsive pleading. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-008(E):** Pleading to be concise and direct; consistency — each averment
  shall be simple, concise, and direct. A party may set forth two or more
  statements of a claim or defense alternatively or hypothetically.
  A party may state as many separate claims or defenses regardless of
  consistency. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-009 — Pleading Special Matters
- **1-009(B):** Fraud, mistake, condition of mind — in all averments of fraud
  or mistake, the circumstances constituting fraud or mistake shall be stated
  with particularity. Malice, intent, knowledge, and other condition of mind
  may be averred generally. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-009(C):** Conditions precedent — in pleading the performance or
  occurrence of conditions precedent, it is sufficient to aver generally
  that all conditions precedent have been performed or have occurred.
  A denial of performance must be made with particularity. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-010 — Form of Pleadings
- **1-010(B):** Paragraphs; separate statements — all averments of claim or
  defense shall be made in numbered paragraphs, the contents of each of
  which shall be limited as far as practicable to a statement of a single
  set of circumstances. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-011 — Signing of Pleadings, Motions, and Other Papers; Sanctions
- **1-011(A):** By presenting a pleading, an attorney certifies that to the
  best of the attorney's knowledge, information, and belief, formed after
  reasonable inquiry: [VERIFY-CITE]
  - (1) It is not presented for any improper purpose
  - (2) The claims, defenses, and other legal contentions are warranted by
    existing law or by a nonfrivolous argument for modification
  - (3) The factual contentions have evidentiary support or will likely have
    support after further investigation or discovery
  - (4) The denials of factual contentions are warranted on the evidence or
    are reasonably based on lack of information or belief
- **1-011(C):** Sanctions — if after notice and opportunity to respond, the
  court determines that 1-011(A) has been violated, the court may impose
  appropriate sanctions. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-012 — Defenses and Objections
- **1-012(B):** How presented — every defense shall be asserted in the
  responsive pleading, except: lack of jurisdiction, improper venue,
  insufficiency of process/service, failure to state a claim, failure to
  join indispensable party. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-012(C):** Motion for judgment on the pleadings — after the pleadings
  are closed but within such time as not to delay the trial, any party may
  move for judgment on the pleadings. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-012(F):** Motion to strike — the court may order stricken from any
  pleading any insufficient defense or any redundant, immaterial, impertinent,
  or scandalous matter. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-036 — Requests for Admission
- **1-036(A):** Any party may serve upon any other party a written request
  for the admission of any matter. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-036(B):** Effect of admission — any matter admitted is conclusively
  established unless the court permits withdrawal. [VERIFY-CITE]

### NMRA 1-037 — Failure to Make Discovery; Sanctions
- **1-037(A):** Motion to compel — party may apply for order compelling
  discovery. Must show good faith effort to resolve. [VERIFY-CITE]
- **1-037(A)(4):** Expenses — court may award reasonable expenses including
  attorney's fees. [VERIFY-CITE]

by operation of NMRA 1-008(D). Evasive or incomplete denials have the
same effect as a failure to deny.

### Specificity of Denials

**Principle:** NMRA 1-008(B) requires that when a pleader intends to deny
only part of an averment, the pleader must specify what is true and deny

### Motion to Strike — Insufficient Defenses

**Principle:** NMRA 1-012(F) permits striking insufficient defenses.
A defense that is a bare legal label without supporting facts fails to

### Sanctions / Certification

**Principle:** NMRA 1-011 imposes a certification obligation. An attorney
who presents a pleading certifies that denials are warranted and defenses

## Authority Hierarchy (ADR Analysis)

When citing authority in the Defect Matrix and Remedy Playbook:

1. **NMSC** — New Mexico Supreme Court (binding)
   Format: `[Case Name], [Year]-NMSC-[###], ¶ [##]`
2. **NMCOA** — New Mexico Court of Appeals (binding unless overruled)
   Format: `[Case Name], [Year]-NMCA-[###], ¶ [##]`
3. **NMRA** — New Mexico Rules of Annotated (binding procedural rules)
   Format: `NMRA 1-[###]([subsection])`
4. **10th Circuit** — persuasive only for state court
   Format: `[Case Name], [F.3d/F.4th cite], [pinpoint] (10th Cir. [Year])`
5. **D.N.M.** — persuasive only for state court
   Format: `[Case Name], [F. Supp. cite], [pinpoint] (D.N.M. [Year])`
6. **Other federal circuits** — least weight; use only when NM/10th Cir. silent
   Always mark: `persuasive only — [Circuit]`
